## [With James Comey possibly facing a new indictment, statute of limitations could be at play](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/james-comey-new-indictment-statute-limitations-halligan-trump)

*A judge‚Äôs dismissal of the former [FBI](https://www.justice.gov/) director‚Äôs indictment left open the possibility of charging him again but noted a timing issue that could thwart a second indictment.*

Dec. 3, 2025, 6:00 AM EST
By [Jordan Rubin](https://www.ms.now/author/jordan-rubin)

The [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) [reportedly is considering](https://www.ms.now/ana-cabrera-reports/watch/doj-considers-seeking-new-james-comey-indictment-source-2472166979851) seeking a new indictment against [James Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey), whose case was [dismissed last week](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/james-comey-james-indictments-dismissed-lindsey-halligan-unlawfully-appointed) by a judge who said the prosecutor who brought the charges, [Lindsey Halligan](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/comey-case-lindsey-halligan-new-york-post-rcna244956), was unlawfully appointed. The judge‚Äôs dismissal was ‚Äúwithout prejudice,‚Äù meaning it wasn‚Äôt a permanent dismissal. It left open the possibility of new charges brought by a lawful prosecutor.

But if the [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) secures another indictment against the former [FBI](https://www.justice.gov/) director, that might only resume the tough time the prosecution [was already having](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/halligan-james-comey-trump-indict-first-investigate-second-rcna242185), even aside from the appointment issue. [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) had several pending pretrial motions that were unresolved at the point of the dismissal. Among them was his claim that the [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)-demanded charges, secured by a former personal lawyer of the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)‚Äôs without prior prosecutorial experience, were [unconstitutionally vindictive](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/lindsey-halligan-stalking-horse-trump-james-comey-rcna243096).

On top of that issue and others, if [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) were to face a new indictment, then his lawyers could argue that the charges are barred by the statute of limitations, which [Halligan](https://www.justice.gov/usao-edva/) barely beat when she obtained the indictment in September (over the objection of career prosecutors).

The timing issue surfaced in part of [U.S. District Judge Cameron McGowan Currie‚Äôs¬†ruling](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.vaed.582136/gov.uscourts.vaed.582136.213.0_3.pdf) that dismissed [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey)‚Äôs indictment on the grounds of unlawful appointment. It came up when the judge rejected [Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Pam Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-attorney-general/)‚Äôs attempt to retroactively salvage [Halligan](https://www.justice.gov/usao-edva/)‚Äôs appointment in October. [Currie](https://www.scd.uscourts.gov/Judges/distjudge.asp) wrote that [Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-attorney-general/)‚Äôs bid was too late because the statute of limitations to charge [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) with lying in his 2020 Senate testimony had expired in September. In a footnote, the judge observed that, as a general matter, indictments pause the statute of limitations, but not if they‚Äôre invalid to begin with.

The [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) had argued that [Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-attorney-general/) could have personally obtained a new indictment under a federal law that gives prosecutors six months to refile dismissed charges, so her ratification of [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey)‚Äôs existing indictment was therefore proper. [Currie](https://www.scd.uscourts.gov/Judges/distjudge.asp) rejected that argument because that law applies when a case is dismissed, while [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey)‚Äôs indictment was still pending when [Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-attorney-general/) made her move.

Yet now that the case has been dismissed and the [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) is reportedly considering charging [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) again, [that federal law](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3288) ‚Äì [18 U.S Code 3288](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title18-section3288&num=0&edition=prelim) ‚Äì could come into play in litigation over the statute of limitations if he is charged anew.

The law gives prosecutors six months to bring new charges when a felony indictment is dismissed ‚Äúfor any reason after the period prescribed by the applicable statute of limitations has expired.‚Äù During litigation ahead of [Currie](https://www.scd.uscourts.gov/Judges/distjudge.asp)‚Äôs ruling on the appointment issue, prosecutors highlighted that language ‚Äì emphasizing ‚Äúany reason,‚Äù which, [the [DOJ argued](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.vaed.582136/gov.uscourts.vaed.582136.137.0_4.pdf), ‚Äúobviously includes a defective appointment.‚Äù

On the other hand, in addition to arguing there was no valid indictment in the first place, [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey)‚Äôs lawyers have cited another part of that same law that they said should bar a new indictment. It says that new indictments aren‚Äôt allowed ‚Äúwhere the reason for the dismissal was the failure to file the indictment ‚Ä¶ within the period prescribed by the applicable statute of limitations, or some other reason that would bar a new prosecution.‚Äù

They highlighted the ‚Äúsome other reason that would bar a new prosecution‚Äù language [to argue that](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.vaed.582135/gov.uscourts.vaed.582135.175.0.pdf) a new indictment would be barred here, writing: ‚ÄúUsing an illegal appointment to procure an indictment days before the statute of limitations expires ‚Ä¶ is precisely the type of ‚Äòreason‚Äô that must ‚Äòbar a new prosecution.‚Äô‚Äù

So, if [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) is charged again, the [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) would argue that the law plainly gives the prosecution more time, while he would argue that time has already run out because it‚Äôs as if the [Halligan](https://www.justice.gov/usao-edva/)-secured indictment never existed and it would be unfair to give prosecutors what amounts to an end-run around the statute of limitations.

The challenge [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) could face is that the law itself might provide for such an end-run. But the issue would need to be litigated more fully by the parties and decided by a judge examining it directly ‚Äì if a new indictment comes.

If it does, then the statute of limitations is just one issue on top of the existing unresolved claims that [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) had raised prior to the dismissal, saying nothing of the hurdle that the [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) would face in proving the case beyond a reasonable doubt at trial, if the case made it that far. And unless the government succeeds in pressing its promised appeal of [Halligan](https://www.justice.gov/usao-edva/)‚Äôs disqualification, reviving the case would require the normally simple step of getting past a grand jury, something that [has proved difficult](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/sidney-reid-not-guilty-verdict-ice-trump-doj-rcna238137) for the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) in political cases.

[Judge Currie](https://www.scd.uscourts.gov/Judges/distjudge.asp) also [dismissed the indictment](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.vaed.583341/gov.uscourts.vaed.583341.140.0_1.pdf) [Halligan](https://www.justice.gov/usao-edva/) brought against [New York](https://www.ny.gov/) [Attorney General](https://ag.ny.gov/) [Letitia James](https://ag.ny.gov/about/meet-letitia-james), which wouldn‚Äôt face the same statute of limitations argument if she‚Äôs charged again ‚Äì though, like [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey), she had raised [other unresolved pretrial arguments](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/letitia-james-outrageous-government-conduct-motion-dismiss-rcna244607) at the time of her dismissal that the [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) would need to defeat to move a new case forward against her.
