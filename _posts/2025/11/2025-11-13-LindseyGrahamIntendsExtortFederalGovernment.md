"[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and I have had a hell of a journey. I hate it being this way. Oh, my God, I hate it. From my point of view, he has been a consequential President. But today, the first thing you will see, all I can say is, count me out. Enough is enough. I tried to be helpful."

That was [Senator Graham (T-SC)](https://www.lgraham.senate.gov/) in the Congressional Record on the evening of January 6 after order was restored to the Capitol. 

Weeks earlier, [Georgia](https://georgia.gov/) [Secretary of State](https://sos.ga.gov/) [Raffensperger](https://sos.ga.gov/page/about-secretary-raffensperger) stated that [Graham](https://www.lgraham.senate.gov/) called, asking to discard mail-in ballots. Of course, there's the infamous recorded conversation where Trump wanted Georgia to find 11,780 more votes. 

November 18, 2022, Attorney General Merrick Garland appointed [Jack Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) as Special Counsel to investigate Trump‚Äôs election interference and classified documents theft. [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) requested call records, number dialed and duration from the past as part of his investigations.

Earlier this year, at a Senate hearing with a combative [Kash Patel](https://www.fbi.gov/about/leadership-and-structure/director-patel), numerous Trumpian senators were livid that [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) "wiretapped" their cellphones.

Wiretapping is defined as recording or listening to conversations as they are taking place. There's a gaping hole in their logic. How can [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) know details of conversations that took place after they happened?

It is impossible to wiretap history!

Flash forward to last week, there's a provision in the Senate amended CR with a provision to sue the government individually for $500,000 for "wiretapping". In reality, this is called extortion. 

[Congressman Jim Jordan (T-OH4)](https://jordan.house.gov/) requested that [Jack Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) testify before the [House Judiciary Committee](https://judiciary.house.gov/) in a closed door hearing.
