## [Attorney General Alan Wilson issues statement on Supreme Court decision to uphold birthright citizenship - South Carolina Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/news/attorney-general-alan-wilson-issues-statement-on-supreme-court-decision-to-uphold-birthright-citizenship/)

JUN 30, 2026

([COLUMBIA](https://www.columbiasc.gov/), [S.C.](https://www.sc.gov/)) –[South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) [Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/) [Alan Wilson](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/meet-the-attorney-general/) made the following statement regarding the [U.S. Supreme Court](https://www.supremecourt.gov/)'s decision to uphold birthright citizenship.

“Today, the [Supreme Court](https://www.supremecourt.gov/) struck down [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s executive order providing that children born to parents unlawfully or temporarily present in the [United States](https://www.usa.gov/) are not citizens of the [United States](https://www.usa.gov/). While we are disappointed in the [Court](https://www.supremecourt.gov/)’s decision, we respect the process and will comply with the decision."

"For 150 years, the [14th Amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-14/) has been misapplied, granting birthright citizenship to those never intended by the drafters. This [amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-14/) was rightfully designed to bestow citizenship on emancipated slaves, but since then, it has been misinterpreted to incentivize the ridiculous notion that someone can come to the [United States](https://www.usa.gov/) in the dead of night, drop a child like an anchor, and suddenly that child is granted citizenship forever.​ As [Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/), I will continue to fight alongside [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) to secure our borders and defend our values.”

[Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/) [Alan Wilson](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/meet-the-attorney-general/) has joined multiple multistate amicus briefs throughout the legal process defending [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)'s [executive order on birthright citizenship](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/protecting-the-meaning-and-value-of-american-citizenship/). The briefs emphasized that the intention of the drafters of the [Constitution](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/) was not to grant automatic citizenship to individuals born to non-citizen parents.

You can read the opinion [here](https://gcc02.safelinks.protection.outlook.com/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fclick-1252989.icptrack.com%2Ficp%2Frelay.php%3Fr%3D44924690%26msgid%3D522997%26act%3D8HTH%26c%3D1252989%26pid%3D2186718%26destination%3Dhttps%253A%252F%252Fwww.scag.gov%252Fmedia%252Ff04n1gar%252Fbirthright-citizenship-decision.pdf%26cf%3D5808%26v%3Dae6eed46a75b4cadb0838424c168e2f814535f11984e8e07095cc13951270def&data=05%7C02%7Crkittle%40scag.gov%7Cf6afde8dea5047a6e36908ded6d6f4a0%7Caa43a17bbf734a4baea2e6f1f30c7e04%7C0%7C0%7C639184416056751718%7CUnknown%7CTWFpbGZsb3d8eyJFbXB0eU1hcGkiOnRydWUsIlYiOiIwLjAuMDAwMCIsIlAiOiJXaW4zMiIsIkFOIjoiTWFpbCIsIldUIjoyfQ%3D%3D%7C0%7C%7C%7C&sdata=ke7%2B4CcAGJye6NrjRGqd0Gc7UvIL%2B0PYN%2FLDnTL2ZOU%3D&reserved=0).

## Sixteen Tons | Tennessee Ernie Ford | Oct 18, 1956

Nov 25, 2012  #tennesseeernieford #thefordshow #sixteentons
[Ernie](https://www.ernieford.com/)'s performs Merle Travis's timeless anthem to the working man Sixteen Tons on [The Ford Show](https://www.ernieford.com/), October 18, 1956. This was the first time he performed this blockbuster hit on his show - the third episode of [The Ford Show](https://www.ernieford.com/).

[Tennessee Ernie Ford](https://www.ernieford.com/) wasn’t the first artist to record “Sixteen Tons,” but he made it his signature. The song, inspired by the travails of the real-life coal miners of [Kentucky](https://www.kentucky.gov/), was first recorded by one of his forerunners on the Capitol label, Merle Travis, in 1947.

In 2015, the song was recognized by the [U.S. Library of Congress](https://www.loc.gov/) for its cultural significance, and it was also adopted into the [National Recording Registry](https://www.loc.gov/programs/national-recording-preservation-board/recording-registry/).

About [Tennessee Ernie Ford](https://www.ernieford.com/)

Born [Ernest Jennings Ford](https://www.ernieford.com/) on February 13th, 1919 in [Bristol](https://www.bristoltn.gov/), [Tennessee](https://www.tn.gov/), [Tennessee Ernie Ford](https://www.ernieford.com/) has made an indelible impact on America and the world through his unparalleled accomplishments in radio, records and television.

Throughout his legendary career spanning over 50 years, [Ernie Ford](https://www.ernieford.com/)'s early successes as a radio personality led to his signing with [Capitol Records](https://www.capitolrecords.com/) in 1949. Through 1976, he released a total of eighty-three albums on the label--and literally scores of single records, including his classic version of Merle Travis' timeless anthem to the working man, "Sixteen Tons"...at the time of its release, the fastest selling single in Capitol's history.

About [The Ford Show](https://www.ernieford.com/)

On Thursday night, October 4th, 1956, [NBC](https://www.nbc.cpm/) premiered [The Ford Show](https://www.ernieford.com/) Starring [Tennessee Ernie Ford](https://www.ernieford.com/).

As prime-time's # 1 half-hour variety show for five years, [The Ford Show](https://www.ernieford.com/) featured all the ingredients of the top variety shows of the day; Hollywood's greatest guest stars, top-of-the-line production, and terrific music. But one simple element separated it from all others...[Ernie](https://www.ernieford.com/) closed virtually every show with a hymn, a spiritual or a song of faith. For the time, it was a bold and powerful statement for a popular entertainer of [Ford](https://www.ernieford.com/)'s stature to be making on live television, especially in the face of the initial objections from Madison Avenue and the  network. But it was a statement that would become the trademark of his career, and in many ways, the mark of his life. It would earn him the distinction of almost single-handedly bringing inspirational music into the mainstream of American entertainment...an enduring legacy of the lasting impact that one man's expression of faith can have on millions of people.
