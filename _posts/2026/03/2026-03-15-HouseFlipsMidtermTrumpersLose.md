## [‘No one thinks we’re keeping the majority’: House Republicans fear they’re losing](https://www.ms.now/news/house-republicans-midterms-majority-2026)

_[GOP](https://www.gop.com/) lawmakers returning from their [Doral](https://www.trumpgolfdoral.com/) retreat are voicing deep frustration about the party’s lack of accomplishments and are divided over how to spend their remaining time in power._

Mar. 13, 2026, 5:00 AM EDT
By [Mychael Schnell](https://www.ms.now/author/mychael-schnell) and Syedah Asghar

DORAL, Fla. — [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) leaders are publicly projecting confidence about their chances of holding the majority in the midterms. Privately, many of their members sound far less certain.

Gathered this week at a [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)-owned resort in [Doral](https://www.trumpgolfdoral.com/), [Florida](https://www.myflorida.gov/), to coordinate strategy for the rest of the legislative year and the looming campaign season, [Speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/) [Mike Johnson  (T-LA4)](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/), struck an upbeat tone, telling reporters, “Do not bet against the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)” as they try to maintain their narrow edge in the chamber.

But beneath the public optimism, there is deep frustration — and, in some cases, outright pessimism — simmering inside [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/)’s conference.

“No one thinks we’re keeping the majority except for the [speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/),” one [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), who requested anonymity to discuss the internal sentiment, told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/).

The lawmaker suggested [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/)’s confidence was less a reflection of the mood inside the conference and more about the impossible position he’s in.

“What’s he going to go out there and say? ‘We’re going to lose the majority’? He can’t do that,” the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) said. “Money would dry up.”

The candid assessment comes as [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) barrels toward the November midterm elections that [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)s see as make or break: Retain the majority and continue to provide [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) with a legislative partner, or hand the gavel to [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/)  many of whom are clamoring to launch impeachment proceedings against the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/).
Much of the current frustrations are centered on the party’s accomplishments — or lack thereof.

Since muscling through the reconciliation bill last summer, [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) have struggled to find an agenda that can become law. And as lawmakers prepare to hit the campaign trail in what has historically been an unkind political environment for the party in power, they’re grappling with a basic question: What, exactly, can they run on?

“In order to keep the majority, you have to have an agenda,” the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) said. “What are we working on?”

Not everyone has given up. [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Tim Burchett (T-TN2](https://burchett.house.gov/), told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) that [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)s could hold onto the [House](https://www.house.gov/) if they just “get our heads out of our collective rears.”

He called on the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) to start passing bills — both real ones that could become law and messaging bills — and to move them “without having to allow the staff to water them down.”

“Because America wants it,” [Burchett](https://burchett.house.gov/) said.

‘They see [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) as a bunch of do-nothing, kind of idiots’
Those sentiments came into clear view on March 1, when [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) convened for a member-only call that was meant to focus on the joint U.S.-[Israeli](https;//www.gov.il/) strikes on [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) — which began one day earlier — but quickly devolved into an airing of grievances over the party’s lack of accomplishments and struggles surrounding the passage of the [SAVE America Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296), which would require voter ID to cast a ballot and proof of citizenship to register to vote.

During the conversation, [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Brandon Gill (T-TX26)](https://gill.house.gov/), president of the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) conference’s freshman class, spoke up and said voters “have to have something to vote for, not just against,” according to a source on the call. Voters, he argued, don’t think [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) has given them enough to root for.

“The problem that we have now is that a lot of our voters support what the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s doing, but they see [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) as a bunch of do-nothing, kind of idiots, candidly,” he said, per the source on the call.

[Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) pushed back, calling the notion of a “do-nothing” [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) “patently absurd,” and pointed to the conference’s passage of the reconciliation bill last year, according to a source on the call. While [Republicans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) did approve the sprawling legislation, they’ve since had difficulty selling it on the campaign trail in a way that resonates with voters.

Reached by [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), [Gill](https://gill.house.gov/) said he wouldn’t comment on a private conversation but stood by his broader critique, with a particular focus on the [SAVE America Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296).

“By refusing to pass the [SAVE America Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296), the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) is setting us up to get slaughtered in the midterms,” he said.

On the call, [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Keith Self (T-TX3)](https://keithself.house.gov/), spoke next and sided with his fellow [Texas](https://www.texas.gov/) [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) — “defense doesn’t win, offense wins” — before tearing into [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/).

“I think we have a beaten-wife syndrome here,” Self said, according to a source on the call. “I think we ought to stop saying we’ve got unified government because we might have an R behind our names, but I’m not sure we’ve got unified government. The people don’t feel it. We talk about them feeling the economy all the time, but they’re not feeling it with [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/). They’re just not.”

[Self](https://keithself.house.gov/) did not respond to [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/)’s request for comment.

A bill, a bottleneck and rising stakes
The frustrations are focused largely on the fate of the [SAVE America Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296), which [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has labeled his top legislative priority heading into the midterms. The [House](https://www.house.gov/) approved the legislation in February but it has stalled in the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/), where [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)s have been unable to pierce the 60-vote threshold needed to break the filibuster.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has aggressively lobbied [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) to pass the bill, framing it as about “national survival,” warning that he won’t sign other pieces of legislation until it is passed and declaring that the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/)’s midterm chances hinge on that measure.

“We don’t have a country if we’re going to have elections that are so corrupt and so dishonest like we’ve witnessed over the last period of time,” [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) told lawmakers at the [Doral](https://www.trumpgolfdoral.com/) retreat.

“It’ll guarantee the midterms,” he said of the bill during a press conference minutes later. “If you don’t get it, big trouble.”

But [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) himself has warned against that kind of rhetoric. On the call, he cautioned that if lawmakers say the [SAVE America Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296) is “existential to the survival” of the U.S., “it might become a self-fulfilling prophecy,” according to a source on the call. If [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)s are unable to send [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) the [SAVE America Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296), he continued, conservative voters may skip the polls entirely because they think, “What’s the point? We’re going to lose anyway.”

“It’s a delicate balance,” [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) said of the messaging, according to a source on the call.

The numbers and the headwinds
On paper, [Republicans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’ position looks more manageable than the internal mood suggests.

The [National Republican Congressional Committee](https://nrcc.org/) outraised the [Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee](https://dccc.org/) in 2025, \$117.2 million to \$115 million, and [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) gave \$1 million to the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) campaign arm during his speech in [Doral](https://www.trumpgolfdoral.com/). [Republicans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) are also faring positively in the redistricting war: As things currently stand, the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) is on track to gain two to three seats from redrawn maps, according to [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2026/us/politics/midterms-house-maps-redistricting.html). And after trailing [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) by several points on the generic congressional ballot, [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)s have pulled [pulled even in some recent surveys](https://x.com/IAPolls2022/status/2028589118825857109?s=20).

But the political headwinds are building.

As the war in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) drags on with no ending in sight, gas prices are climbing sharply. According to [AAA](https://www.aaa.com/), the national average gas price is \$3.60 a gallon, up more than 60 cents from a month prior.

At the retreat, [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) called the spike a “temporary blip.” And [House](https://www.house.gov/) [GOP Conference](https://www.gop.com/) Chair [Lisa McClain (T-MI9)](https://mcclain.house.gov/) described the prices as “a snapshot in time.”

But some rank-and-file members are less sanguine.

“That’s an issue,” [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Mario Diaz-Balart (T-FL26)](https://mariodiazbalart.house.gov/), told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) at the retreat. “I think this is very temporary. Obviously, this is all because of the Strait of Hormuz, but, the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has said it, the goal is not, this is not going to be a forever war. The goal is to deal with this really dangerous terrorist regime in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) and hopefully that can be done as soon as possible, as quickly as possible.”

[Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Michael McCaul T-TX10)](https://mccaul.house.gov/) predicted that the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is likely “most concerned about rising gas prices,” but suggested that the prices were likely “a short-term problem.”

Then there is the weight of history. The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s party has lost [House](https://www.house.gov/) seats in the first midterm election in nearly every modern presidency. [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) thinks he can beat that trend.

“Defying history to win the majority is not a simple task, but it is one that we will do,” he told reporters at the retreat.

Some colleagues are less convinced.

“We’re being a little bit rosy here,” a [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) who requested anonymity to discuss the outlook, told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/).

Another [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), who represents a swing district, dinged leadership’s overall control of the conference’s midterm strategy, questioning its understanding of the congressional map and how to run in tough races.

“[Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) doesn’t live in a swing district, has never represented a swing district and doesn’t know what it’s like to manage a swing district,” the lawmaker told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/). “Any member in a tough district should never be looking to leadership to save them from the race, because leadership doesn’t know how to run races, because they’re all in R+30 or D+30 districts.”

“They don’t have races,” the lawmaker said. “They don’t run competitive districts.”

‘It will not be as big, but it can be just as beautiful’
Even as [Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) lawmakers clamor for more to run on, they can’t agree what to do next.

That debate has crystalized into one question: Should the party attempt a second budget reconciliation package?

[Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) has been teasing another effort for months, and during a fireside chat at the retreat, announced that he’s working on a Venn diagram to identify areas in which the conference can find unity.

Other [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) lawmakers, including ones instrumental to the process, are highly skeptical.

“I would absolutely love a second reconciliation bill. I would love that, but I just don’t think it will ever happen,” [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Ways and Means Committee](https://waysandmeans.house.gov/) Chairman [Jason Smith (T-MO8)](https://jasonsmith.house.gov/) who played a key role in the first bill’s passage, said at the retreat.

In response to the internal criticisms, [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) said he’s “more optimistic” than [Smith](https://jasonsmith.house.gov/).

“Let’s be realistic about it: It will not be as big, but it can be just as beautiful,” [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) told reporters.”Reconciliation 2.0 can be just as beautiful.”

Still, what would make it into the package remains a key question, especially as [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and [House](https://www.house.gov/) [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) leadership seem to be singing from different songsheets.

During the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) retreat, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) needled [Democrat](https://www.democrats.org/)s for using the word “affordability,” said the opposing party doesn’t talk about the issue anymore “because we brought down prices so much,” and said voters only talk about the [SAVE America Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296) and nothing else: “They don’t talk about housing, they don’t talk about anything.”

In remarks to reporters the next day, [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) said the cost of living would be the main focus for the conference moving forward.

“Our number-one objective for the remainder of the year — top priority — is to continue to work with [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) to reduce the cost of living for hard-working families,” [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) said.

Asked about the apparent contradiction, [McClain](https://mcclain.house.gov/) told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) the party can deal with all issues at the same time.

“I don’t think it’s an or; I think it’s an and,” she said. “If you look at economic issues, that is really what is important to a lot of Americans. It’s pocketbook issues, right? So it’s ‘and’ and not an ‘or,’ is how I would frame it.”
