## [MAGA pretends to misunderstand requests of phone toll records](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/phone-records-jack-smith-2020-trump-kash-patel-fbi-firings)

_Law enforcement routinely obtains phone records when conducting investigations. [Republicans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) have tried to paint that common move as scandalous._

Feb. 26, 2026, 2:28 PM EST
By [Jordan Rubin](https://www.ms.now/author/jordan-rubin)

[Republican](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) lawmakers [have been criticizing](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/jack-smith-defends-criminal-investigations-into-trump-during-house-hearing) former [special counsel](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) [Jack Smith](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/jack-smith-justice-department-prosecution-ask-jordan) for obtaining some of their phone toll records in his [2020 election interference investigation]({% link January6DocumentDocumentLinks.md %}). Yet it’s a routine step in criminal probes to secretly get such records, which don’t capture the substance of conversations like wiretaps do but rather show which numbers were in contact at certain points in time.

Regarding the nondisclosure orders his team obtained with those records, [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) [explained to lawmakers](https://judiciary.house.gov/sites/evo-subsites/republicans-judiciary.house.gov/files/2025-12/Smith-Depo-Transcript_Redacted-w-Errata.pdf) that “there was a grave risk of obstruction of justice, given the obstructive conduct of [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) as is set forth, for example, in the indictment in [Florida](https://www.myflorida.gov/),” referring to the other case he brought against [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), related to [classified documents](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/jack-smith-classified-documents-florida-aileen-cannon-rcna240017).

Both of [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s cases vanished with [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [2024 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/2024presgeresults.pdf) win, due to the [Justice Department](https;//www.justice.gov)’s [policy against prosecuting sitting presidents](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/trump-classified-documents-case-dismissed-nauta-de-oliveira-rcna191679). But instead of leaving the dismissals as windfalls for the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and trying to move on, [Republicans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) have been keeping the memory of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s alleged criminality alive by attacking [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) and other government workers associated with the efforts against the revenge-minded [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/).

The latest example, [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) [reported Wednesday night](https://www.ms.now/news/fbi-fires-agents-patel-trump-documents-case), is that the [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) “has fired at least 10 agents and support staff over allegations that they obtained phone records connected to [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) [Director Kash Patel](https://www.fbi.gov/about/leadership-and-structure/director-patel) and [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Chief of Staff Susie Wiles](https://www.linkedin.com/in/susiewiles/)” as part of the classified documents probe. [Patel](https://www.fbi.gov/about/leadership-and-structure/director-patel) called it “outrageous and deeply alarming that the previous [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) leadership secretly subpoenaed my own phone records — along with those of now [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Chief of Staff](https://www.linkedin.com/in/susiewiles/) [Susie Wiles](https://www.linkedin.com/in/susiewiles/) — using flimsy pretexts and burying the entire process in prohibited case files designed to evade all oversight.”

But like the complaints from [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) lawmakers, [Patel](https://www.fbi.gov/about/leadership-and-structure/director-patel) risks conveying a misleading impression about these routine requests while simultaneously drawing further attention to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s alleged criminality. The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) pleaded not guilty in all four of his criminal cases, with only his [New York hush money case]({% NewYork34GuiltyVerdicts.md %}) having gone to trial before the [2024 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/2024presgeresults.pdf) (and ending in a conviction that’s [on appeal](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/trump-appeals-hush-money-conviction-citing-supreme-court-immunity-case-rcna240294)). [Judge](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/) [Aileen Cannon](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/content/judge-aileen-m-cannon), a [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) appointee, [ruled this week](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/judge-cannon-blocks-release-of-jack-smiths-classified-documents-report) that [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s report on the documents case should stay secret.

To be sure, a person getting their phone records subpoenaed during an investigation doesn’t mean they’re guilty of any crimes or even that law enforcement thinks they are. But the [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) firings, which, perhaps not coincidentally, come amid the [latest scrutiny](https://www.ms.now/news/senator-slams-kash-patel-for-use-of-fbi-jet-asks-for-investigation) of [Patel](https://www.fbi.gov/about/leadership-and-structure/director-patel)’s directorship, also recall his own strange ties to the classified documents case, in which he testified in the grand jury after publicly claiming that [Trump declassified documents](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/trump-looks-rally-voters-kash-patels-immunity-intrigues-rcna55673) before leaving the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) the first time.

So, while [Patel](https://www.fbi.gov/about/leadership-and-structure/director-patel) has claimed that a “pretext” was used against him to get his records when he was a private citizen, he could be relying on a misleading rationale himself for these latest firings. Of course, it’s [only the latest](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/fbi-agents-trump-firing-civil-lawsuits-rcna190653) revenge-based move by this [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) against people who did their jobs. That the move is some mix of [Trumpian](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) vengeance and self-preservation on [Patel](https://www.fbi.gov/about/leadership-and-structure/director-patel)’s part seems clear, while painting a potentially ordinary investigative step as scandalous in the process.
