## [Why it's fair to blame Trump's Iran war for rising gas prices](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-war-gas-prices-hormuz)

_The ongoing war against [Tehran](https://tehran.ir/) has driven up oil prices as shipping through the Strait of Hormuz has ground to a halt._

Mar. 11, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Hayes Brown](https://www.ms.now/author/hayes-brown)

There’s a lot that Americans don’t understand about the presidency, the economy or how those two interact. Accordingly, [presidents](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) tend to get credit for things out of their control in good times and likewise catch the blame for events beyond their remit in bad times. [Inflation](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/why-biden-can-t-fix-his-poll-numbers-inflation-alone-n1295292) and [gas  prices](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/political-debate-gas-prices-mess-rcna19751) are two particularly prominent examples where the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) usually has few levers to pull that can move the needle.

Kudos to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) for finding a loophole that I had not considered: starting a needless war against one of the world’s major oil suppliers.

Since last week’s start of the [U.S.](https://www.usa.gov/) and [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/)-led military campaign against [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), energy prices have surged. As [CNBC noted Monday](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/09/the-us-iran-war-is-the-biggest-oil-supply-disruption-in-history.html), the war nobody — well, [almost nobody](https://www.wsj.com/politics/policy/lindsey-graham-trump-iran-fa5f54f0?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqdE72SNjpDqjHuSkmclyiKNQpAznkggPcs7P0IkTes7QIDeAaUp9sEOEmhjTn0%3D&gaa_ts=69ac917b&gaa_sig=GQGg2qZ7SUSzWyaLRN_MBgehu3MpQZ-K9ms2grL-xDr-MTMOcNMOWR49iBHpMJnY_R2iouflRTZ_joCr1hFA8Q%3D%3D) — asked for has caused “the largest oil supply disruption in history, more than double the previous record set during the Middle East crisis of the 1950s, according to an analysis by consulting firm [Rapidan Energy](https://www.rapidanenergy.com/).”

The sudden staunching of the flow of oil is multicausal. [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) forces [struck 30 fuel depots in Tehran](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-war-israel-goals-oil-fuel-depot-strike) in [Tehran](https://tehran.ir/) over the weekend; more attacks on [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) energy infrastructure could mean a further drop in the global oil supply. Meanwhile, fears about reprisals against vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, one of the most critical choke points for maritime shipping, caused a major backlog of tankers unwilling to take the risk of crossing the narrow stretch of ocean separating [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) from the Arabian Peninsula. That backlog caused [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s Arab neighbors in the Gulf to  [cut production](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2jxe382pwo), further stressing supplies worldwide.

Taken together, as [The Associated Press noted](https://apnews.com/article/markets-oil-iran-trump-war-brent-72e8c9a29c2ba1fd761ee968f3d4e553) noted, prices for oil went soaring beyond the roughly \$70 a barrel that was the norm before the war started: “The price of Brent crude, the international benchmark, briefly surged to \$119.50 per barrel on Monday — its highest level since the summer after [Russia](http://kremlin.ru/) invaded [Ukraine](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/) in 2022. West Texas Intermediate, which is produced in the U.S., also soared to \$119.48 per barrel at one point.”

Prices [declined again Monday](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2jxe382pwo) when [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) claimed the war was “very complete.” That would likely be a surprise to the [Iranians](https://irangov.ir/), given [Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth’s declaration](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/iran/live-blog/live-updates-iran-war-oil-prices-trump-hormuz-israel-rcna262670/rcrd103725?canonicalCard=true) the next day that “today will be, yet again, our most intense day of strikes inside [Iran](https://irangov.ir/).”

Oil prices were still hovering around $86 per barrel as of Tuesday evening. All the uncertainty [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) unleashed has quickly been felt at American gas stations, according to [the latest figures from AAA](https://gasprices.aaa.com/).

There’s plenty of room for things to get worse, despite [Trump’s sweaty claim Sunday](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116196014942465383) that a gain in “short term oil prices” was a “very small price to pay” for “safety and peace.” Because while [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s claim that the war was very complete may have caught investors’ eyes, in the [same conversation with CBS News](https://x.com/weijia/status/2031089231695851549), he also said he was “thinking about taking it over” — it being the Strait of Hormuz. That may be why [CNN reported Tuesday](https://www.cnn.com/world/live-news/iran-war-us-israel-trump-03-10-26?post-id=cmmkzi03a0000356ydfcuzu0o) that, according to unnamed U.S. intelligence sources, [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) was beginning to seed the strait with mines, sure to further hamper oil tankers’ transit.

It’s hard to believe that just three years ago, the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) was [hammering Presiden Joe Biden](https://twitter.com/NathanBrandWA/status/1501153590207000577) as the average gas price in the U.S. hit a record. The cost at the pump for Americans increased as the world’s demand for oil reached pre-pandemic levels, even as U.S. production was higher than ever. Not helping matters was [Russia](http://government.ru/)’s invasion of [Ukraine](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/), which prompted [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) to halt imports of [Russian](http://government.ru/) oil. I [wrote at the time](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/record-high-gas-prices-are-not-joe-biden-s-fault-n1291238) that the “[president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) does not, as \[[Republicans](https://www.gop.com/)] would have you believe, have a dial in the [Oval Office](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) that he uses to set gas prices.”

Much as with his tariffs and their inflationary pressure, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has managed to find a way to prove me wrong about just how much power a [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) can have over the economy. While there’s no dial that he’s turning to set gas prices, in unleashing this war, he is absolutely the root cause of the rising rates we’re seeing. (And ironically, he is reportedly considering [reducing the Biden-era sanctions on Russia](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/us-weighs-easing-russian-oil-sanctions-cool-global-price-surge-sources-say-2026-03-09/) to help ease the strain on global supplies.)

So far, [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) have tried to claim that there’s [nothing to worry about politically](https://www.ms.now/news/republicans-shrug-gas-prices-trump-iran-war) from the climbing prices. But we’re in an era where the U.S. is a [net exporter of oil](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/business/gasoline-price-energy-costs.html) and domestic drilling pushing to its full capacity. Without their plea to just drill more to fall back on, and [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s general denial of a problem, the pain at the pump could soon translate into pain for the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) at the ballot box.
