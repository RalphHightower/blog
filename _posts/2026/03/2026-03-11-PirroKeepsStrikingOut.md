## [Why Jeanine Pirro's failed Biden autopen investigation matters](https://www.ms.now/opinion/jeanine-pirro-biden-autopen-investigation)

_The [Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/) host turned U.S. attorney has had a string of public setbacks in her efforts to prosecute [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s enemies._

Mar. 7, 2026, 6:00 AM EST
By

It’s been a rough start to the year for the [U.S. attorney’s office in [Washington](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/). [Jeanine Pirro](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/jeanine-pirro-fox-news-trump-prosecutor-dc-weapon-rcna215526), the [Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/) host turned top federal prosecutor, has endured a string of embarrassing setbacks since January. According to the most recent reports, [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney)’s office [shelved its effort to indict former President Joe Biden](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trumps-justice-dept-reportedly-failed-to-find-a-crime-in-bidens-use-of-autopen) for his [use of an autopen](https://www.ms.now/top-stories/latest/what-is-autopen-trump-biden-jan-6-pardons-void-rcna196743) to sign executive actions while in office. Her office reportedly couldn’t assemble anything even resembling a criminal case.

The autopen inquiry, such as it was, was just the latest [collapsed effort from Pirro](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/democrats-want-to-turn-the-tables-on-pirro-following-failed-indictment-effort) to punish [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s enemies. Her failures are a sign that even as the [Justice Department](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has fallen under the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s control, the law still doesn’t automatically bend to his will. And [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s vendettas remain stalled out. The limits to his power have become a little clearer.

Her failures are a sign that even as the [Justice Department](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has fallen under the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s control, the law still doesn’t automatically bend to his will.

As if in the market for a new conspiracy, some [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) have attempted to [make hay out of Biden’s autopen usage](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/house-oversight-republicans-doj-biden-autopen-rcna240307). In short, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and others have claimed without evidence that [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) aides essentially forged the former [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s signature on executive orders toward the end of his term. [House Oversight Committee Chair James Comer, (T-KY1)](https://oversight.house.gov/landing/the-biden-autopen-presidency/), went so far as to claim last year that “[Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) Autopen Presidency will go down as one of the biggest political scandals in U.S. history.” The supposed scandal has even become something of a meme within the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), as showcased on the [recently installed so-called Wall of Fame](https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/trump-hangs-tacky-walk-of-fame-sign-outside-white-house.html) where [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) replaced [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/)’s portrait with an autopen.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) was anything but subtle about his [demands that Biden](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/trump-biden-autopen-investigation-rcna211223) for, well, something autopen-related. In June, he issued [a memo to Attorney General Pam Bondi](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/06/reviewing-certain-presidential-actions/) demanding the [Justice Department](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) investigate whether the autopen was part of a grand plot to hide the former [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s alleged cognitive decline. ([Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) has denied [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s claims, saying last year in a statement, “Let me be clear: I made the decisions during my presidency.”)

The [U.S. attorney](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) for the [District of Columbia](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/) has jurisdiction over federal crimes committed within [Washington](dc.gov/), making it an obvious choice of venue for this fishing expedition. [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney)’s predecessor, [acting U.S. attorney Ed Martin](https://www.ms.now/news/weaponization-team-lead-ousted-in-justice-dept-power-struggle), opened an investigation into [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) last spring, [according to The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/04/us/politics/trump-biden-autopen.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share), and it continued after [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) was confirmed to her role in August. But the probe reportedly suffered, the [Times](https://www.nytimes.com/) reports, because:

Investigators were never quite clear what crime, if any, had been committed by the [Biden administration](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/)’s use of the autopen.

It was also unclear whether investigators should focus their attention on the actions of [Mr. Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/)’s aides or on [Mr. Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) himself, given that the [United States Supreme Court](https://www.supremecourt.gov/), in a landmark ruling in 2024, granted broad immunity to [presidents](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) for most acts undertaken as part of their official duties.

In recent months, prosecutors determined that despite [Mr. Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s desire to seek vengeance against [Mr. Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) and his aides, there was no credible case to bring, the people said. The prosecutors never brought a potential indictment before a grand jury.

It’s hard to tell whether the autopen case never making it to a grand jury makes it more or less of an embarrassment than other recent fiascos. Last month, federal prosecutors from [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney)’s office [failed to persuade a grand jury to indict](https://www.ms.now/news/grand-jury-sedition-slotkin-kelly-trump) six [Democratic members](https://www.democrats.org/) of [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) on charges of seditious conspiracy. Their alleged crime: [filming a video](https://x.com/SenatorSlotkin/status/1990774492356902948) in which lawmakers reminded members of the military and intelligence community that they have an obligation not to follow illegal orders. Speaking of those lawmakers and their video, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) said in an interview last year that “in the old days, if you said a thing like that, that was punishable by death.” The grand jury’s refusal to indict, though, is a good sign its members disagree.

[Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) has managed at times to be a bit too gung-ho about working toward [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s implied goals. In January, [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) [Board Chair](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/default.htm) [Jerome Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) revealed that the [Justice Department](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) had [opened an investigation into him](https://www.ms.now/opinion/jerome-powell-investigation-jeanine-pirro) over renovations to the independent agency’s [Washington](dc.gov/) headquarters. [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) approved the inquiry last fall, [according to the Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/11/us/politics/jerome-powell-fed-inquiry-trump.html), which prompted the normally staid [Powell]() to go public after receiving a subpoena from her office. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) denied any knowledge of the investigation, distancing himself from what [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) might have hoped would win her praise.

It is for the best that [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney)’s efforts have languished. It is good that the justice system continues to rely on facts and evidence. But her failure to deliver on carrying out [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s vindictive agenda can’t overshadow the fact that she felt the need to try at all — and will likely try again.
