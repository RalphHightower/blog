## [Trump says he doesn’t have ‘the yips’ for boots in Iran. Republicans might with a war vote.](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-yips-boots-on-the-ground-iran-republicans-war-vote)

_[Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) in [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) are offering plenty of excuses as to why they don't need a war vote even if [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) puts boots on the ground in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)._

Mar. 11, 2026, 5:00 AM EDT
By [Mychael Schnell](https://www.ms.now/author/mychael-schnell) and [Kevin Frey](https://www.ms.now/author/kevin-frey)

When the U.S. first launched missiles at [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) late last month, most [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) on Capitol Hill gave [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) a pass, raising no objections to unilateral action despite [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) holding the sole constitutional power to declare war.

They reasoned, in part, that it was just an air attack.

But as the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) toys with going a step further and potentially deploying ground troops, few [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) are moving to intervene. Most are dismissing the idea as a hypothetical. Some are expressing zero interest in a vote, even if [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) deploys troops.

In short, [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) in [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) want to defer to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/).

Asked on Tuesday if he would ever want [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) to weigh in on the war at any point, [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Ron Johnson (T-WI)](https://www.ronjohnson.senate.gov/) offered this threshold for a vote: “When there’s enough support to support what the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s doing.”

“Honestly, I don’t think we ought to be holding divisive votes here, undermining what our commander-in-chief is doing, trying to take care of a menace,” he said.

[Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Rick Scott (T-FL)](https://www.rickscott.senate.gov/) told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) that since Harry S. Truman, “every [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has a right for military action without a formal declaration of war.”

When [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) asked [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) whether they’d want a vote to authorize war if [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) put boots on the ground in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), many [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) lawmakers simply declined to engage in “hypotheticals.”

“That is incredibly speculative. I don’t have any comment,” [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [James Risch (T-ID)](https://www.risch.senate.gov/), told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/).

“I’m not going to answer hypotheticals,” [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [John Kennedy (T-LA)](https://www.kennedy.senate.gov/) said. “I don’t think the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is going to commit troops.”

“I don’t think we’ll have to deploy conventional forces,” [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Joni Ernst (T-IA)](https://www.ernst.senate.gov/) said.

And [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Mike Rounds (T-SD)](https://www.rounds.senate.gov/) called the idea of boots on the ground “hypothetical at this point.”

“I know of no plans like that,” he said.

Of course, the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and his team have repeatedly sidestepped opportunities to rule out the idea of putting boots on the ground.

During an interview on Monday, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) told the [New York Post](https://nypost.com/2026/03/09/us-news/trump-tells-the-post-hes-nowhere-near-ordering-iran-ground-mission-at-nuke-facility-not-happy-with-tehrans-new-leader/), “I don’t have the yips with respect to boots on the ground — like every [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) says, ‘There will be no boots on the ground.’ I don’t say it.”

[Axios also over the weekend reported](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/08/iran-ground-troops-special-forces-nuclear) that the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) is weighing sending special forces into [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) to secure highly enriched uranium.

When [Speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/) [Mike Johnson (T-LA4)](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) was asked Tuesday when [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) would need to assert itself to authorize military action in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), his answer was far from definitive: “We’ll have to see.”

“We have asserted ourselves,” [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) claimed. “The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), up to this moment, and his [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) have complied in every way with every provision of law.”

Speaking at the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) retreat in [Florida](https;//www.myflorida.gov/), [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) suggested the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) had fulfilled its “legal obligation” by keeping [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) informed “during the operation.”

“If it comes to a declaration of war, then [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) would still act and exercise its authority,” [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) said. “But we all know that that has not happened in many decades, in spite of the various excursions we’ve been involved in.”

([Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) did authorize the use of military force in advance of the two most notable U.S.-involved conflicts of the past two decades: the wars in [Afghanistan](https://moi.gov.af/) and [Iraq](https://ur.gov.iq/). In fact, the [Afghanistan](https://moi.gov.af/) AUMF — because it was so broadly written — has been used to authorize more than three dozen different military engagements, in 14 different countries, over nearly 25 years.)

But as some [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) lawmakers brush aside the need for a vote, and others dodge the question entirely, a handful of [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) have made it clear they want [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) to come and ask for [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/)’ permission before escalating this conflict — a notable shift from their response to the initial strikes.

[Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Josh Hawley (T-MO)](https://www.hawley.senate.gov/) told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) that “when you put troops into combat situations, I think that [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) would need to authorize that.”

“That was my position in [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/),” he said, referring to the U.S. raid that resulted in the capture of [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Nicolás Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php).

“It’s always better when the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) works with [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) on these matters,” [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Michael McCaul (T-TX10)](https://mccaul.house.gov/), the former chairman of the [House Foreign Affairs Committee](https://foreignaffairs.house.gov/), told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) Tuesday.

Asked if a vote in [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) for boots on the ground would be a red line for him, [McCaul](https://mccaul.house.gov/) said it would be, noting the potential domino effects of deploying land forces.

“You put boots on the ground, you’re creating a magnet for jihadists,” he said. “You’re putting targets on the ground, not unlike what we saw in [Iraq](https://ur.gov.iq/) and [Afghanistan](https://moi.gov.af/). I think that would be a mistake.”

[Rep.](https:/www.house.gov/) [Mario Diaz-Balart, (T-FL26)](https://mariodiazbalart.house.gov/), who sits on the [House Appropriations](https://appropriations.house.gov/) [subcommittee](http://appropriations.house.gov/subcommittees/defense-0) that oversees defense spending, also opened the door to [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) getting involved if the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) moves toward sending in troops.

Already, the clock is ticking on the need for congressional action.

The [War Powers Resolution, first adopted in 1973](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R47603), sets a timer, automatically terminating the use of armed forces 60 days after the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) launches hostilities — unless [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) declares war or votes to authorize the use of military forces.

In the Capitol last week, retiring [Rep.](https:/www.house.gov/) [Don Bacon (T-NE2)](https://bacon.house.gov/) said if the 60-day mark comes, [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) should hold a vote.

“And I plan on supporting it,” he said. “Of course, we got — if it’s 60 more days, there’ll be a lot more events that will occur, and can always revise our opinions as time goes on.”

“But right now, I think the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) did the right thing,” he said.

[Rep.](https:/www.house.gov/) [Nancy Mace (T-SC1)](https://mace.house.gov/), the first female to graduate from [The Citadel](https://www.citadel.edu/), said last week that she supports holding a vote 60 days into the conflict.

“Let your voters and your constituents know where you stand, whether you agree with it or don’t agree with it,” she said.

Just last week, the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) declined to adopt a war powers resolution to rein in the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s military authority with respect to [Iran](https://irangov.ir/). [The vote fell nearly along party lines, 47-53](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-iran-war-powers-senate).

[Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Rand Paul (T-KY)](https://www.paul.senate.gov/) was the sole [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) to back the measure, while [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [John Fetterman (D-PA)](https://www.fetterman.senate.gov/) was the sole [Democrat](https://www.democrats.org/) to vote against.

[Paul](https://www.paul.senate.gov/), in an [opinion piece published on Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/opinion/sen-rand-paul-america-war-but-americans-didnt-vote), argued that, because [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) did not consent to the attack on [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), the American people “have been robbed of a public debate.” He also offered a broadside against top lawmakers in [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/). “The congressional leadership — resigned to their own irrelevance — will gladly hand the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) the power to initiate war in exchange for plausible deniability,” he said.

The [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/), however, may soon get a chance to vote again — and again and again — on the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s war powers.

A group of six [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) [Senators](https://www.senate.gov/), angry about what they argue is insufficient transparency from the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) about this conflict, is threatening to force votes on similar [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) war power resolutions unless they get a public hearing about the war.

The tactic would have the practical effect of tying up action on the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) floor while allowing for public debate about the merits of the conflict.

It also would force [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) to, once again, face a vote on the [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) conflict.

[Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Tammy Baldwin (D-WI)](https://www.baldwin.senate.gov/)accused [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) on Capitol Hill of “abdicating” their responsibilities.

“We have filed our resolutions in order to get our [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) colleagues to acknowledge their duties as senators — to be a check and balance to this [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/),” she told reporters Monday.

And [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Chris Murphy (D-CT)](https://www.murphy.senate.gov/), predicted it would become “harder and harder as this war gets uglier and uglier, deadlier and deadlier, more costly and more costly for [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) to continue to vote in favor of this war.”

Jack Fitzpatrick, Nora McKee, and Peggy Helman contributed to this report.
