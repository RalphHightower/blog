## [Trump appeared to have business motive for keeping classified documents, Jack Smith finds](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-classified-documents-smith-investigation-business-motive)

_The special counsel’s office found that [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) held on to documents so secret that only six people could legally review them — and the team believed his reason for doing so was financial gain._

Mar. 25, 2026, 5:00 AM EDT
By [Carol Leonnig](https://www.ms.now/author/carol-leonnig) and [Jacqueline Alemany](https://www.ms.now/author/jacqueline-alemany)

[Special counsel Jack Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) gathered evidence that then-candidate [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) took many top secret documents that related to his worldwide business interests, and investigators considered this a likely motive for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) concealing them at his [Florida club](https://www.maralagoclub.com/) after he left the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), according to newly released case records.

The [special prosecutor](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) also had evidence indicating that after leaving office [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) had shown a classified map to passengers on a private plane, including his future chief of staff, [Susie Wiles](https://www.linkedin.com/in/susiewiles/), and took at least one document that was so secret that only six people had authority to review it, according to a memo reviewed by [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) and cited by the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Judiciary Committee](https://judiciary.house.gov/)’s ranking [Democrat](https://www.democrats.org/), [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Jamie Raskin (D-MD8)](https://raskin.house.gov/).

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s reason for [hundreds of pages of classified documents](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/judge-cannon-blocks-release-of-jack-smiths-classified-documents-report) when he left office in January 2021 — and then concealing them when the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) subpoenaed him for their return in May 2022 — has been one of the larger mysteries of the case. [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) agents conducting [an unannounced search of Trump’s Mar-a-Lago residence](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-arrest-classified-documents-probe-maralago-rcna241155) in August 2022 discovered hundreds more pages of top secret records that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and his lawyers had failed to return to the government after claiming they had fully returned all classified materials.

In a January 2023 “progress memo” reviewed by [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s office discussed the possible motive after the [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) discovered that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) held on to many documents related to his businesses.

“[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) possessed classified documents pertinent to his business interests — establishing a motive for retaining them,” according to the memo, which tracked progress in the documents and election-interference investigations. “We must have those documents.”

In a letter to [Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Pam Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney/) on Tuesday, [Raskin](https://raskin.house.gov/) insisted that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) has sought to cover up the details of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s “hoarding” of classified government secrets and storing them in his [Mar-a-Lago](https://www.maralagoclub.com/) club’s showers and closets — which put national security at risk — as well as the clues to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s motives for doing so.

“These new disclosures suggest that [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) stole documents so sensitive that only six people in the entire U.S. government had access to them, that the documents [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) stole pertained to his business interests,” [Raskin](https://raskin.house.gov/) wrote to [Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney/). 

“This glimpse into the trove of evidence behind the coverup reveals a [President of the United States](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) who may have sold out our national security to enrich himself.”

In a statement to [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) spokesperson Abigail Jackson defended [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and denied the allegations in [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s progress memo.

“It’s pathetic that [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) with zero credibility like [Jamie Raskin](https://raskin.house.gov/) are still clinging to deranged Jack [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) and his lies in 2026,” Jackson said. “[President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) did nothing wrong, which is why he easily defeated the [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/)’s unprecedented lawfare campaign against him and then won nearly 80 million votes in a landslide election victory.”

[Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) has been largely barred from discussing details of his work or evidence in the case that is not yet public. In January, [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) [declined in public testimony before Congress](https://www.ms.now/news/former-special-counsel-jack-smith-to-deliver-first-public-testimony) to describe his work other than the public filings and successful indictments he brought against [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) in the summer of 2023. He cited [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) instructions that he could not discuss the evidence in or conclusions of Volume 2 of his investigation, which was not yet public. 

[Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) testified that he believed [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) would try to use any misstep by him as justification to investigate him or bring criminal charges against him at [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s request. 

Indeed, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) took to social media on the day of [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s scheduled testimony to urge [Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney/) to investigate [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) for weaponizing the investigation against him.

“[Jack Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) is a deranged animal, who shouldn’t be allowed to practice Law. If he were a [Republican](https://www.gop.com/), his license would be taken away from him, and far worse!” [wrote on Truth Social](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/115939889414201547). “Hopefully the [Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) is looking at what he’s done, including some of the crooked and corrupt witnesses that he was attempting to use in his case against me.”

Last month, [Judge](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/) [Aileen Cannon](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/content/judge-aileen-m-cannon) approved [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s request and [blocked the Justice Department and Smith from ever releasing Volume 2](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/judge-cannon-blocks-release-of-jack-smiths-classified-documents-report) of his report on the classified documents case he brought in [Florida](https://www.myflorida.gov/) and the evidence he had gathered. After [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s team presented evidence against [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), a grand jury indicted him on 37 counts of withholding classified national security information and obstructing justice by misleading investigators.  

But [Smith’s case collapsed](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DQVa4BbkSI4/) when [Cannon](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/content/judge-aileen-m-cannon) dismissed the charges in July 2024 after her conclusion that [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) had not been properly appointed. 

These new revelations emerged after [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) released a tranche of records on March 13 to the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Judiciary Committee](https://judiciary.house.gov/). Most of them deal with the [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/)’s “Arctic Frost” probe, which examined how [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s campaign and [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) allies sought to block the certification of [Joe Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/)’s election victory. But that tranche, [Raskin](https://raskin.house.gov/) said, included the January 2023 tracking memo from [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s office following both cases, which much of the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/)’s release focused on.

[Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) in [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) have cited some specific documentation in the election interference case to claim that [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) and the [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/)-era [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) politically and improperly targeted [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) allies for investigation.

“Apparently blinded by the frenzied search to find any scrap of evidence that could be twisted and distorted to level an attack against [Special Counsel Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) (despite constantly coming up empty-handed), you have, quite amazingly, missed the fact that some of the documents you provided include damning evidence about your boss’s conduct and may well violate the gag order your [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) and [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) demanded from [Judge](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/) [Aileen Cannon](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/content/judge-aileen-m-cannon),” [Raskin](https://raskin.house.gov/) wrote [Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney/).

[DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) spokesperson [Chad Gilmartin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/chad-gilmartin-0904a3115/) called [Raskin](https://raskin.house.gov/)’s claims about [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s conduct and his [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) a “cheap political stunt.” 

“[Jack Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s team was desperate to prosecute [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/)’s top political opponent, so it is no surprise that his files contain salacious and untrue claims about [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/),” [Gilmartin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/chad-gilmartin-0904a3115/) said. “The accusations [Raskin](https://raskin.house.gov/) makes are baseless. [Judge](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/) [Cannon](https://www.flsd.uscourts.gov/content/judge-aileen-m-cannon)’s protective order was not violated, and none of the documents produced by [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) violated 6e as none of them disclosed matters occurring before a grand jury.”
