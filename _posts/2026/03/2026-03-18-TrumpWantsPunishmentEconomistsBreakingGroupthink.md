## [White House eyes punishments for economists who told the truth about tariffs, consumers](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/white-house-eyes-punishments-for-economists-who-told-the-truth-about-tariffs-consumers)

_[Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s top economist didn’t just disagree with consensus economic research, he also suggested he wants to see researchers “disciplined.”_

Feb. 18, 2026, 3:31 PM EST
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

Throughout his second term, [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has heard economists tell him that American consumers will end up shouldering the increased costs associated with his trade tariffs. The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has consistently rejected those analyses, despite everything we know about Economics 101.

Unfortunately for the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), almost a year after [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) advanced his tariffs agenda, experts have scrutinized the evidence and concluded that their predictions were correct. From the [Congressional Budget Office](https://www.cbo.gov/publication/61882?) to [international scholars](https://www.kielinstitut.de/publications/news/americas-own-goal-americans-pay-almost-entirely-for-trumps-tariffs/?) to the [National Bureau of Economic Research](https://www.nber.org/papers/w34620), all of the available data points in the same direction: American consumers, not foreign companies, are paying more as a result of the Republican [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s policy.

Last week, still more evidence emerged. Research published by economists at the [Federal Reserve Bank of New York](https://www.newyorkfed.org/) and [Columbia University](https://www.columbia.edu/content/) found that, through last fall, 90% of the economic burden from [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s tariffs fell on Americans, not foreigners.

The findings didn’t generate a ton of headlines or national discussion, which was understandable. After all, the economists and researchers had simply found evidence that proved what we already knew. That said, the reports made reality clear, in case there were any lingering doubts.

The trouble is, [Team Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) doesn’t want economists and researchers to make reality clear.

During a Wednesday-morning appearance on [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/), Kevin Hassett, the director of the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) National Economic Council, not only disagreed with the extensive research, he also suggested that the [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) punish the researchers who worked with [Columbia](https://www.columbia.edu/content/) on a study the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) didn’t like.

“The people associated with this paper should presumably be disciplined,” he said.

[Justin Wolfers](https://fordschool.umich.edu/faculty/justin-wolfers), an economist at the [University of Michigan](https://www.umich.edu/), mocked Hassett’s on-air comments, [writing via Bluesky](https://bsky.app/profile/justinwolfers.bsky.social/post/3mf5hgeepws2a), “Happy Kevin wants economists to be disciplined by their bosses for contradicting the regime’s understanding of The Truth.”

[Paul Krugman](https://substack.com/@paulkrugman) [released a related video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lzZdxmSmBaM) on the subject, making a compelling case that Hassett’s call to discipline economists was “thuggish.” [Krugman](https://substack.com/@paulkrugman) added, “That’s incredible. That’s like saying, ‘If you carefully study the data and come up with results that we in the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) don’t like, we will punish you personally — or we will try to.”

In all likelihood, if the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) were serious about targeting the researchers, those efforts would fail. But as [Krugman](https://substack.com/@paulkrugman) concluded, the public threat is itself “horrible.”

The war on dissent is ongoing, and there’s every reason to believe it’s getting worse.
