## [New GDP data paints an even uglier picture on the faltering Trump-era economy](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/new-gdp-data-paints-an-even-uglier-picture-on-the-faltering-trump-era-economy)

_In the first year of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s second term, economic growth fell to a nine-year low, and job growth fell to a 16-year low. The [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) hasn’t said why._

Mar. 13, 2026, 10:37 AM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

Throughout last year, as the U.S. economy struggled, [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) officials repeatedly insisted that there was good news on the way, and Americans wouldn’t have to wait too much longer to see satisfying results. As recently as August, for example, [Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/) [Secretary Scott Bessent](https://home.treasury.gov/about/general-information/officials/scott-bessent) [confidently predicted to a national television audience](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/americans-sour-trump-economy-white-house-moves-goalposts-rcna245121) that the U.S. economy is “really going to pick up in the fourth quarter” of 2025.

It did not pick up in the fourth quarter of 2025. [CNBC reported](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/fourth-quarter-gdp-revised-down-to-just-0point7percent-growth-january-core-inflation-was-3point1percent.html):

> _Economic growth was much slower than expected in the final three months of 2025 while core inflation rose to start 2026, the [Commerce Department](https://www.commerce.gov/) reported Friday._

Gross domestic product, a measure of all the goods and services produced across the sprawling U.S. economy, rose at a seasonally and inflation-adjusted annual rate of just 0.7% in the fourth quarter, according to the department’s [Bureau of Economic Analysis]().

The original expectations for the fourth quarter, spanning October through December, was 2.5% growth. This led to [disappointment a month ago](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/new-gdp-data-falls-short-trump) when a preliminary tally showed 1.4% growth.

This newly revised figure showed the economy grew at just half of that earlier, disappointing data.

What’s more, in light of this new information, we now know that the economy grew at a 2.1% pace across all of 2025 — down from 2.8% in 2024. Excluding the pandemic, 2025 showed the weakest economic growth in the United States [in nine years](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/A191RL1A225NBEA).

In other words, despite endless [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) hype, economic growth [and job growth](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/team-trump-struggles-to-spin-on-the-worst-job-numbers-since-the-great-recession) were significantly stronger during [Joe Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/)’s final year in office compared with the first year of [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s second term.

The [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has not yet offered a persuasive explanation as to why the economy got worse after the [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) returned to the [Oval Office](https://www.whitehouse.gov/).

On the contrary, during one of [JD Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/)’s recent [Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/) appearances, [the vice president celebrated](https://www.foxnews.com/video/6389706829112) the “[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) boom” in the economy. A week earlier, Peter Navarro, a leading [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) voice on trade and economic policy, told Fox News that the U.S. economy [was “perfect.”](https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mevtv6dd6226)

If leading members of [Team Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) were trying to appear out of touch, would they have said anything different?
