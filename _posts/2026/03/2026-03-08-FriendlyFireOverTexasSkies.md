## [Democrats decry Trump admin’s latest reported drone debacle](https://www.ms.now/opinion/drone-texas-el-paso-balloon-laser-cbp-dei-duckworth)
_With a new incident reported in [Texas](https://www.texas.gov/), the incompetence and security risks at [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/), [FAA](https://www.faa.gov/) and [DHS](https://www.dhs.gov/) seem more glaring than ever._

Feb. 27, 2026, 4:39 PM EST
By [Ja'han Jones](https://www.ms.now/author/jahan-jones)

It was just weeks ago that [rather embarrassing incident](https://www.ms.now/news/el-paso-faa-air-space-laser) by [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s militaristic [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) led to an airspace shutdown in [El Paso](https://www.elpasotexas.gov/), [Texas](https://www.texas.gov/). And after that incident, in which [U.S. Customs and Border Protection](https://www.cbl.gov/) officials fired an anti-drone laser after [reportedly mistaking a party balloon](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7REQ6SWDRE0) for a [“cartel drone incursion,”](https://x.com/SecDuffy/status/2021594420806639787?s=20) we now have an absurdly similar incident.

On Thursday, top [congressional](https://www.congress.gov/) [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) were again questioning the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s competence after the [Defense Department](https://www.defense.gov/) reportedly fired another laser in [Texas](https://www.texas.gov/) — at what turned out to be a [CBP](https://www.cbp.gov/) drone. [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/) [first reported](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/26/us/drone-faa-dhs-el-paso-airspace.html) the news, saying the incident resulted in the temporary closure of airspace above [Fort Hancock](https://www.co.hudspeth.tx.us/), a community not far from [El Paso](https://www.elpasotexas.gov/). [The Times](https://www.nytimes.com/)’ report, which hasn’t been independently confirmed by [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), suggested there was poor communication between [CBP](https://www.cbp.gov/) and the [Defense Department](https://www.defense.gov/).

A joint statement to the [Times](https://www.nytimes.com/) from the [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/), the [Federal Aviation Administration](https://www.faa.gov/) and [CBP](https://www.cbp.gov/) didn’t include a denial:

> _‘This reported engagement occurred when the [Department of War](https://www.defense.gov/) employed counter-unmanned aircraft system authorities to mitigate a seemingly threatening unmanned aerial system operating within military airspace,’ the statement read, adding that the [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/), the [F.A.A.](https://www.faa.gov/) and [Customs and Border Protection](https://www.cbp.gov/) ‘will continue to work on increased cooperation and communication to prevent such incidents in the future.’_

You may have noticed that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and his allies have spent ample time peddling bigoted lies that diversity, equity and inclusion policies were breeding incompetence and security risks at the [Pentagon](https://thefulcrum.us/governance-legislation/hegseth-anti-dei-military-national-security) and, particularly, the [FAA](https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2025/01/trump-dc-plane-crash-dei-conspiracy/). And yet, despite the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) [declaring that DEI is dead](https://www.whitehouse.gov/past-events/dei-is-dead-under-the-trump-administration/) is dead on [numerous occasions](https://thegrio.com/2026/02/25/trump-gleefully-celebrated-the-end-of-dei-at-the-sotu-but-has-it-really-ended/), it’s noteworthy that the incompetence and security risks at these agencies seem more glaring than ever.

In a [joint statement of their own](https://democrats-homeland.house.gov/news/press-releases/ranking-members-larsen-thompson-and-carson-statement-after-dod-reportedly-shot-down-cbp-drone), [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) [Reps.](https://www.house.gov/) [Bennie Thompson (D-MS2)](https://benniethompson.house.gov/), [André Carson (D-IN7)](https://carson.house.gov/) and [Rick Larsen (D-WA2)](https://larsen.house.gov/) — who each sit on homeland security and transportation-related committees — were apoplectic:

> _Our heads are exploding over the news that [DoD](https://www.defense.gov/) reportedly shot down a [Customs and Border Protection](https://www.cbp.gov/) drone using a high-risk counter-unmanned aircraft system. We said MONTHS ago that the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s decision to sidestep a bipartisan, tri-committee bill to appropriately train C-UAS operators and address the lack of coordination between the [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/), [DHS](https://www.dhs.gov/) and the [FAA](https://www.faa.gov/) was a short-sighted idea. Now, we’re seeing the result of its incompetence._

The lawmakers are referring to [bipartisan legislation they backed](https://transportation.house.gov/news/documentsingle.aspx?DocumentID=409042#:~:text=The%20Transportation%20and%20Infrastructure%20Committee%20(T&I)%20approved,to%20partner%20with%20critical%20infrastructure%20facility%20operators) they backed that supports more training and improved federal coordination to assist “counter-unmanned aircraft system” operators — or people who take out drones.

[Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) are using the latest [Texas](https://www.texas.gov/) incident to highlight the urgent need for such legislation. [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Tammy Duckworth (D-IL)](https://www.duckworth.senate.gov/) said the reported lack of coordination is reminiscent of the [deadly air crash](https://www.ms.now/news/trumps-safety-board-chair-blames-faa-for-100-preventable-d-c-air-crash) that occurred shortly after [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) took office last year, in which a [Defense Department](https://www.defense.gov/) [helicopter collided with a commercial flight over the Potomac River](https://www.ntsb.gov/investigations/Pages/DCA25MA108.aspx) in [Washington](https://dc.gov/).

“It’s this kind of failure to communicate between [DoD](https://www.defense.gov/) and [FAA](https://www.faa.gov/) that led to the tragic loss of life above [DCA](https://www.flyreagan.com/),” [Duckworth](https://www.duckworth.senate.gov/) [wrote on X](https://x.com/SenDuckworth/status/2027235390889894050), referring to [Reagan National Airport](https://www.flyreagan.com/). She added:

> _It’s also deeply troubling to once again learn about this chaos in the news — rather than through official channels.<br /><br />This situation is alarming and demands a thorough, independent investigation._

I’ll be calling on the IGs of [DoD](https://www.defense.gov/), [DOT](https://www.transportation.gov/) and [DHS](https://www.dhs.gov/) to launch a joint investigation immediately.”

[Ja'han Jones](https://www.ms.now/author/jahan-jones) is an [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) opinion blogger. He previously wrote The ReidOut Blog.
