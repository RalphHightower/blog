## [High gas prices from Trump’s war on Iran are his next political crisis](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-gas-prices-iran-oil-hormuz-war-sanctions)

_The [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) war is creating an affordability problem [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) can't talk his way out of._

Mar. 23, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Zeeshan Aleem](https://www.ms.now/author/zeeshan-aleem)

The [surge in gas prices](https://www.ms.now/news/oil-natural-gas-prices-soar-iran-attacks-gulf-energy) triggered by [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [ill-conceived war on Iran](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-war-plan-attack) is shaping up to be a political crisis for the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/). The changes make an [unpopular military conflict](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/latest-polling-shows-the-war-in-iran-is-already-unpopular-at-a-historic-scale) tangible in Americans’ everyday lives, and they exacerbate an issue that was already a weakness for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) (and his predecessor): [affordability](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-poll-cost-living-economy-affordability).

Two things should be particularly worrisome to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/): It’s unclear exactly when the price increases will end, and things could still become worse before they get better.

> **These numbers should be flashing warning signs for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) as the midterm elections approach.**

A [Yahoo/YouGov poll](https://www.yahoo.com/news/politics/article/poll-66-of-americans-disapprove-of-trump-on-gas-prices-as-they-feel-effects-of-iran-war-120000483.html) published last week found that gas prices are already doing serious damage to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s popularity. The poll, a survey of about 1,700 U.S. adults conducted March 12-16, found that 66% disapprove of how the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is handling gas prices. Only 27% approve, it found. A similar breakdown emerged over cost of living: 67% disapprove of the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s approach, while 26% approve.

The poll also documented a plunge in public perception of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s handling of the economy compared with findings a month earlier. Approval dropped from 37% to 32%, and disapproval rose from 57% to 61%.

These numbers should be flashing warning signs for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) as the midterm elections approach. Consider this finding from Yahoo News’ own writeup of the poll: “Yahoo and YouGov have never measured a lower economic approval rating for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) — not even at the height of the COVID-19 pandemic during his first term.” And [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s approval rating on his approach to cost of living is the “lowest to date” in Yahoo/YouGov polls.

Consumer frustration with rising gas prices likely explains why [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s overall job approval rating, which has declined fairly steadily since his second term began in 2025, has hit new lows in polling aggregators, including the ones run by polling analyst [Nate Silver](https://x.com/NateSilver538/status/2034297804684751233) and [RealClearPolitics](https://www.realclearpolling.com/polls/approval/donald-trump/approval-rating).

Growing disapproval is not [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s only challenge. There is also the very real affordability problem — you know, the issue [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has dismissed as a [Democratic “hoax”](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/11/nx-s1-5639957/trump-affordability-hoax-economy-midterms), claimed to have [won” and “solved”](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-economic-policies-affordability), and belittled as a matter of [buying fewer dolls and pencils](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/you-can-give-up-certain-products-trump-tells-americans-to-settle-for-less). [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is increasingly at risk of becoming branded with the stigma of unaffordability the way that [President Joe Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) eventually was. But there is a key difference in the two situations: For [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/), inflation was [neither entirely his fault nor entirely under his control](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/06/06/us/politics/biden-inflation-greedflation-economy.html). But [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [deserves all of the blame](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-war-gas-prices-hormuz) for the recent rise in oil prices. And Americans can see this — and feel it in their wallets.

In just the few weeks since [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) launched attacks against [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), the average price of gas in the U.S. has [surged nearly a dollar a gallon](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/18/upshot/gas-prices-lookup.html). According to [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/), “It’s the second-largest four-week increase in at least 30 years — bigger than the one at the start of the war in [Ukraine](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/) in 2022, or the ones associated with the [post-recession surge of 2009](https://www.theguardian.com/business/2009/jun/10/oil-price-increase) and the [OPEC production cuts in 1999](https://www.nytimes.com/1999/07/07/business/the-markets-commodities-oil-price-up-as-opec-s-cuts-seem-to-hold.html).” The only time prices spiked faster was in 2005 right after Hurricane Katrina.

Gas prices are advertised on signs all across the country, which means that for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), reminders of his error are about as visible as they can get. As of mid-March, the average U.S. driver is spending more than \$30 extra per month on gas, according to the Times.

And there are [no quick solutions on the horizon](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2026/03/23/world/iran-war-oil-trump/f185b851-2149-56cb-bcf7-2cb9e592c9d9?smid=url-share). [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s pleas to other countries for help securing the Strait of Hormuz — the key route for oil supplies whose closure by [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) is the core reason oil prices are surging — [have been rejected](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-strait-hormuz-nato-allies-germany-china). [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [lifted sanctions on some Iranian oil](https://www.ms.now/news/u-s-war-iran-oil-sanctions-israel) on Friday in a bid to lower global prices, but energy analysts estimate that the measure is [unlikely to significantly impact prices](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/mar/20/us-sanctions-iranian-oil) while [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) still controls the strait. (The move also [gives Iran a financial boost](https://x.com/PhilGordonDC/status/2035346997343866924?).)

U.S. efforts to secure the strait without assistance are likely to [take weeks, at best](https://www.wsj.com/world/middle-east/u-s-war-planes-and-helicopters-kick-off-battle-to-reopen-hormuz-530cdb78). Meanwhile, [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) is also placing pressure on the global oil supply by [attacking energy infrastructure](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/03/19/us-iran-war-trump-gas-prices-qatar/) in Gulf countries. Multiple countries and market analysts have predicted the price of oil could [spike dramatically in the coming weeks](https://www.wsj.com/finance/commodities-futures/saudi-arabia-sees-a-spike-to-180-oil-if-energy-shock-persists-past-april-2fe729d7?st=TAy9Bt&). If sustained long enough that could, [in turn, trigger a recession](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2026-03-13/iran-war-the-white-house-s-oil-restraint-toolbox-is-empty).

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) himself [has acknowledged](https://www.politico.com/news/2025/11/21/trump-mamdani-make-love-not-war-00665861) that “affordability” is the political watchword of the moment. Yet he has taken [no meaningful measures to address it](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/23/us/politics/trump-economy-fact-check.html), and his tariff agenda has [only made the problem worse.](https://www.hbs.edu/bigs/us-trade-tariffs-increasing-prices). When it comes to oil prices, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has no excuses and nowhere to hide — and it’s hard to imagine him blustering his way out of this problem of his own making.
