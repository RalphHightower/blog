## [Trump officially nominates Kevin Warsh as Fed chair to replace Jerome Powell](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-nominates-kevin-warsh-fed-chair)

_The [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) on Wednesday took the first formal step toward achieving [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s goal of installing a rate cut-friendly chair of the [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/)._

Mar. 4, 2026, 3:19 PM EST
By [Sydney Carruth](https://www.ms.now/author/sydney-carruth)

[President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [officially asked](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/03/nominations-sent-to-the-senate-b376/) the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/)to consider Kevin Warsh, a former [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) governor, to ‌be the central bank’s next chair.

The formal nomination is the first step in what [could be an uphill battle](https://x.com/SenThomTillis/status/2017221633287590355?s=20) for the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) as he sets out to install a rate cut-friendly economist at the helm of the [Fed.](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) Warsh would succeed [Jerome Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm), whom [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has [repeatedly attacked](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-powell-republicans-congress-red-line) for declining to cut interest rates.

If confirmed by the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/), Warsh would begin his four-year term after [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm)’s term ends on May 15. If the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) gets its way, he’d also be confirmed for a 14-year term as a member of the [board of governors](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/default.htm) of the [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/).

That’s the board seat currently held by [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) governor [Stephen Miran](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/miran.htm), another rate-cut advocate whom [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) nominated in September. [Miran](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/miran.htm)’s term expired at the end of January, but he can stay on the board until a successor is confirmed.

Warsh is a [favorite in Republican circles](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-names-kevin-warsh-as-next-federal-reserve-chair). A former [Morgan Stanley](https://www.morganstanley.com/) investment banker, he was a governor on the [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/)’s board in the early 2000s after being nominated by [President George W. Bush](https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/). He is now a visiting fellow at [Stanford University](https://www.stanford.edu/)’s [Hoover Institution](https://www.hoover.org/). Warsh must be confirmed by the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) to serve as chair.

That process starts with the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Banking Committee](http://www.banking.senate.gov/), which could spell trouble for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/). Retiring [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Thom Tillis (T-NC)](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/), has vowed to block any [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) nomination as long as a [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) investigation ​into [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) ​over his testimony ⁠to the panel about [Federal Reserve](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/prosecutors-open-investigation-into-fed-chair-powell-sparking-immediate-backlash) in Washington remains ongoing.

[Tillis](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/) is a key vote on the [banking committee](http://www.banking.senate.gov/), which handles confirmation hearings for [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) appointees and has a narrow 13-11 [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) majority.

[Tillis](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/), along with [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Kevin Cramer (T-D)](https://www.cramer.senate.gov/), who is also on the committee, has condemned the investigation as an intimidation tactic by [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/). The [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) is investigating whether [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) misled [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) about the ongoing \$2.5 billion building renovation project at the [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/)’s headquarters in Washington. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has repeatedly slammed [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) about the cost of the renovations. [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) has defended the project as necessary for safety reasons.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has threatened to fire [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm), whom the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) nominated during his first term in office, on many occasions because of the [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/)’s refusal to cut rates at his request. But [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm), and every living former [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) chair, [denounced the probe](https://www.theguardian.com/business/2026/jan/12/trump-condemn-federal-reserve-powell-investigation) as an act of partisanship against the leader of an institution that is designed to be insulated from political pressure.
