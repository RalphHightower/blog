---

[Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/), to put it in real estate terms, is a demolition buy. You want the property, but the buildings are ramshackle. The only alternative is to tear down and rebuild.

- The electric grid is kaput. It is off more than it is on. It is beyond bailing wire and duct repair.
- The healthcare is in shambles with decaying buildings, insufficient medications and medical supplies.
- The land is encumbered with property ownership from the pre-Communist period. Cuba took over private property as it's a trait of Communism. Their constitution approved private ownership of property, so there are legal problems with who owns what.

# [Trump’s Cuba strategy is straightforward. The outcome will be anything but.](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-cuba-talks-challenges)

_[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) seems confident he can do to [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) what he did to [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/) just over two months ago. He shouldn't be._

Mar. 15, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Daniel R. DePetris](https://www.defensepriorities.org/people/daniel-depetris/)

Even as the [war in Iran](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-war-11-billion-income-housing) remains in full swing, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is already eying his next target: [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/).

Washington’s oldest adversary in the Western hemisphere is in the midst of an economic and social crisis the likes of which the [Cuban Communist Party](http://www.pcc.cu/) hasn’t confronted since the 1990s. That’s when the collapse of its former patron, the Soviet Union, forced the island to make do with a lot less. The [Trump administration’s Cuba policy](https://www.ms.now/news/doj-running-quiet-operation-in-miami-to-hunt-for-charges-on-cuban-leaders-per-sources) is only exacerbating those fissures.

The strategy is straightforward: place so much financial pressure on the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government that it has no option but to meet [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s demands, like opening up the country to a multi-party democracy. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [Jan. 29 executive order](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/01/addressing-threats-to-the-united-states-by-the-government-of-cuba/) slapping any country that transports or exports fuel to [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) with tariffs, combined with a new [U.S. Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) initiative that [seeks to indict senior Cuban officials](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/03/06/justice-department-cuba-trump-miami/7c2272ce-19aa-11f1-aef0-0aac8e8e94db_story.html), is meant to provide [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) with even more leverage in the ongoing [talks with Havana](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/13/world/americas/cuba-us-talks-trump-oil.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share).

If the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is looking to emulate its success in [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/), it’s likely kicking on a locked door.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) seems confident he can do to [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) what he did to [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/) more than two months ago — decapitate the senior leadership, work with more pragmatic underlings and bring a former U.S. adversary into Washington’s orbit.

“As we achieve a historic transformation in [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/), we’re also looking forward to the great change that will soon be coming to [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/),” [Trump said](https://rollcall.com/factbase/trump/transcript/donald-trump-speech-shield-americas-summit-doral-florida-march-7-2026/) at a [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) event on March 7. His top diplomat in [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/), [Mike Hammer](https://cu.usembassy.gov/chief-of-mission/), [even hinted](https://en.cibercuba.com/noticias/2026-02-12-u1-e43231-s27061-nid320714-mike-hammer-sugiere-eeuu-tendria-delcy-rodriguez-cuba) there could be a [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/)-like equivalent to [Delcy Rodríguez](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php), who took over for former [Venezuelan](https://gob.ve/) dictator [Nicolás Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php) after his capture by U.S. forces in [Caracas](https://www.caracas.gob.ve/) and is now cooperating with the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) on everything from deportations to oil exports.

But if the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is looking to emulate its success in [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/), it’s likely kicking on a locked door.

First, it should be noted that this is hardly the first time in U.S. history that an American president has tried to squeeze the island into submission. From the moment [Fidel Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidel_Castro?wprov=sfla1) ousted the U.S.-backed regime of [Fulgencio Batista](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulgencio_Batista?wprov=sfla1) in 1959, the U.S. has sought to overthrow the [Cuban Communist Party](http://www.pcc.cu/) in its entirety or at least weaken it to the point where a negotiated transition to democracy is possible. [John F. Kennedy](https://www.jfklibrary.org/) instituted a comprehensive [trade embargo](https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/proclamation-3447-embargo-all-trade-with-cuba) on the island and approved the infamous [Bay of Pigs operation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Pigs_Invasion?wprov=sfla1) to oust [Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidel_Castro?wprov=sfla1). [Lyndon Johnson](https://www.lbjlibrary.org/) green-lit sabotage operations against [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/). [Jimmy Carter](https://www.jimmycarterlibrary.gov/jimmy-carter-presidential-library-and-museum) sought to negotiate with [Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidel_Castro?wprov=sfla1), only to pull out of talks. [George W. Bush](https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/) [increased travel restrictions](https://www.nbcnews.com/id/wbna4388549) to the island and strengthened U.S. support for anti-regime opponents. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), meanwhile, overturned [Barack Obama](https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov_)’s diplomatic normalization and replaced it with caps on remittances and more financial sanctions.

None of those efforts worked to change the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) regime from within, let alone topple it. [Fidel Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidel_Castro?wprov=sfla1), [Raul Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Castro?wprov=sfla1) and now [Miguel Díaz-Canel](https://www.presidencia.gob.cu/es/presidencia/) have all used the U.S. embargo and subsequent pressure tactics as a convenient excuse to explain away their own failed economic policies. The only people who have been negatively impacted by U.S. policy over the last six decades are the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) people themselves, whose lives are a constant struggle for basic necessities and who are effectively penalized for their own rulers’ incompetence. Is more of the same really going to bring different results?

Second, the [Cuban Communist Party](http://www.pcc.cu/) is [more unified and durable](https://www.wsj.com/world/americas/the-u-s-is-actively-seeking-regime-change-in-cuba-by-the-end-of-the-year-1d0f178a?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqdcWycGSWPH5OG1ZOLsnes8NUcn8ZZhVQPrEJCZO0jfzSZ14fXWPuXC&gaa_ts=69b3342e&gaa_sig=sdUKqfYTZ1Fz2sOLkx5i8ZPrwglTbGlCap0zVYLQQN07joQAOh3_07HWcfyxPFe5XNP4YNy8-QNzuxQSA3aScQ==) than [Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php)’s regime ever was, which means attempts by the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) to crack it by searching for a more pliable successor will prove more difficult. While [Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php) fancied himself as [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/)’s decisive autocrat, the fact is his position depended on the ability to manage [multiple factions and personalities within the Venezuelan government](https://www.crisisgroup.org/stm/latin-america-caribbean/venezuela-united-states/venezuela-after-maduro-transaction-or-transition), including [Delcy Rodríguez](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php), his interior minister, [Diosdado Cabello](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diosdado_Cabello?wprov=sfla1) and his defense minister, [Vladimir Padrino López](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Padrino_L%C3%B3pez?wprov=sfla1). [Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php) did this in large part by allowing these figures to run their own fiefdoms and earn money through illegal activity. [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/)’s government, in other words, was less a solid structure than a constellation of competing ministers, some of whom had different interests and prerogatives. One of those figures, [Rodríguez](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php), already showed that she was amenable to working with Washington economically when she negotiated with U.S. officials last year over [Chevron](https://www.chevron.com/)’s access to [Venezuelan](https://gob.ve/) oil fields.

Any dissent within the [Cuban Communist Party](http://www.pcc.cu/) or the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) military’s ranks is dealt with harshly, which deters officials in the bureaucracy from engaging in similar activity.

If there is a figure like this in [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/), [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/)’s security services will know the answer. The regime might be awful at governance but it’s quite capable at [snuffing out the smallest signs of discontent](https://www.state.gov/reports/2021-country-reports-on-human-rights-practices/cuba/) and disloyalty. There is no organized democratic opposition on the island, and those brave enough to protest on the streets are rapidly arrested, thrown in prison and in some cases [sentenced for 30-year terms](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-latin-america-60764403). Any dissent within the [Cuban Communist Party](http://www.pcc.cu/) or the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) military’s ranks is dealt with harshly, which deters officials in the bureaucracy from engaging in similar activity.

[Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) isn’t powerless to resist U.S. demands either. Unlike [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/), the island is only 90 miles off [Florida](https://www.myflorida.gov/), and if the regime’s back is against the wall, it will do what it can to complicate U.S. plans. Chief among the levers [Havana](https://www.lahabana.gob.cu/) can pull is irregular migration into the U.S. Some might view such a scheme as reckless or unlikely, but the regime has done it several times before.

The [1980 Mariel boat lift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariel_boatlift?wprov=sfla1), which resulted in an influx of 125,000 [Cubans](http://cubaminrex.cu/) to U.S. shores, was deliberately stoked by [Fidel Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidel_Castro?wprov=sfla1) at a time when U.S.-[Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) relations [were at a low point](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/cuba/2025-01-15/carter-and-cuba-legacy-dedicated-diplomacy-toward-normalization). Fourteen years later, as [Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidel_Castro?wprov=sfla1) was getting frustrated with the [Clinton](https://www.clintonlibrary.gov/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) and dealing with an economic collapse, [Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidel_Castro?wprov=sfla1) instigated yet another migration outflow (known as the “[Balsero Crisis](https://www.migrationpolicy.org/article/cuban-migration-postrevolution-exodus-ebbs-and-flows)”), which eventually forced Washington [to negotiate an immigration accord](https://www.nytimes.com/1994/08/25/world/flight-from-cuba-the-overview-castro-rules-out-any-move-to-halt-flow-of-refugees.html) with [Havana](https://www.lahabana.gob.cu/) to stem the tide.

The [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) is currently in discrete talks with [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/). [Raul Guillermo Rodríguez Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Guillermo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Castro?wprov=sfla1), [Raul Castro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Castro?wprov=sfla1)’s grandson, is taking the lead on the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) side. Whether those negotiations succeed will be determined in part on whether [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is willing to be realistic with his demands.

But if one of his goals happens to be a [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government doing his bidding, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is setting himself up for disappointment.
