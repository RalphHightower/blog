##  [Trump warns of ‘war’ casualties without Congress declaring war](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-iran-strikes-casualties-war-congress-reaction)

_Lawmakers in both parties condemned the strikes as unconstitutional, even as the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) warned of potential American casualties._

Feb. 28, 2026, 4:55 AM EST
By [Kevin Frey](https://www.ms.now/author/kevin-frey) and [Nora McKee](https://www.ms.now/author/nora-mckee)

[President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [launched military strikes against Iran](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-attack-iran-israel-strikes) early Saturday and warned that the “lives of American heroes may be lost” in what he described as a “war,” triggering a swift backlash from lawmakers over his decision to attack without congressional authorization.

In [an eight-minute video posted on Truth Social](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116147082884192486), [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) announced that “the [United States military](https://www.defense.gov/) began major combat operations in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/),” called the operation “massive and ongoing,” and suggested that “the lives of courageous American heroes may be lost and we may have casualties — that often happens in war.”

The statement drew immediate rebukes from lawmakers who invoked the costs of past wars in the Middle East.

“I lost friends in [Iraq](https://ur.gov.iq/) to an illegal war. Young working-class kids should not pay the ultimate price for regime change and a war that hasn’t been explained or justified to the American people,” [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Ruben Gallego, D-AZ)](https://www.gallego.senate.gov/), [said in a post on X](https://x.com/SenRubenGallego/status/2027659990614315149), referencing his service as a [Marine](https://www.marines.mil/).

“We can support the democracy movement and the [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) people without sending our troops to die,” he continued.

[Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Thomas Massie (T-KY4)](https://massie.house.gov/) — a frequent critic of the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) who has called for [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) to reassert itself on the power to declare war — quickly [labeled the strikes](https://x.com/RepThomasMassie/status/2027639993414430864?s=20) “acts of war unauthorized by [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/).”

[Massie](https://massie.house.gov/) is one of the lead [of a war powers resolution](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-concurrent-resolution/38/text) aimed at reining in the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s ability to pursue military action against [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) without congressional approval.

[House](https://www.house.gov/) [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) were poised to force votes this week on that [Massie](https://massie.house.gov/) resolution, which is co-led by [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Ro Khanna (D-CA17)](https://khanna.house.gov/). [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) were planning to force a vote on a similar resolution this week as well.

Prior to Saturday’s attack, [the votes on those resolutions were expected to be close](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-showdown-iran-lawmakers-congress-war-powers), with defections from members in both parties and chambers. [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) have been divided over the value of an all-out push for regime change in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), as opposed to a limited strike. [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) were facing defections from centrists wary of [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s threatening stance toward [Israel](https://www.gov.il/). Both groups may be tested by the strikes, [which the U.S. and Israel jointly conducted](https://www.ms.now/news/apparent-strike-in-irans-capital-happened-near-offices-of-supreme-leader-ayatollah-ali-khamenei) and which [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) suggested have the ultimate goal of toppling the [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) regime.

“To the great, proud people of [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), I say tonight that the hour of your freedom is at hand,” [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) said [in a video statement](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116147082884192486). “When we are finished, take over your government. It will be yours to take.”

That approach may be welcomed by [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Majority Leader](https://bioguide.congress.gov/search/bio/T000250) [John Thune (T-SD)](https://www.thune.senate.gov/), who earlier this week threw his weight behind a push for regime change.

“In my view, if you’re going to do something there, you better well make it about getting new leadership,” Thune told reporters Thursday.

[Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Lindsey Graham, (T-SC)](https://www.lgraham.senate.gov/) — a key [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) ally and critic of the [Obama](https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/)-era [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) nuclear deal  who has advocated military action — [applauded](https://x.com/LindseyGrahamSC/status/2027668221642334226) the strikes, calling them “necessary” and “long justified.”

“The end of the largest state sponsor of terrorism is upon us. God bless [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), our military and our allies in [Israel](https://www.gov/),” [Graham](https://www.lgraham.senate.gov/) wrote.

In a [subsequent social media post](https://x.com/lindseygrahamsc/status/2027675598319964548?s=46), [Graham](https://www.lgraham.senate.gov/) also addressed the potential for U.S. casualties, writing, “If you are injured or fall, I believe with all my heart that your sacrifice makes your country and the world a better and safer place. This moment is why you chose to serve.”

And — underscoring the cross-party disagreements on the potential for action against [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) — [Pennsylvania](www.pa.gov/)’s [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) [Sen. John Fetterman praised the Saturday morning operation, writing](https://x.com/SenFettermanPA/status/2027674658091204808), “[President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has been willing to do what’s right and necessary to produce real peace in the region.”

But the memory of America’s decades of fighting in [Iraq](https://ur.gov.iq/) and [Afghanistan](https://moi.gov.af/) has left many lawmakers wary.

[House](https://www.house.gov/) [Minority Leader](http://democraticleader.house.gov/) [Hakeem Jeffries (D-NY6)](https://jeffries.house.gov/), told reporters on Tuesday that the American people “understand that under no circumstances should the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) get us into another failed, foreign forever war when we know the outcome, particularly in the Middle East.”

“It’s going to cost American lives,” Jeffries said.

Jack Fitzpatrick contributed to this report.
