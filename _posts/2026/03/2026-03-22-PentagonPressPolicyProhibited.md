## [Judge rules Pentagon press policy unconstitutional](https://www.ms.now/news/judge-rules-pentagon-press-policy-unconstitutional)

_The ruling is a major blow to the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s effort to heavily restrict media coverage._

Mar. 20, 2026, 9:23 PM EDT
By [Ebony Davis](https://www.ms.now/author/ebony-davis)

A federal judge ruled Friday that the [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/)’s restrictive [press access policy](https://www.ms.now/news/high-stakes-first-amendment-suit-against-the-pentagon-heads-to-court) is unlawful and ordered the [Department of Defense](https://www.defense.gov/) to reinstate press credentials for affected journalists.

[its ruling](https://www.ms.now/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/gov.uscourts.dcd_.287334.35.0_1-1.pdf), the [U.S. District Court for Washington, D.C.](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/), found that the policy — which allowed officials to revoke credentials from reporters who sought or published unauthorized information — violated the [First Amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-1/) and granted the government overly broad authority to control access to the press corps.

“A primary purpose of the [First Amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-1/) is to enable the press to publish what it will and the public to read what it chooses, free of any official proscription,” [U.S. District](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/) [Judge Paul Friedman](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/senior-judge-paul-l-friedman) said in the opinion.

“Those who drafted the [First Amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-1/) believed that the nation’s security requires a free press and an informed people and that such security is endangered by governmental suppression of political speech. It must not be abandoned now,” he said.

The [New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/10/15/business/media/pentagon-press-rules.html) had previously legally challenged the [policy](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/pete-hegseth-imposes-new-restrictions-pentagon-communications-congress-rcna239136), arguing it violates its [First Amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-1/) and due process.

The policy, introduced last year under [Defense](https://www.defense.gov/) [Secretary Pete Hegseth](https://www.defense.gov/About/Biographies/Biography/Article/4040890/hon-pete-hegseth/), required reporters covering the [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/) to agree not to obtain or report on information that had not been officially cleared for release — even if the material was unclassified. Critics argued the rules amounted to censorship and would punish routine news-gathering practices.

Under the guidelines, journalists risked losing credentials for conduct deemed a security risk, including attempts to access nonpublic information, a provision that alarmed major news organizations and press freedom groups.

Nearly every major U.S. news outlet, including [Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/), [refused to sign the policy](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/hegseth-eyes-new-restrictions-press-media-outlets-fox-news-refuse-rcna237628) when it was introduced, with many forfeiting their [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/) credentials in protest.

In Friday’s decision, [Friedman](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/senior-judge-paul-l-friedman) sided with media organizations that argued the policy imposed unconstitutional conditions on access. He said the policy also violated the [Fifth Amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-5/) because “it provides no way for journalists to know how they may do their jobs without losing their credentials.”

[Friedman](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/senior-judge-paul-l-friedman) emphasized that amid the U.S.’ recent military actions in [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/) and the ongoing war with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), it is critical to ensure the public has access to a broad range of information to allow Americans to support or oppose policies and make informed decisions.

“It is more important than ever that the public have access to information from a variety of perspectives about what its government is doing,” [Friedman](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/senior-judge-paul-l-friedman) said.

The [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/) said in a [statement](https://x.com/SeanParnellASW/status/2035134876639265081) on Friday it plans to appeal the decision.

“We disagree with the decision and are pursuing an immediate appeal,” [DOD](https://www.defense.gov/) spokesperson Sean Parnell said.

The ruling marks the latest legal setback for the [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/)’s efforts to tighten control over media access, which had already drawn widespread criticism from press freedom groups.

“The judge was right to see the [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/)’s outrageous censorship for what it is, but this wasn’t exactly a close call,” the [Friedman](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/senior-judge-paul-l-friedman) said in a [statement](https://freedom.press/issues/amid-war-pentagon-quashing-of-reporter-access-is-blatant-censorship/).

The [National Press Club](https://www.press.org/) [said](https://x.com/i/status/2035114499078799555) the ruling “affirmed what should be beyond dispute.”

“The government cannot pick and choose which journalists are allowed to cover it or deny access based on their reporting — a core [First Amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-1/) protection,” said [NPC](https://www.press.org/) [President Mark Schoeff](https://www.press.org/leadership).
