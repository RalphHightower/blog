## [Judge quashes grand jury subpoenas in Powell probe, dealing blow to DOJ](https://www.ms.now/news/jerome-powell-subpoenas-quashed-trump-doj)

_[Chief Judge James Boasberg](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/chief-judge-james-e-boasberg) found “essentially zero evidence” of a crime, calling the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/)'s case against the [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) [chair](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) “so thin and unsubstantiated” that it could only be pretextual._

Mar. 13, 2026, 5:30 PM EDT
By [Jacqueline Alemany](https://www.ms.now/author/jacqueline-alemany), [Carol Leonnig](https://www.ms.now/author/carol-leonnig), [Jake Traylor](https://www.ms.now/author/jake-traylor) and [Fallon Gallagher](https://www.ms.now/author/fallon-gallagher)

### Bless Her Heart. Jeanine Pirro's Unhinged Rant

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/TzGT18lmG80?si=A1VekSGx7Fh6D4GO" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe>

> [U.S. attorney Jeanine Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) reacted to a [federal judge](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/chief-judge-james-e-boasberg)'s blocking the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/)'s subpoenas against [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov) [Chair Jerome Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) and the investigation into the renovation of the [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov). 

A [federal judge](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/chief-judge-james-e-boasberg) has quashed the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/)’s subpoenas targeting [Federal Reserve](https;//www.federalreserve.gov/) [Chair Jerome Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm), according to a court filing unsealed Friday — a major blow to the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s criminal investigation into the central bank’s leader.

In a remarkable decision, [Chief Judge James E. Boasberg](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/chief-judge-james-e-boasberg) of the [U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/) wrote that “a mountain of evidence” suggested that “the Government served these subpoenas on the [Federal Reserve](https;//www.federalreserve.gov/) [Board](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/default.htm) to pressure its [Chair](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) into voting for lower interest rates or resigning.” [Boasberg](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/chief-judge-james-e-boasberg) added that federal prosecutors “produced essentially zero evidence to suspect [Chair Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) of a crime,” calling the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s case “so thin and unsubstantiated that the [Court](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/) can only conclude that they are pretextual.” 

[Boasberg](https://www.dcd.uscourts.gov/content/chief-judge-james-e-boasberg) went even further, writing that the investigation into [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) fit a “pattern” that several of the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s adversaries have now faced from [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [Department of Justice](https://www.justice.gov/). 

“Being perceived as the [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s adversary has become risky in recent years,” he wrote. “In his second term, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has urged the [Department of Justice](https://www.justice.gov/) to prosecute such people, and the [Department](https://www.justice.gov/)’s prosecutors have listened.” 

The decision casts doubt on whether the case opened by [Jeanine Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney), the U.S. attorney for the [District of Columbia](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/), can proceed.

[Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) responded immediately and defiantly at a press conference, calling the decision “wrong and without legal authority,” and claiming that it was “untethered to the law.” 

“No one is above the law, but for the first time a judge’s ruling that a grand jury subpoena on its face, legal in all regards, can be ignored because a judge thinks the subject is beyond reproach,” [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) said. She added that she would “not only [be] appealing, but making a motion to reconsider.”

[Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm)’s lawyers fought the grand jury subpoenas issued to [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) as a part of the [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/)’s investigation into the costly renovation of the [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/)’s headquarters last month after the chairman publicly argued that the investigation was motivated by [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s desire to slash interest rates. A lawyer for Powell declined to comment on Friday’s ruling.

There have been tensions between senior officials at the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) and [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) over her decision to pursue such a probe against [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm), according to two people familiar with the dynamic. [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) signed off on investigating [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) in November 2025, blindsiding senior [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) leaders and officials at the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), who were similarly frustrated by her actions and disagreed with them.

One [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) official told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) that [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney) acted irrationally in doing so, and suggested that the investigation would likely never materialize. But despite internal [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) frustration with the investigation, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) favored [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney)’s aggressiveness in pursuing [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm), someone the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has long been scathingly critical of, according to the same official.

After intense backlash from [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) allies and [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) lawmakers, who warned that the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) was endangering the central bank’s credibility and independence, officials at the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) discussed quietly closing the investigation, according to a person familiar with the matter. However, the subpoenas against [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) were ultimately not withdrawn, and [Pirro](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/staff-profile/meet-us-attorney)’s office maintained that the investigation was active and ongoing. 

The [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) and the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) did not immediately respond to requests for comment. 

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), who publicly denied knowing “[anything about](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/donald-trump/trump-denies-involvement-doj-fed-subpoena-jerome-powell-rcna253526)” the [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) probe, has waged a highly unusual and public campaign against [Powell](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-jerome-powell-federal-reserve-interest-rates-rcna240327), whom he blames for not acting fast enough to lower interest rates that affect the economy. But during a Dec. 30 news conference, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) called [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) a “fool,” and said he was considering filing a lawsuit against him over the renovation of the [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) building.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has repeatedly urged the [Justice Department](https://19thnews.org/2025/10/letitia-james-james-comey-trump/) to pursue legal actions against his political enemies, including [New York](https://www.ny.gov/) [Attorney General](https://ag.ny.gov/) [Letitia James](https://ag.ny.gov/about/meet-letitia-james) and former [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) director [James Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey), and critics worry that the investigation into [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) is an extension of that pattern.

[Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Thom Tillis (T-NC](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/), who has vowed to block all [Federal Reserve](https;//www.federalreserve.gov/) nominations over the criminal investigation into [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm), said in a [post on X](https://x.com/SenThomTillis/status/2032536691534074208?s=20) that the ruling “confirms just how weak and frivolous the criminal investigation of [Chairman Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) is and it is nothing more than a failed attack on [Fed](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) independence.” 

“We all know how this is going to end and the [D.C. U.S. Attorney’s Office](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/) should save itself further embarrassment and move on,” [Tillis](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/) added. 

Earlier this week, after meeting with [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s pick for chair, Kevin Warsh, [Tillis](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/) told reporters that federal prosecutors should admit to making a mistake by pursuing an investigation against [Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm). 

“There’s one way out of a box canyon,” said Tillis. “It’s admitting you made a mistake.”
