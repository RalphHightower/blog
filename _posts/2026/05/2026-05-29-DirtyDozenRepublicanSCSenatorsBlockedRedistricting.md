It's more 55% [Trumpian](https://www.gop.com/) and 45% [Democrat](https://www.democrats.org/); that is is average margin of victory for statewide officers and for the [US Senate](https://www.senate.gov/).. ## [Why a dozen South Carolina senators bucked Trump on redistricting](https://www.ms.now/news/why-a-dozen-south-carolina-senators-bucked-trump-on-redistricting)

_Objections to the process and outside pressure helped scuttle the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/)’s plan to steal [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [James Clyburn](https://clyburn.house.gov/)’s seat, for now._

May. 27, 2026, 2:59 PM EDT
By [Alex Tabet](https://www.ms.now/author/alex-tabet)
 and [Will McDuffie](https://www.ms.now/author/will-mcduffie)

The [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) [senators](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) who broke ranks this week to block a new [new congressional map](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/sess126_2025-2026/maps/1265683/5683_Large.pdf) that [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) wants insist they’re loyal [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/), but they cite concerns about the rushed redistricting process and the primary ballots that voters had already cast.

A more quietly looming factor: fears about what could happen to their own seats down the road if they broke up the heavily blue [U.S. House district](https://clyburn.house.gov/) occupied for decades by [Rep. James Clyburn](https://www.ms.now/morning-joe/watch/rev-al-reveals-why-trump-s-redistricting-map-push-failed-in-south-carolina-2501506115899).

The [state](https://www.sc.gov/) [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) adjourned Tuesday [without passing](https://www.ms.now/news/south-carolina-senate-rejects-trump-push-congressional-map-clyburn) the redistricting bill, effectively guaranteeing there won’t be a new congressional map for the 2026 midterms. The new map was an explicit effort to oust [Clyburn](https://clyburn.house.gov/), the [Democratic stalwart](https://www.ms.now/news/al-sharpton-south-carolina-rejects-trump-redistricting-push-james-clyburn) and lone African American among the seven members of the [U.S. House](https://www.house.gov/) representing a state that is about one-fourth Black.

Whether it was idealism, pragmatism, wariness of [Clyburn](https://clyburn.house.gov/)’s clout or even some chafing at taking orders from [Washington](https://dc.gov/), the decision by those dozen senators has set [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) apart in a primary season dominated by redistricting.

[South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/)’s proposed map was part of the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/)’s broader and mostly successful effort to [gerrymander more House seats](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/wednesdays-campaign-round-up-5-27-26-redistricting-takes-2-steps-forward-2-steps-back) before the general election in November. In [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) statehouse after [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) statehouse, those efforts have run into little resistance, with the exception of [Indiana](https://www.in.gov/core/index.html), where [GOP](https://www.sc.gop/) [state representatives](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/house.php) who bucked [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s redistricting directive paid the price in their primaries.

In [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/), the state [House](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/house.php) fell in line. It was the state [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php), where lawmakers aren’t up for re-election this year, where the map ran into problems. 

Earlier this month, five [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) in the state [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php), including the majority leader, [rejected an attempt](https://www.ms.now/the-weekend/watch/greatest-threat-to-democracy-trump-forces-hand-as-south-carolina-gop-targets-clyburn-s-district-2501166659843) by their colleagues to discuss a new map that had been presented to legislators by Adam Kincaid, the executive director of the [National Republican Redistricting Trust](https://republicanredistricting.org/). 

[Republican](https://www.gop.com/) [Gov.](https://governor.sc.gov/) [Henry McMaster](https://governor.sc.gov/governors-biography/) called a special session anyway, and early last week the map began making its way through the legislature.

In the meantime, early voting began, highlighted by a huge turnout Tuesday, the first day of in-person voting. More than 56,000 people voted that day, according to [data](https://scvotes.gov/elections-statistics/voter-participation/) from the [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) [Elections Commission](https://scvotes.gov/). [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) saw hundreds of people funneling into early voting locations through [Columbia](https://columbiasc.gov/), braving thunderstorms to cast ballots in what African American lawmakers dubbed “Black Tuesday.”

“It didn’t go without notice that there was a record turnout,” said state [Sen.](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) [Tom Davis](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=0456818127), who opposed the redistricting effort from the start and is one of a handful of [Republican](https://www.sc.gop/) state legislators, officials and consultants who spoke to [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/).

“I think people felt differently once early voting began,” [Davis](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=0456818127) added of his [Republican](https://www.sc.gop/) colleagues.

For weeks, [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) have been uneasy about redrawing congressional lines so close to the June 9 primary.

Multiple [Republican](https://www.sc.gop/) [state senators](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) pointed to a May 12 appearance by Kincaid in front of a state [House](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/house.php) subcommittee, in which Kincaid spoke for less than eight minutes and left without taking questions, citing a timing conflict. 

“He never made himself available to the [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php). I have no idea how to reach him,” [Davis](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=0456818127) said.

Kincaid told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) he had made it clear that he was willing to speak to the [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) [Judiciary Committee](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/CommitteeInfo/senatejudiciary.php), which heard hours of public testimony about the map last week. “[Committee](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/CommitteeInfo/senatejudiciary.php) staff informed me I was not needed,” he said on Wednesday.

Even some [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) who voted in favor of the map resented the influence federal officials and Washington insiders had on the process.

[Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) [Majority Leader Shane Massey](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=1185227131) has said [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) called him about supporting the bill, while [Davis](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=0456818127) told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) he was contacted early on by Matt Brasseaux, a [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) aide.

“I think it’s absolutely ridiculous that the White House may be telling [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) what we’re going to do with our congressional seats. That bothered me greatly,” said [Republican](https://www.sc.gop/) state [Sen.](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) [JD Chaplin](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=0346590868), who nevertheless voted repeatedly to advance the bill because he thought the new lines would help his district, northeast of [Columbia](https://www.columbiasc.gov/).

[Chaplin](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=0346590868) said he blamed [McMaster](https://governor.sc.gov/governors-biography/) for not acting earlier this year to try to redraw district lines.  

State [Rep.](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/house.php) [Luke Rankin](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=1511363455), who co-sponsored the new congressional map in the [House](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/house.php), was livid with his colleagues in the Senate.

“What we saw in the state [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) today was weak-kneed [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) that caved under the pressure from [Democrats](https://www.scdp.org/),” [Rankin](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=1511363455) told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) late Tuesday.

[Rankin](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=1511363455) hopes the [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) who killed the map for the 2026 cycle will face retribution at the polls, similar to what [Indiana](https://www.in.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) who defied [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) faced in primaries earlier this month.

[State senators](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) will be up for re-election in 2028.

“I think the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) are disgruntled over what the [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) just did,” [Rankin](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=1511363455) said. “You’ll see a lot of backlash, and I think it will cost some of these [senators](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) their seats.”

The flip side of breaking up the concentration of [Democratic](https://www.scdp.org/) voters in [Clyburn](https://clyburn.house.gov/)’s district, of course, is that they wind up in congressional districts that are currently heavily red, making it slightly trickier for [Republican](https://www.sc.gop/) members of [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) seeking re-election. That could play a part in when and whether [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) revisit the map.

[Clyburn](https://clyburn.house.gov/) himself noted that his district, which stretches from [Charleston](https://charleston-sc.gov/) and other stretches of the coast inland to the [Columbia](https://columbiasc.gov/) area, is about 45% Black, yet he routinely gets re-elected with north of 60% of the vote.

“It must mean that I’m getting some votes from people who are not African American, and that’s a fact. I’m supported by Blacks and whites, [Democrats](https://www.scdp.org/) and [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) and independents,” he told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) before the special session ended.

“And I don’t know how you decide that a state with 27% African Americans deserves zero African Americans in their representation.”

While state [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) retreated to their home districts, some [Democrats](https://www.scdp.org/) hung around the statehouse to celebrate their victory Tuesday afternoon.

“We had a bipartisan group of senators who understood that, even if redistricting needs to be looked at, the timing was inappropriate,” state [Sen.](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) [Tameika Isaac Devine](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=0477840852) said.
