## [James Comey vows to keep criticizing Donald Trump despite latest criminal case](https://www.ms.now/news/james-comey-nicole-wallace-interview-trump)

_The former [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) [director](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) and target of the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) spoke with [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/)’s [Nicolle Wallace](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house) Monday in his first interview since his latest indictment._

May. 11, 2026, 9:33 PM EDT
By [Erum Salam](https://www.ms.now/author/erum-salam)

Former [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) Director [James Comey](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/james-comey-indicted-again-trump-doj) vowed not to stop criticizing [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.ms.now/white-house), despite the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s criminal case against him, during his first public interview since the government’s latest indictment against him.

“[Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has a bottomless desire to gain revenge against those who’ve criticized him,” [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) told [MS NOW’s Nicolle Wallace](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house) on Monday. “And I’m not going to stop criticizing him, because I think that’s required if you care about America.”

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) [indicted Comey for a second time](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/james-comey-indicted-again-trump-doj) last month, this time over an [Instagram]() post [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) published in May 2025 featuring a picture of seashells spelling out “86 47.” The number 86 is [slang commonly used in restaurants](https://www.merriam-webster.com/wordplay/eighty-six-meaning-origin) meaning to “throw out,” cancel or refuse service to a customer, while 47 is often used to refer to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) as the 47th U.S. president.

The [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) accused [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) of making a threatening statement against [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) with that photo. [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) has maintained that he did not know the phrase could be interpreted as violent.

The [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) first indicted [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey), a vocal [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) critic, in October on charges of making a false statement to [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) and obstructing a congressional investigation. [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) pleaded not guilty and the case was later dismissed.

[Acting Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/dag/) has [insisted](https://www.ms.now/news/todd-blanche-james-comey-indictment-8647-seashells-photo) that the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s [new indictment against Comey](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/james-comey-indicted-again-trump-doj) is “not just about a single Instagram post” featuring seashells, but he has declined to disclose what other evidence the government may have against him.

[Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) said Monday that his family’s association with him has had consequences, referring to family members who have lost government jobs amid his legal battles.

“It’s bad when someone you love is indicted,” [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) told MS NOW, adding, “Because I’ve been a critic of [DT](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), I’m a target.”

[Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) said his daughter [Maurene Comey](https://www.pbwt.com/maurene-comey), a former federal prosecutor, was fired “only because she has my last name.”

In July 2025, the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) fired [Maurene Comey](https://www.pbwt.com/maurene-comey) from her role as senior trial counsel for the [U.S. Attorney’s Office for the Southern District of New York](https://www.justice.gov/usao-sdny). She [challenged her termination](https://www.ms.now/news/maurene-comey-doj-lawsuit-trump-judge) in federal court, arguing that she was removed because of her father.

“That’s stupid. That’s immoral. That’s illegal in my view,” [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) said of his daughter’s firing.

While he said he could not explicitly defend himself because of court rules surrounding the ongoing case, [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/): “I am not only not guilty, I am innocent.” 

“There’s a cost to speaking up in this strange, awful era we’re in now,” [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) said.

Despite his challenges, the former [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) director said he remains optimistic. He reaffirmed his “faith in the independent federal judiciary,” saying that “they believe in something, and it’s very different from the way [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) approaches justice.”

Looking ahead, [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) said another leg will be added to the “wobbly stool” of democracy in the U.S. during the midterm elections, [when Democrats hope to make significant gains](https://www.ms.now/news/democrats-midterms-election-redistricting-virginia-map).

[Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) also called on other high-ranking officials who have been targeted by [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), such as former [Attorney General Bill Barr](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/problem-bill-barrs-denunciation-former-boss-rcna17921), to speak out against the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s weaponization of the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/).

“There’s a real threat that we become numb to this,” [Comey](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/james-b-comey) said, reminding Americans to “pinch yourself constantly” because “this is not who we are.”

On Friday, a federal judge [set Comey’s trial date](https://thehill.com/regulation/court-battles/5870078-federal-judge-sets-comey-trial/) on the latest charges for July 15.
