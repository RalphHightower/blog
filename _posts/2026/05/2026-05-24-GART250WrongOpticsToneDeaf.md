The money behind [GART250](https://greatamericanroadtrip.org/) would be far more effectively spent on promotional videos showcasing America — its landscapes, its National Parks, its cities, its small towns — instead of a seven‑month, security‑escorted road performance.

## [The problem(s) with the transportation secretary’s tone-deaf reality series](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/sean-duffy-reality-show-trump-transportation-secretary)

_[Sean Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy)’s return to reality programming isn’t just a political mess, it also raises unavoidable ethical questions._

May. 11, 2026, 7:58 AM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

Just two weeks into [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s second term, [Axios](https://www.axios.com/) published [a report](https://www.axios.com/2025/02/04/trump-immigration-strategy-homeland-security) noting that the [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) [administration/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s immigration crackdown included an emphasis on “choreography” and “wardrobe changes.” A [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) official conceded at the time that the focus on “the visuals” was deliberate.

[The New York Times added](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/02/13/arts/television/donald-trump-tv-ice.html) that enforcement efforts surrounding the [administration/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s immigration policies were packaged “like mini reality-TV shows — complete with perp walks and even guest stars.” In the months that followed, then-[Homeland Security](https://www.dhs.gov/) [Secretary Kristi Noem](https://www.dhs.gov/kristi-noem) appeared so preoccupied with camera-ready optics that some started [referring to](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/04/05/arts/television/trump-administration-deportation-videos.html) the [administration/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s “Department of Homeland Publicity.”

After a while, it was hard not to get the impression that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) officials were less an executive branch and more like a theater troupe that was principally concerned with putting on a good show.

Despite all of the previous efforts related to performative politics, however, [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Cabinet secretaries](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/the-cabinet/) never actually starred in their own reality program during their official tenures — or so we thought. [NBC News reported](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/sean-duffy-dot-fire-back-critics-new-reality-show-rcna344428):

> _[Transportation](https://www.transportation.gov/) [Secretary Sean Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy) is making a controversial road trip back to reality TV._

[“The Great American Road Trip,”](https://greatamericanroadtrip.org/) a five-part reality series set to air on YouTube in celebration of the United States’ 250th anniversary, follows [Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy) as he travels across the country with his wife, [Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/) host [Rachel Campos-Duffy](https://www.foxnews.com/person/c/rachel-campos-duffy), and their nine children.

In a [trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QPNmTYUi9DY) for his new show, which was filmed over the course of seven months, the [Wisconsin](https://www.wisconsin.gov/Pages/Home.aspx) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) tells viewers, “It’s more than a road trip. It’s a civic experience.” [Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy) also promoted the show last week on [Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/), where he worked before joining the [administration/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/).

If seeing the words “[Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy)” and “reality show” in the same sentence seems at all familiar, it’s not your imagination. In fact, he was part of two MTV reality shows in the late 1990s, before he began his political career.

Evidently, the secretary found a way to combine both interests in 2025.

Not surprisingly, the unveiling of [Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy)’s multipart reality series did not go unnoticed, especially given the fact that he’s promoting a lengthy road trip at a time when millions of Americans are struggling to deal with the burdens of high gas prices, generated by the [administration/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s unnecessary war in Iran.

One of his predecessors, former [Transportation](https://www.transportation.gov/) Secretary Pete Buttigieg, for example, wrote online, “I love a good road trip, but this is brutally out of touch: a [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) Cabinet member making a documentary about himself while regular families can’t afford road trips anymore, because [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and his war put gas prices through the roof.”

What’s more, there are related questions about how [Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy) managed to squeeze this into his schedule. Indeed, let’s not forget that he not only spent last year ostensibly leading the [Department of Transportation](https://www.transportation.gov/) (during multiple air travel accidents), he also simultaneously spent six months [as the head of NASA](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trump-taps-sean-duffy-nasa-space-agencys-future-increasingly-doubt-rcna217951).

But in case that weren’t quite enough, [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/)’ report went on to note that “several of the show’s sponsors — including [Boeing](https://www.boeing.com/), [Toyota](https://www.toyota.com/), [Shell](https://www.shell.us/), [Royal Caribbean Group](https://www.royalcaribbeangroup.com/) and [United Airlines](https://www.united.com/en/us) — are companies that [Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy)’s department oversees and regulates.”

Put another way, the secretary’s reality series isn’t just a political mess, it’s also raising unavoidable ethical questions.

For its part, the [Department of Transportation](https://www.transportation.gov/) said in a statement that the [Great American Road Trip Inc.](https://greatamericanroadtrip.org/) is an independent organization, and “how and who they accept donations from in furtherance of their mission to celebrate America’s 250th birthday is their decision.”

That’s good, insofar as taxpayers apparently weren’t on the hook for the five-part series, but it’s still not much of a defense, since it suggests [Duffy](https://www.transportation.gov/meet-secretary/us-transportation-secretary-sean-duffy), in addition to running [NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/) and a federal Cabinet agency, also freelanced with an independent entity to co-star in a reality show.
