## [CIA director meets with top Cuban officials amid threats of U.S. intervention](https://www.ms.now/news/cia-director-meets-with-top-cuban-officials-amid-threats-of-u-s-intervention)

_The meeting comes amid heightened tensions between the two countries and U.S. sanctions that have further strained the struggling [Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) economy._

May. 14, 2026, 8:44 PM EDT
By [Erum Salam](https://www.ms.now/author/erum-salam) and [David Rohde](https://www.ms.now/author/david-rohde)

[CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) [Director John Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/) traveled to [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) on Thursday to meet with top [Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) officials, a [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) official told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/).

[Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/), the highest-ranking U.S. official to travel to [Havana](https://www.lahabana.gob.cu/) since [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) returned to office, met with Raulito Rodriguez Castro, the grandson of former leader Raúl Castro; Minister of Interior Lazaro Alvarez Casas; and the head of [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/)’s intelligence services.

The meeting comes amid heightened tensions between the two countries, including [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s threats of military action and sweeping U.S. sanctions that have placed additional strain on [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/)’s struggling economy.

According to the [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) official, [Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/) was sent to personally deliver [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s message that the U.S. is prepared to seriously engage on economic and security issues, but only if [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) makes fundamental changes.

It is unclear what specific changes the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) is seeking.

During the meeting, [Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/) and [Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) officials discussed intelligence cooperation, economic stability and security issues, according to the official. The discussions took place against the backdrop of the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s position that [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) can no longer be a safe haven for U.S. adversaries in the Western Hemisphere.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) officials, including [Secretary of](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) [State](https://www.state.gov/) [Marco Rubio](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) and [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) himself, have repeatedly criticized the communist government and threatened foreign intervention.

The [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) official said [Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/) emphasized that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) prefers dialogue, while also warning the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) would enforce redlines. According to the official, [Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/) told [Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) leaders that they must decide whether to seize this moment with the U.S. or continue down an unsustainable path that only leads to deeper isolation and instability.

In addition to the [U.S. energy blockade](https://www.ms.now/news/cuba-preparing-u-s-aggression-conflict-war) driven by [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s threats to impose tariffs on countries that provide oil to [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/), the U.S. [expanded sanctions](https://www.whitehouse.gov/president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)ial-actions/2026/05/imposing-sanctions-on-those-responsible-for-repression-in-cuba-and-for-threats-to-united-states-national-security-and-foreign-policy/) on [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) on May 1 to include [Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) companies, institutions and officials, threatening to freeze their U.S.-based assets. The [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s moves against [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) further hamper the country already burdened by [broader, long-standing restrictions](https://2017-2021.state.gov/cuba-sanctions/) imposed since the 1960s following the revolution.

In recent months, [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) has suffered nationwide blackouts, plunging nearly 10 million people into darkness and disrupting access to food and water. The New York Times reported in March that the blackouts severely affected hospitals, [causing deaths that would otherwise be preventable](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/26/world/americas/cubas-health-system-us-oil-blockade.html). [Cuba](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) has run out of oil and diesel as of Wednesday, [Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) Energy Minister Vicente de la O Levy said.

The U.S. has [offered Cuba \$100 million](https://www.state.gov/releases/office-of-the-spokesperson/2026/05/the-united-states-is-ready-to-provide-100-million-in-direct-assistance-to-the-cuban-people-if-the-cuban-regime-will-permit-it/) in “direct humanitarian assistance” to be distributed in coordination with the [Catholic Church](https://www.vatican.va/) and other groups, the [State Department](https://www.state.gov/) announced Wednesday.
[Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) Foreign Minister Bruno Rodriguez, who [denounced](https://x.com/BrunoRguezP/status/2050290353689833592) the most recent sanctions as “collective punishment,” cautiously welcomed the offer.

“We expect it would be free from political maneuvers or any attempt to take advantage of the shortages and hardships of a people under harassment,” [Rodriguez wrote on X](https://x.com/BrunoRguezP/status/2054970862642634769).

“The best assistance that the US government could offer to the noble [Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) people in this or any other moment would be to de-escalate the measures of the energy, economic, commercial and financial blockade, which has been tightened like never before during the last few months and severely affects all sectors of the [Cuban](http://www.parlamentocubano.cu/) economy and society.”
