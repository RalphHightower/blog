## [‘We are already cooked’: Republicans brace for a midterm reckoning](https://www.ms.now/news/republicans-midterms-trump-iran-economy-2026)

Polls, the war in [Iran](https://iran.gov/) and a distracted president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) have [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) strategists and lawmakers bracing for losses, even as the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) insists there is still time to recover.

May. 2, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Jacqueline Alemany](https://www.ms.now/author/jacqueline-alemany), [Mychael Schnell](https://www.ms.now/author/mychael-schnell) and [Jake Traylor](https://www.ms.now/author/jake-traylor)
 
Gas prices are at [a four-year high](https://abcnews.com/Business/us-gasoline-prices-hit-highest-level-4-years/story?id=132459162). Annual inflation has jumped to [a nearly three-year peak](https://www.reuters.com/business/annual-us-inflation-posts-biggest-gain-nearly-three-years-march-2026-04-30/). Americans are souring on congressional [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/), [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s handling of inflation and the war in [Iran](https://iran.gov/). And his [approval rating](https://apnorc.org/projects/fewer-approve-of-trumps-handling-of-the-economy/) is at the lowest point of his second term in several polls.

The message from the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/): Things are still better than they were under [Joe Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/). 

It is not, on its face, the stuff of a winning campaign message — a backward-looking defense at a moment when voters are asking forward-looking questions about their grocery bills, gas tanks and a war with no clear end.

And privately, even some of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s aides acknowledge it.

“The vibe right now is we know we are already cooked in the midterms,” a [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) official told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), who was granted anonymity to speak candidly.

The numbers help explain the gloom. For the first time since 2010, voters say they trust [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) more than [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) to handle the economy, 52% to 48%, according to [a recent Faux News poll](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/voters-say-republicans-outdo-democrats-key-issues-fox-news-poll). Economists have largely scaled back their forecasts for the remainder of the year. Energy analysts are warning that oil prices could surge even higher. And [Moody’s](https://www.moodys.com/) recession model now puts the odds of a U.S. recession in the next 12 months at nearly a coin flip.

Six months out from the November election, [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) are favored to take the [House](https://www.house.gov/) and are [increasingly rosy about their prospects for the Senate](https://www.ms.now/news/a-political-vibe-shift-the-senate-is-suddenly-in-play) for the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) despite a difficult map. 

“As of this moment, of course you have to be very concerned,” [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Jeff Van Drew (T-NJ2)](https://vandrew.house.gov/), told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/). “If you aren’t concerned, you’d be kind of foolish. … We are either going to win the majority by a little, lose the majority by a little, or lose it by a lot.”

Even so, more than a dozen [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) strategists, lawmakers and [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) officials who spoke with [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), said they remain cautiously optimistic that [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)s have enough time to at least stave off a blue wave. 

But that optimism is contingent on several unpredictable factors — chiefly, whether the war in [Iran](https://iran.gov/) comes to a speedy conclusion, and how long its economic aftershocks linger. 

[White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) officials and their allies cautioned against writing the party’s obituary just yet. If 2024 proved anything, they argued, it is that the political environment can change dramatically in a matter of weeks, that news cycles move quickly and that voters have short memories. Internal polling circulating in the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is not as dire as the public polling, according to one [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) official. “Certainly there’s still a lot of work to be done, and that’s not a secret to anyone,” the official said. “But there’s still a lot of time left.”

Retiring [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Thom Tillis (T-NC)](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/), one of the few sitting [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) lawmakers openly critical of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), expects [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) to hold the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/), and told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) that [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) can hang on to their razor-thin majority.

“If we’re disciplined, we keep control of the [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/),” [Tillis](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/) said. “If we’ve lost, on Wednesday morning, don’t blame the [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/). [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/), go to the nearest mirror and look in the mirror. That’s why we’ll lose. If we play team ball, if we set aside our petty differences and recognize [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)s getting elected are the most important thing — no purity test, get them elected — then they’re not playing team ball, and they’re part of the problem.”

The plan, according to two [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) officials, is a familiar one: pivot to kitchen-table issues and flood battleground states with [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and [Cabinet surrogates](https:///www.whitehouse.gov/administration/the-cabinet/) in the coming months. [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/), these officials insisted, have a record to run on, including a cut in taxes via the [One Big Beautiful Bill](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/1/text) efforts to reduce drug prices and beefed-up border security.

But conspicuously absent from the game plan outlined by [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) officials: any expectation of message discipline from [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) himself.

What matters, the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) official told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), is “doing what we need to do out on the campaign trail — the events, the fundraising, the retail politics of actually showing up in districts.”

“[President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is the unequivocal leader, best messenger, and unmatched motivator for the [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) party and he is committed to maintaining [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)s’ majority in [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) to continue delivering wins for the American people,” [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) spokesperson Olivia Wales said in a statement to [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/). [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), she added, would continue to draw “a sharp contrast” between his agenda and that of congressional [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/), whom she said allowed “millions of illegal aliens to flow through the border, unanimously opposed the [Working Families Tax Cuts](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/1/text), and are soft-on-crime.”

But there is bubbling frustration among [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) strategists working [House](https://www.house.gov/) and [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) races that the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and his team have been slow to focus on any of it. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has been consumed by the war in [Iran](https://iran.gov/) and by the construction of a $400 million [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) ballroom that has become an unlikely political liability — a gilded symbol, his critics argue, of a [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) more focused on monuments to himself than on voters squeezed by more everyday concerns.

Some of the major fights [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has picked of late have only made life harder for [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) incumbents. One [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) up for re-election in a swing district pointed to the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s inflammatory Easter morning social media posts, his attacks on the pope and his habit of naming things after himself — episodes which, the lawmaker said, only serve to “fire up the people that want to put a check on his power, instead of taking his energy and focusing on stuff that makes their lives better at home.” 

“I still think there’s a lot of members that don’t understand what we’re up against — and that includes leadership,” the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) said, granted anonymity in order to speak candidly. “It’s hard to tell if they truly believe the rhetoric that we’re gonna hold the [House](https://www.house.gov/), or if they’re just saying that to make us feel like we can take some risks and take some really [bad] votes, and they’re just trying to get us to walk the plank for another piece of legislation that they feel they need.”

Several [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) strategists who spoke with [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) pointed to missed opportunities to tout the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s record, and said that the window to alter the trajectory of the election is narrowing.

“If we can somehow — on a grand scale — tout our wins, get our message out and find some clearly stated wins with [Iran](https://iran.gov/) and foreign policy, we’ll be on better footing,” said T.W. Arrighi, a [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) strategist and former spokesperson for the [National Republican Senatorial Committee](https://www.nrsc.org/).” But I want to spend the summer doing that, with prices ticking down. I don’t want to be spending just two months of the fall doing it.”
