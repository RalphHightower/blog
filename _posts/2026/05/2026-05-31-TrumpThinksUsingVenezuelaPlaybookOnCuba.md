## [Trump abandons subtlety, eyes Venezuela playbook for Cuba](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trump-cuba-castro-venezuela-playbook)

_The American [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s ambitions toward [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) are increasingly obvious. They’re also eerily familiar._

May. 21, 2026, 9:03 AM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

Despite assuring voters that his foreign policy vision was rooted in restraint, [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has spent his second term launching military strikes in [Venezuela](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/its-not-just-venezuela-trump-already-eyeing-a-series-of-potential-foreign-targets), [Yemen](https://apnews.com/article/trump-houthis-end-of-bombing-yemen-israel-e2f08ac04024ef3d6409b4ce97092489), [Syria](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/syria/us-forces-launch-strike-isis-syria-retaliation-attack-killed-3-america-rcna250163), [Nigeria](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/after-striking-nigeria-trump-pushes-dubious-claim-about-targeting-venezuelan-facility), [Somalia](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/u-s-military-strikes-islamic-state-operatives-in-somalia-for-the-first-time-in-trumps-new-term), and [Iran](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trumps-military-offensive-in-iran-leads-to-mysteries-that-shouldnt-exist), as well as [several dozen](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/10/29/us/us-caribbean-pacific-boat-strikes.html) attacks [against civilian boats](https://apnews.com/article/military-strikes-cartels-drug-boats-caribbean-cadab97919b92939ad069a05b567c00c) in international waters.

The question now is whether [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) will soon join the list.

About a week after starting the ongoing war with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), the [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) suggested he was prepared to use military force to take control of the island nation, 90 miles from the U.S. coast. “It may not be a friendly takeover,” [he said](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2026/03/09/us/trump-news/8ed6c3b0-50d5-5492-858a-0fb08e143d02?smid=url-share).

A week later, [Trump added](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/03/16/cuba-blackout-energy-grid-collapse/), “Taking [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) in some form, yeah. I mean, whether I free it, take it, think I can do anything I want with it.”

This month, he’s been even less subtle. On May 1, the American [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [explicitly said](https://rollcall.com/factbase/trump/transcript/donald-trump-speech-the-forum-club-west-palm-beach-florida-may-1-2026/) [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) is a country “which we will be taking over almost immediately,” adding that he [intends to deploy](https://bsky.app/profile/thebulwark.com/post/3mktg3ktkvi25) an aircraft carrier in [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/)’s direction.

All of which set the stage for this week, when [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) spoke at the [Coast Guard Academy](https://uscga.edu/)’s commencement ceremony, declaring, “From the Gulf of America to the frozen waters of the Arctic, from the shores of [Havana](https://www.lahabana.gob.cu/) to the banks of the [Panama Canal](https://pancanal.com/), we will drive out the forces of lawlessness and crime and foreign encroachment just like we’ve been doing.”

Around the same time, his [acting attorney general, Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/dag/), appeared in [Miami](https://www.miami.gov/Home), where he [announced an indictment](https://www.ms.now/news/u-s-indicts-raul-castro-cuba-brother-of-fidel) against 94-year-old former [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) [President](https://www.presidencia.gob.cu/) Raúl Castro on murder charges. [Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/), [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s former lawyer, didn’t elaborate on how, exactly, the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) intends to get Castro to the United States to face the criminal charges, though [he did say](https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mmcisdav5o2c) the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) expects “he will show up here by his own will — or by another way.”

Soon after, [The New York Times reported](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/20/us/politics/aircraft-carrier-caribbean-cuba-trump.html), “The aircraft carrier [Nimitz](https://www.airpac.navy.mil/Organization/USS-Nimitz-CVN-68/) and its escort warships entered the southern Caribbean Sea on Wednesday and will remain in the region for at least a few days as part of the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s campaign to pressure the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government, according to the military’s [Southern Command](http://www.southcom.mil/) and a U.S. official.”

All of this coincides with [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) [Director John Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/)’s [meeting in Havana](https://apnews.com/article/cuba-us-meeting-cia-john-9a3e7946460f8e5e48424f3a59df3fe8) less than a week ago, and [Secretary of](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) [State](https://www.state.gov/) [Marco Rubio](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) filming a video that addressed the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/)n public directly in Spanish, urging them to stand with the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/).

Nothing about this is subtle. Indeed, it’s eerily familiar. As [The Wall Street Journal summarized](https://www.wsj.com/world/americas/with-castro-indictment-trump-bets-his-venezuela-playbook-will-work-in-cuba-79f46a7b?mod=hp_lead_pos8), “With the murder indictment of [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) leader Raúl Castro, [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is applying the playbook he used to upend Venezuela’s leadership to force [Havana](https://www.lahabana.gob.cu/)’s [Communist government](http://www.pcc.cu/) into submission.”

For its part, [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) officials are understandably terrified and telling anyone who will listen that they’re eager to negotiate with the U.S. To date, those attempts at diplomacy have gone nowhere.

There’s no evidence that the American mainstream has any appetite for yet another foreign military intervention — recent polling shows an electorate far more interested in gas prices and the affordability crisis — but by all appearances, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) simply no longer cares. Watch this space.
