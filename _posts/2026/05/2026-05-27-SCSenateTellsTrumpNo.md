## [South Carolina Senate rejects Trump push to redraw Clyburn district on congressional map](https://www.ms.now/news/south-carolina-senate-rejects-trump-push-congressional-map-clyburn)

_The vote was an unexpected rebuff of the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), who pushed for redistricting in the hopes of retaining his party’s slim majority in the [U.S. House](https://www.house.gov/)._

May. 26, 2026, 2:38 PM EDT
By [Sydney Carruth](https://www.ms.now/author/sydney-carruth) and [Clarissa-Jan Lim](https://www.ms.now/author/clarissa-jan-lim)

The [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) rejected a new congressional map Tuesday that [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) hoped would eliminate the state’s only [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) seat, a Black-majority district represented for more than three decades by [Rep. James Clyburn](https://www.ms.now/morning-joe/watch/rep-clyburn-democrats-are-excited-about-the-upcoming-elections-and-they-are-engaged-2495876675622).

The vote against the new map — which would have helped [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) in their quest for a clean sweep of the state’s seven congressional districts this fall — was an unexpected rebuff of [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), who had pushed for the redistricting effort in hopes of retaining his party’s slim majority in the [U.S. House of Representatives](https://www.house.gov/) past November.

The new map [passed the state House last week](https://www.ms.now/news/south-carolina-house-passes-map-to-eliminate-clyburns-district) last week. But a motion to end debate on the map failed in the state [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) on Tuesday after 12 [Republicans](https://www.sc.gop/) joined their [Democratic](https://www.scdp.org/) colleagues in voting against it.

“Neither my conscience nor the common sense will allow me to stop an election that is already underway,” state [Sen.](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) [Richard Cash](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=0328409052) said prior to the vote. Tens of thousands of votes had already been cast Tuesday, the first day of early voting.

The [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) adjourned Tuesday with a motion to reconvene June 10, one day after the state’s scheduled primaries, making it highly unlikely that any redistricting effort will move forward in time for the 2026 midterms.

[South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) conservatives were at first hesitant to heed [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s demands for a new map. [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) [Gov.](https://governor.sc.gov/) [Henry McMaster](https://governor.sc.gov/governors-biography) initially declined to call for a special session to consider redistricting. [Shane Massey](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=1185227131), the Republican leader of the state [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php), drew national attention for bucking the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) in a 45-minute speech on the chamber’s floor about the perils of anti-democratic gerrymandering.

[McMaster](https://governor.sc.gov/governors-biography) ultimately called for a special session after the [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php), led by [Massey](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/member.php?code=1185227131), rejected a measure that would have extended its current session to consider a redraw.

In hours of floor debate ahead of the [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) vote, opponents of the map argued that it would further disenfranchise Black voters as minority districts across the country disappear amid the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/)-led redistricting push.

That concern intensified after the [Supreme Court](https://www.supremecourt.gov/) in April clawed back key [Voting Rights Act](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/STATUTE-79/pdf/STATUTE-79-Pg437.pdf) provisions. The landmark decision effectively opened the door for racial gerrymandering across America and teed up the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) to gain at least three to six seats through redistricting, most of which are held by Black [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) in Southern states.

Black lawmakers on [Capitol Hill](https://www.congress.gov/) have accused [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) of stifling the voices of Black voters and undermining critical civil rights protections. [Clyburn](https://clyburn.house.gov/) called the midcycle redistricting “a comprehensive approach to creating Jim Crow 2.0” during a news conference along with members of the [Congressional Black Caucus](https://cbc.house.gov/) in May.

“I don’t know how you decide that a state with 27% African Americans deserves zero African Americans in the representation,” [Clyburn](https://clyburn.house.gov/) told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) shortly before the state House sent the new map to the [Senate](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/senate.php) for a vote.

Alexander Tabet contributed reporting.
