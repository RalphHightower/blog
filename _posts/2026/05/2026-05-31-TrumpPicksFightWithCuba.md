Cuba is a demolition buy. It will cost more to fix Cuba than to tear it down and start over.

## [The U.S. is picking a fight with Cuba. It’s not clear how it’s supposed to end](https://www.ms.now/news/news-analysis/trump-cuba-foreign-policy-project-47)

_The [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) has not bothered to outline its strategy, plans or even demands._

May. 7, 2026, 12:41 PM EDT
By Joseph Zeballos-Roig

While the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) scrambles for an exit strategy in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), a second conflict that’s brewing just off southern [Florida](https://www.myflorida.gov/) also lacks a clear endgame.

For the last four months, the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) has been turning up the pressure on the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government with a fuel imports blockade that the [United Nations](https://www.un.org/) [warned](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/02/1166895) has left the country on the edge of collapse.

Reports from the Caribbean island [show](https://english.elpais.com/international/2026-02-21/the-sinking-of-cuba-we-are-a-sacrificial-altar.html) a deteriorating quality of life that has grown more dire with every passing week. Blackouts are frequent and can last up to 12 hours a day in the capital, [Havana](https://www.lahabana.gob.cu/).

No part of the island is immune from power outages, [leading to delays](https://unsdg.un.org/latest/stories/cuba-running-out-time-we-need-fuel-now-save-lives) in medical procedures and a virtual halt to public transportation. Even safe drinking water has become harder to access as aging pumping systems going offline from the lack of electricity. A carton of eggs [now costs](https://translatingcuba.com/with-their-pension-cuban-retirees-will-be-able-to-buy-one-carton-of-eggs-a-month/) the same as a retiree’s monthly pension.

Living in [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) means experiencing what author Leonardo Padura recently described as a “[near-catatonic state](https://time.com/article/2026/04/23/in-cuba-to-be-or-not-to-be/).”

> **The blockade would typically be at the center of a carefully orchestrated public campaign.**

In a typical [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/), the blockade would be at the center of a carefully orchestrated public campaign. Usually there’s a sequence of events: The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) would have given a big speech, maybe even in prime time or before [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/), while his top foreign policy staffers would be answering questions on major news shows. Talking points would have gone out to allies outside the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) while sympathetic think tanks spun up white papers.

Even if you disagreed, you would know the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s theory of the case and be able to quibble or muster opposition to it.

That’s simply not happening. [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) policy is being drowned out by the [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) conflict and a frenetic news cycle — the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) ballroom! [Spirit Airlines](https://www.spiritrestructuring.com/)! gerrymandering! deportations! — that’s enough to make me question whether the Old World should have just stayed put. Meanwhile, the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) has yet to release a basic strategic road map or even a clear set of demands.

[President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has brought up [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) more frequently in recent weeks, but it’s usually been while predicting an easy victory similar to the nighttime raid that [captured Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro](https://qz.com/trump-takeover-venezuela-cuba-regime-change). Last week, [he suggested](https://x.com/Acyn/status/2050372004134723818?s=20) that the [USS Abraham Lincoln](https://www.airpac.navy.mil/Organization/USS-Abraham-Lincoln-CVN-72/) could be deployed just off the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/)n coast to force a quick surrender. Before that, [he said](https://www.ms.now/news/cuba-preparing-u-s-aggression-conflict-war) he would have “the honor of taking [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/).”

[Secretary of](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) [State](https://www.state.gov/) [Marco Rubio](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/), who would normally be a top surrogate making the case on the issue, said Tuesday only that there is “an unacceptable status quo in [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/)” that will be addressed at a later date.

“Their economic model doesn’t work, and the people in charge can’t fix it,” [Rubio](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) [said](https://www.state.gov/releases/office-of-the-spokesperson/2026/05/secretary-of-state-marco-rubio-remarks-to-press-9/) at a [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) press conference, labeling the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) leadership “incompetent communists.” 

Hours earlier, he [stood for a photo](https://x.com/Southcom/status/2051723140254843043?s=20) with [Gen. Francis Donovan](https://www.southcom.mil/About/Leadership/Bio-Article-View/Article/4398122/gen-francis-l-donovan/), head of [U.S. Southern Command](http://www.southcom.mil/), posed pointedly in front of a map of [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/). For a man who built a career on [a crusade against the regime of Fidel Castro](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/12/us/politics/rubio-cuba-venezuela.html), the gesture was about as subtle as a sledgehammer. It was also provocatively vague. Was the photo a prelude to a ground invasion? A carrier-led show of force? Or a return to the [absurd cloak-and-dagger era](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-latin-america-38121583) era of the Cold War with [exploding seashells](https://content.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,2008962_2008964_2008953,00.html) and [poison pens](https://www.nbcnews.com/storyline/fidel-castros-death/fidel-castro-cia-s-7-most-bizarre-assassination-attempts-n688951)? There are no reliable answers to these questions.

Before the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s recent efforts, [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) was already destitute. Decades of mismanagement have yielded a sclerotic economy that mixes central planning [with a small dose](https://horizontecubano.law.columbia.edu/news/cubas-private-sector) of private entrepreneurship. The U.S. trade embargo — in effect since 1960— hasn’t helped, while a repressive police state overseen by geriatric men with first-hand or family ties to the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) Revolution stunted the country’s overall development. Though Miguel Díaz-Canel is ostensibly [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/)’s [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), Fidel’s brother Raúl Castro is widely viewed as the real power behind the throne.

This is not the first time [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has tried leaving a footprint in [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/). In 1998 he sent consultants [to explore](https://www.newsweek.com/2016/10/14/donald-trump-cuban-embargo-castro-violated-florida-504059.html) a hotel deal, though it violated strict sanctions barring Americans from conducting business with the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government. 

A quarter century later, the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) is [reportedly seeking](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/18/world/americas/us-officials-cuba-trump.html) economic and political reforms meant to loosen the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government’s tight grip on its citizens. In April, U.S. diplomats met with [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) officials bearing a list of demands that included fostering a private sector that attracts foreign investment, the release of political prisoners and compensation for U.S. companies and citizens that had their property seized in the early 1960s. 

The [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government has responded by re-running a playbook that’s showing abundant signs of wear and tear. It’s staging large demonstrations with anti-imperialist slogans, vowing that it will never be an American colony and it’s capable of defending itself against any U.S. military aggression.

[Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) is already weighing in. Last week, the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) rejected a war powers resolution that would have tied [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s hands from carrying out military action against [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) without an explicit green light from lawmakers. [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Tim Kaine](https://www.kaine.senate.gov/) of [Virginia](https://www.virginia.gov/), a sponsor of the resolution, argued that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) wants to topple the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government simply for the sake of toppling it and warned of cementing power in the hands of hard-liners.

“He’s done regime change in two nations, and hard-liners are still running things. So it’s not like he has produced a [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/) for [Venezuelans](https://gob.ve/) or an [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) for [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)ians,” [Kaine](https://www.kaine.senate.gov/) told me shortly after the vote. “The U.S. has a very poor track record of successfully executing regime change, particularly in the Americas.”

[Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) is one of the U.S.’ smallest foes, measuring about one-fifteenth the size of [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), with roughly the same number of residents as [North Carolina](https://www.nc.gov/). But the current regime has managed to foil the well-laid plans of 13 [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)s dating back to [Dwight Eisenhower](https://www.eisenhowerlibrary.gov/home). 

Without a plan, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) may go down in history as the 14th [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) regime outlasts.
