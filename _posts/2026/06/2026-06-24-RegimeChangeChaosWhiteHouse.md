## ['Regime Change' book offers an unsettling account of Trump's return to the White House](https://www.ms.now/opinion/regime-change-trump-maggie-haberman-jonathan-swan)

_In Maggie Haberman and Jonathan Swan’s “Regime Change,” the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) makes his staff worse — and they happily return the favor._

Jun. 23, 2026, 4:11 PM EDT
By [Paul Waldman](https://www.ms.now/author/paul-waldman)

[“Regime Change”](https://www.ms.now/morning-joe/watch/maggie-haberman-and-jonathan-swan-on-their-new-trump-book-regime-change-2504459843810) — the new book from [New York Times reporters](https://www.ms.now/the-last-word/watch/haberman-and-swan-react-to-trump-s-attacks-on-them-ahead-of-damning-book-regime-change-2504432195806) Maggie Haberman and Jonathan Swan — is the latest in a very long line of books promising a behind-the-scenes look at a presidency. [Their book](https://www.ms.now/the-last-word/watch/a-form-of-magical-thinking-haberman-and-swan-on-trump-s-iran-war-strategy-2504432195807) has only a few of the dishy details one often finds in these volumes. But as readers learn about the dynamic between [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and the loyalists who surround him, a genuinely sinister picture emerges. The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has finally created an [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) that works exactly how he wants it to, and the result is a vicious cycle of incompetence and moral corruption.

In short, everything about how the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) operates exacerbates [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s most pernicious instincts and character flaws. His aides enable him to be the worst version of himself, and in turn he makes them the worst version of themselves.

> **The result is an [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) full of people who either agree with [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s most self-destructive impulses or know that objections are all but pointless.**

That’s not to say “Regime Change” is bereft of gossipy anecdotes. Haberman and Swan write that in his bedroom, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) “would frequently leave an array of empty potato chip bags, Starbucks wrappers, and ice cream cartons in the trash, or on the floor,” and for some reason was “sometimes throwing out [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) sterling silver utensils.” We also learn that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s hearing has declined — hardly surprising for someone who just turned 80 — and that is why joint press conferences with visiting dignitaries or other leaders are now often held in the [Oval Office](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) rather than the larger [East Room](https://www.whitehouse.gov/).

But the big story is in the cocoon of sycophancy [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has built around himself, with dire consequences for the country.

That cocoon was being constructed from the moment [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) began staffing up his second [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/). We know how much [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has always valued loyalty, but as Haberman and Swan report, “there was a new acid test: January 6.” Anyone seeking a place near the center of power had to say it was the act of patriots who were subsequently abused by the [Biden administration](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/). That weeded out anyone with a real commitment to American democracy. And it forced everyone to publicly proclaim a lie. When you abandon your integrity in that way, you become much more willing to do terrible things in the future.

Even in this [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/), to be clear, now and again someone will raise a doubt or gently suggest a different course to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/). Haberman and Swan describe [Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/) [Secretary Scott Bessent](https://home.treasury.gov/about/general-information/officials/scott-bessent) urging the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) to say publicly he had no intention of firing [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) Chair [Jerome Powell](https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/powell.htm) in order to calm the markets. [Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/dag/), who was [deputy attorney general](https://www.justice.gov/) at the time, tells [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) there were no grounds to indict [New York](https://www.ny.gov/) [Attorney General](https://ag.ny.gov/) [Letitia James](https://ag.ny.gov/about/meet-letitia-james), despite [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s insistence on revenge against her. But the moments of dissent are few and far between, and [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) can only be constrained for a moment; he did get his bogus indictment of [James](https://ag.ny.gov/about/meet-letitia-james), for instance, a case that [quickly fell apart](https://apnews.com/article/letitia-james-justice-department-mortgage-fraud-fa10cc83a925ecbb628f44572ee7931b).

The result is an [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) full of people who either agree with [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s most self-destructive impulses or know that objections are all but pointless. “Few if any of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s aides disagreed with the idea of sending in the [National Guard](https://www.nationalguard.mil/)” to American cities to enforce immigration law, Haberman and Swan report — but that militarization of immigration enforcement has proved disastrous. The authors note that the tone of official [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) communications with the press — “abusive, offensive, trolling — had been set from the top and set from the start.” While it may represent the true [MAGA](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) spirit, no one in the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) seems to have considered whether it carries any actual political benefit.

> **Even the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s interactions with those from outside only reinforce his pathologies.**

Internal debate is nowhere to be found, at least not that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) will ever see.  When [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) suggests that the U.S. take possession of Gaza — a self-evidently ludicrous notion — no one disagrees. “That’s a good idea, right? We’ll just own Gaza,” [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) says, then turns to communications director Steven Cheung and says, “You like the idea, right?” Cheung responds, “It’s a strong move.” Privately, some [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) aides were more realistic. “Legitimately nutso,” a senior aide told Haberman and Swan. “But very on brand.”

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has treated the presidency as [one giant cash grab for himself and his family](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/americans-witness-the-most-corrupt-week-of-donald-trumps-political-career), which means that his aides have to become apologists for corruption. In addition, “[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) repeatedly told aides in private that he planned to preemptively pardon ‘anyone who came within 250 feet of the [Oval Office](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’ before his term ended.” That could be interpreted as fatherly protectiveness — or permission to act with as much impunity as they want.

Even the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s interactions with those from outside only reinforce his pathologies. When [Apple](https://www.apple.com/) [CEO](https://www.apple.com/leadership/tim-cook/) [Tim Cook](https://www.apple.com/leadership/tim-cook/) [presents Trump](https://www.huffpost.com/entry/tim-cook-gift-giving-meeting-with-trump_n_6894a18ae4b0b5385d5f56db) with an engraved plaque on a 24-karat gold base, it only reinforces [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s sense of power and invincibility, making foolish decisions more likely in the future.

Which brings us to [Iran](https://irangov.ir/). We do learn in the book that [Vice President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) [JD Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) expressed misgivings about the disastrous war, [echoing other reports](https://www.ms.now/news/vance-iran-war-ceasefire-talks-islamabad). “But with the exception of the [vice president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/), nobody on the senior team — not his [secretary](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) of [state](https://www.state.gov/), not his chairman of the [Joint Chiefs](https://www.jcs.mil/), not his [chief of staff](https://www.linkedin.com/in/susiewiles/) — had made a real effort” to talk [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) out of it. And [Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/)’s objections were less than forceful. The internal debate seemed to be about whether the [MAGA base](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) that had been told [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) was anti-war would feel betrayed, not about whether the war might go badly — as it has.

Almost halfway through the book, Haberman and Swan summarize how the [Oval Office](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) looks in [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s second term. They describe him “spending his days at the [Resolute Desk](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) in a series of rolling bull sessions, accompanied by a core group of intimates; these were supplemented by a rotating cast of extras, and on any given day they could be [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) lawmakers, titans of industry, former pro wrestlers, country musicians, Gulf royals, crypto bros, or friends of felons seeking pardons.”

This is a portrait of an unserious [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) surrounded by unserious people, all bringing out the worst in each other. Most frightening of all, there are still 2 1/2 years left.
