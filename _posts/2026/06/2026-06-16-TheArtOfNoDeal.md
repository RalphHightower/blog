## [With tentative Iran deal, Trump secures breakthroughs that he already had](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trump-iran-deal-strait-hormuz-already-toll-free)

_Last month the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) said he wanted [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s “unconditional surrender” and a “perfect” deal. As the war nears an apparent end, he’s achieved neither._

Jun. 15, 2026, 8:07 AM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

After far too many instances in which the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) told the world that an agreement with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) was imminent, only to see those hopes dashed soon after, there actually appears to be something resembling a deal to end the monthslong conflict. [MS NOW reported](https://www.ms.now/news/u-s-and-iran-finally-reach-deal-trump-says):

> _[Iran](https://irangov.ir/) and the United States reached a deal Sunday aimed at ending the Middle East war, according to [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) Donald [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and [Tehran](https://tehran.ir/)’s deputy foreign minister, marking a major breakthrough after months of conflict and on-again, off-again negotiations.
> 
> The statements from [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and [Tehran](https://tehran.ir/) raised hopes for an end to fighting that has left more than 7,500 dead, most of them in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), and rocked the global oil market._

In [an item](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/116750587569914985) published to his social media platform, the American [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) boasted, “The Deal with the [Islamic Republic of Iran](https://irangov.ir/) is now complete. Congratulations to all!”

Neither [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) nor anyone in his administration has been willing to share any of the details surrounding this apparent breakthrough, which makes it effectively impossible to evaluate it on the merits. That said, a few things stand out.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s many unmet goals: On Feb. 28, when [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) announced the start of the U.S. offensive [in a prerecorded video](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/even-when-launching-a-war-trump-couldnt-pry-himself-from-mar-a-lago) filmed at [his glorified country club in Florida](https://www.maralagoclub.com/), the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) didn’t explain why he’d decided to launch the war, but he did present the public with five objectives.

The [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)’s goals were relatively straightforward: (1) destroy [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s missiles and missile industry; (2) annihilate the [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)ian navy; (3) sever the ties between [Tehran](https://tehran.ir/) and its proxies in the Middle East; (4) prevent [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) from acquiring a nuclear weapon; and (5) create the conditions necessary for regime change. More than three months later, [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s navy has [certainly been decimated](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/01/us/politics/trump-goals-iran-war.html), but the other four objectives have plainly not been met.

Similarly, in May, the American [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) said he wanted [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s “[unconditional surrender](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/the-problems-with-trumps-demand-for-irans-unconditional-surrender)” and a “[perfect](https://politicalwire.com/2026/05/27/trump-holds-out-for-perfect-deal-with-iran/)” agreement, and as the war nears an apparent end, he’s achieved neither.

It’s not entirely clear if [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) understands his own deal: On Sunday afternoon, at 5:29 p.m. ET, the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [wrote online](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump116750587569914985), “I hereby fully authorize the toll free opening of the Strait of Hormuz.” Just 58 minutes later, [he added](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/116750814874397998) that the Strait of Hormuz would open “on Friday.”

Around the same time, he spoke to The New York Times at some length about the deal, though [the newspaper’s coverage noted](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/14/us/politics/trump-iran-deal-strait-of-hormuz.html) that he “seemed to be describing [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)ian concessions that the country has not yet made.”

It’s something to keep in mind in the coming days: [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), who has long struggled to familiarize himself with policy details, may say all sorts of things about the burgeoning agreement, but it would be a mistake to accept his descriptions and assessments at face value.

The deal appears to give [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) what he already had: In his Times interview, the [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) boasted that the strait would soon be open, there would be no tolls for passing ships, and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s nuclear program would be limited to enriching uranium at low levels.

What he neglected to mention is that the strait used to be open, there were no tolls for passing ships, and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s nuclear program used to be limited to enriching uranium at low levels. As the Times summarized, “the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is essentially celebrating a return to the prewar status quo,” which in turn raises questions anew about why the United States went to war in the first place.

The deal isn’t quite done: It’s impossible to say what will happen between now and Friday, when the burgeoning agreement is scheduled to be signed, but more important is the fact that the deal sets the stage for [a 60-day window](https://www.ms.now/news/u-s-and-iran-finally-reach-deal-trump-says) to negotiate additional details regarding [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s nuclear program.

The process, in other words, is ongoing.

[Tehran](https://tehran.ir/) has reason to be pleased: As [The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/)’s [Tom Nichols summarized](https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/2026/06/trump-iran-deal/687547/), “The reality is that the war will close with the regime in [Tehran](https://tehran.ir/) intact and in the grip of the [Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps](https://sepahnews.ir/); the Strait of Hormuz will remain under the threat of [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)ian attacks; [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) will continue to possess significant drone and missile stocks; the regime will maintain the capability to be a state sponsor of terror; and many sanctions will be lifted and billions of dollars in unfrozen assets will flow to [Iran](https://irangov.ir/). In other words, the [Iranians](https://irangov.ir/) have achieved their key strategic aims — regime survival above all — while the Americans have achieved none of their own.”

Given everything we know about [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), he will almost certainly characterize the latest developments as a breakthrough for the ages. While we wait for additional details to come into focus, it seems those claims will be impossible to take seriously.
