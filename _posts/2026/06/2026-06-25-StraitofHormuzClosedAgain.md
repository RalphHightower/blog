## [Iran says Strait of Hormuz is closed, citing Israeli attacks in Lebanon](https://www.ms.now/news/iran-says-strait-of-hormuz-is-closed-citing-israeli-attacks-in-lebanon)

_The U.S. military contradicted that, while conflicting statements by [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) and Hezbollah further complicated the looming talks between the U.S. and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)._

Jun. 20, 2026, 8:17 PM EDT
By [Clarissa-Jan Lim](https://www.ms.now/author/clarissa-jan-lim), [Emily Hung](https://www.ms.now/author/emily-hung), [David Rohde](https://www.ms.now/author/david-rohde) and [Inzamam Rashid](https://www.ms.now/author/inzamam-rashid)

[Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s military declared Saturday that the Strait of Hormuz is closed due to [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) attacks on Hezbollah in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/), [The Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/iran-us-israel-lebanon-hezbollah-june-20-2026-6e23fb5f37e23427dbfc2bc80c59bda8?taid=6a369baf39b3a10001a60d41&) reported citing [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) state media, on the eve of detailed talks about a long-term peace deal.

[U.S. Central Command](https://x.com/centcom/status/2068340095581552766?s=46&t=WQsDYy0pGgaELUk2cT8RgQ) countered shortly afterward that commercial ship traffic in the strait actually increased Saturday.

“[Iran](https://irangov.ir/) does not control the Strait of Hormuz. Traffic continues to flow, and U.S. forces are monitoring the situation to ensure this remains the case,” [CENTCOM](https://www.centcom.mil/) spokesperson Capt. Tim Hawkins said later, adding that the U.S. military has not tracked any movement from [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) to close the strait so far.

[Iran](https://irangov.ir/) blamed [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) for violating a freshly announced ceasefire with Hezbollah by continuing to attack targets in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/). The [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) military, in turn, accused the [Tehran](https://tehran.ir/)-backed militia of breaking the terms first.

The conflicting statements about the crucial maritime chokepoint and the state of play in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) come as U.S. and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) officials head to [Switzerland](https://x.com/ForeignOfficePk/status/2068335548339823073?s=20) to dig into specifics after landing a much-trumpeted but vague memorandum of understanding earlier this week.

Two senior [Pakistan](http://pakistan.gov.pk/) government officials confirmed to [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) that [Pakistan](http://pakistan.gov.pk/)’s [Prime Minister](http://pmo.gov.pk/) [Shahbaz Sharif](http://pmo.gov.pk/pm_profile.php) and Field Marshal Gen. Asim Munir will travel to [Switzerland](https://www.admin.ch/gov/) to join the U.S. and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) in negotiations. [Pakistan](http://pakistan.gov.pk/) has been a key mediator in discussions between the warring parties.

Access to the Strait of Hormuz, which was unfettered before the U.S. and [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) attacked [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) on Feb. 28, has been a major point of leverage for [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) in the war. [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) [reopen the strait](https://www.ms.now/news/us-iran-mou-contents-donald-trump) the strait to commercial vessels as part of the memorandum of understanding with the U.S.; American officials had feared [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) might renege on that amid ongoing talks.

[Vice President JD Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) said Saturday morning on [Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/) that there was no evidence that the strait remains closed, though he acknowledged that mines in the waterway may continue to affect commercial routes. It was unclear if [Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) was aware of the latest reporting on the strait during his morning interview.

[Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) said special envoy [Steve Witkoff](https://www.witkoff.com/) and [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s son-in-law, Jared Kushner, had been on the ground in [Switzerland](https://www.admin.ch/gov/) “for a few hours dealing with some of the technical elements” regarding talks on implementation of the memo.

The vice president departed for [Switzerland](https://www.admin.ch/gov/) on Saturday afternoon, shortly after the arrival of the [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) delegation that included [Parliament Speaker](https://www.majlis.ir/) [Mohammad-Bagher Ghalibaf](https://ghalibaf.ir/), who posted a photo himself walking beneath a Meraj Airlines jet emblazoned with the hashtag #minab168. It is a reference to the number of lives lost when an airstrike hit a school in Minab, near the strait. U.S. intelligence agencies long ago concluded American forces were to blame, according to multiple reports, but the Pentagon has yet to acknowledge responsibility.

[Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) told reporters the parties would likely have a “couple days of talks” and said U.S. officials will be focusing on making progress on discussions about [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s nuclear program and the ceasefire between [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) and Hezbollah in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/).

U.S. officials have repeatedly urged [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) to tamp down its strikes in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) so as to not derail U.S. negotiations with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/). [Israel](https://www.gov.il/)’s military campaign against Hezbollah has killed nearly 3,900 people since March 2, according to the [Lebanese Public Health Ministry](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-flash-update-36-escalation-hostilities-lebanon-18-june-2026), the vast majority in southern [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/).

“You don’t have to knock down an apartment house every time you’re looking for somebody,” [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) said Tuesday, chastising [Israel](https://www.gov.il/). “Because there are a lot of people in those houses, and they’re not all Hezbollah.”

A U.S. official told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) on Friday that [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) and Hezbollah had agreed to a ceasefire. [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) [Ambassador to the U.S.](https://embassies.gov.il/usa/) [Yichael Leiter](https://embassies.gov.il/usa/) said on X that the country halted offensive operations Friday morning local time, but [Israel](https://www.gov.il/)i forces remain in southern [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) to fight Hezbollah.

Despite the ceasefire Friday, U.S. intelligence agencies believe [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) [Prime Minister](https://www.gov.il/en/departments/prime_ministers_office) [Benjamin Netanyahu](https://www.gov.il/en/pages/netanyahu-b) is likely to continue attacking the group in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) even if it hinders long-term peace negotiations between the U.S. and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), a U.S. official with knowledge of the matter told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/). The U.S. intelligence assessment was first reported by [The Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/).

[Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/)’s civil defense agency said Saturday that [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) strikes killed at least 16 people in the south overnight. The [Israeli Defense Forces](https://www.idf.il/en) accused Hezbollah of firing dozens of projectiles toward soldiers in southern [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/).
