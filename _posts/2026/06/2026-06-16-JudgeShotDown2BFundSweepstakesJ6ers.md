## [Why a judge indefinitely blocked Trump’s ‘beautiful’ payout fund](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-weaponization-payout-fund-judge-block-republicans)

[Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)s’ bark is bigger than their bite.

Jun. 15, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Zeeshan Aleem](https://www.ms.now/author/zeeshan-aleem)

A federal judge indefinitely blocked [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s proposed nearly [$1.8 billion “anti-weaponization” fund](https://www.ms.now/opinion/how-trump-is-angling-to-give-cash-to-a-maga-paramilitary) on Friday and demanded that the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) provide a sworn statement within a week that it will not try to go ahead with creating it in the future. 

[U.S. District](https://www.uscourts.gov/) [Judge Leonie Brinkema](https://www.vaed.uscourts.gov/Alexandria)’s decision demonstrates a more punitive attitude toward the fund than [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/), who have [made a fuss about the effort](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-republicans-weaponization-fund-bill) but proven [unwilling to take binding action](https://www.ms.now/news/senate-republicans-fall-in-line-trump-pass-reconciliation).

> **[Brinkema](https://www.vaed.uscourts.gov/Alexandria) is right to be suspicious and demand a clear disavowal.**

Of course, that the fund is even a possibility is astonishing. It would use the pretext of compensating people treated unfairly by the federal government to effectively act as a slush fund for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s political allies and [potentially be used to pay out violent Jan. 6 rioters](https://www.ms.now/opinion/how-trump-is-angling-to-give-cash-to-a-maga-paramilitary) who seek compensation for being penalized by the government.

[Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) recognized it as so obviously politically toxic that in May they [revolted en masse](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-republicans-weaponization-fund-bill) through public objections and an acrimonious two-hour meeting with acting [Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/ag). [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Ted Cruz (T-TX)](https://www.cruz.senate.gov/), [described it as](https://www.cbsnews.com/amp/news/cruz-doj-anti-weaponization-fund-blanche-meeting-gop-revolt) “one of the roughest meetings I’ve seen in my entire time in the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/).” 

The [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) temporarily retreated, but declined to decisively rule out reviving the fund in the future. [Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/ag/) said, “We’re not moving forward with the fund. Period.” But he [refused to commit](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/doj-asks-court-reject-anti-weaponization-fund-lawsuits-blanche-trump/) to the promise in writing when asked to do so by [Democratic](https://.www.democrats.org/) lawmakers. And [Trump continued](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/03/us/politics/trump-fund.html) the idea of the payout fund as “beautiful” and “important.”

[Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)s had an opportunity to muzzle [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) on the fund and put the issue to rest with amendments to an immigration bill they passed earlier in June — [but in a remarkable display of subservience, chose not to do so.](https://www.ms.now/news/senate-republicans-fall-in-line-trump-pass-reconciliation) Perhaps they hoped they could take [Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/ag/) at his word that the fund was fizzling out while avoiding direct confrontation with [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/).

[Brinkema](https://www.vaed.uscourts.gov/Alexandria) is therefore stepping in where a [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)-controlled [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) has failed. The fund is still being challenged in court by a pro-democracy group and, [according to The Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2026/06/12/federal-judge-indefinitely-blocks-trumps-18-billion-payout-fund/), at a hearing in Virginia on Friday, [Brinkema](https://www.vaed.uscourts.gov/Alexandria) “forcefully rejected the government’s arguments that the case was moot, citing [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s praising of the idea and acting [attorney general Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/ag/)’s unwillingness to say under the penalty of perjury that the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) will not try to stand it up in the future.”

She said, according to the [Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/), “We don’t have the kind of absolute certainty that this fund wouldn’t rear its head” again, and that the “public interest in this case is very, very strong in my view.”

The [Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/) notes that [Brinkema](https://www.vaed.uscourts.gov/Alexandria)’s latest decision came after [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) officials had called off the fund in court filings and argued the issue was moot.

[Brinkema](https://www.vaed.uscourts.gov/Alexandria) is right to be suspicious and demand a clear disavowal. [The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/), citing eight people familiar with the fund, [reported on Thursday](https://www.theatlantic.com/politics/2026/06/trump-anti-weaponization-fund/687500/) that [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) officials and people close to the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) “indicated that the payout idea has not actually been scrapped,” and that “officials are exploring whether elements of the fund can be reactivated while also examining alternative arrangements to make sure loyalists get compensated.”

[Brinkema](https://www.vaed.uscourts.gov/Alexandria) was right to try to find a way to preempt these efforts. At the same time, it’s not clear what shape any alternative arrangements might take and whether they could circumvent a block on the fund.

There are also other ways that [Jan. 6 defendants](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL0W4zBOS45K_NAfZ-MFphiK1y73LVa05q) might be able to get compensation. As [Anthony Coley](https://anthonycoley.com/), the former director of the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/)’s [Office of Public Affairs](https://www.justice.gov/opa) in the [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/), [wrote recently](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-weaponization-fund-jan-6-todd-blanche) for [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), hundreds of [Jan. 6 defendants](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL0W4zBOS45K_NAfZ-MFphiK1y73LVa05q) have filed claims against the federal government under the [Federal Tort Claims Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/100th-congress/house-bill/4612). This policy acts as a “back door” to pay out [Jan. 6](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL0W4zBOS45K_NAfZ-MFphiK1y73LVa05q) rioters through a process hidden from public view.  “A [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) run by the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s former defense lawyers can still settle such claims one by one, on its own authority, with no public hearing and no vote,” Coley](https://anthonycoley.com/) wrote.

[Coley](https://anthonycoley.com/) also pointed out that [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) has a few options to pass legislation to bar that from happening. But given the GOP’s track record so far on opposing [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), there’s little reason for optimism.
