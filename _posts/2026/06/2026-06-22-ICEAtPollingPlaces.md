## [The problem(s) with Mullin’s answer about ICE agents at voting precincts](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/the-problems-with-mullins-answer-on-ice-agents-at-local-voting-precincts)

_The [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) keeps confronting opportunities to make clear that it won’t deploy [ICE](https://www.ice.gov/) agents to polling places. It also keeps leaving the door wide open._

Jun. 15, 2026, 3:21 PM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

For months, [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) officials have shared their widespread concern that the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) may try to deploy federal troops to local voting precincts in the fall. Those fears were heightened last week when [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) on the [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Armed Services Committee](https://www.armed-services.senate.gov/) [blocked an effort](https://www.ms.now/news/troops-polling-republicans-senate-elections) to strengthen existing limits.

But it’s not just military personnel who may be deployed.

In recent months there has been related talk in conservative circles about sending immigration agents to polling places, which seemed ridiculous — right up until a variety of [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) [on Capitol Hill](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/its-not-just-bannon-republican-lawmakers-want-ice-agents-at-local-voting-precincts) and [conservative media outlets](https://www.mediamatters.org/fox-news/jesse-watters-argues-ice-agents-polling-locations?_bhlid=d72f403e86618a3ec7e329739b9326de0c49976f) started endorsing the suggestion. The louder that conversation became on the right, the more important it became when [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) officials [refused to rule out](https://bsky.app/profile/ab21.bsky.social/post/3mg6d2uccvs2q) the possibility.

As recently as last month, a reporter asked [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) directly about possibly sending agents from [Immigration and Customs Enforcement](https://www.ice.gov/) to voting locations. “You know what?” [the president replied](https://bsky.app/profile/acyn.bsky.social/post/3mloev3aqoc24). “I’ll do anything necessary to make sure we have honest elections.”

That came on the heels of acting [Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/dag/) appearing at a conservative event and [asking the rhetorical question](https://www.ms.now/opinion/todd-blanche-overlooking-the-law-doesnt-object-to-ice-at-polling-places), “Why is there objection to sending [ICE](https://www.ice.gov/) officers to polling places? Illegals can’t vote.”

With 20 weeks to go before Election Day 2026, the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s position is still not inspiring confidence.

On [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/)’s “[State of the Union](https://www.cnn.com/videos/title-2248506),” Kasie Hunt asked [Homeland Security](https://www.dhs.gov/) [Secretary Markwayne Mullin](https://www.dhs.gov/markwayne-mullin) whether he was prepared to rule out the possible deployment of [ICE](https://www.ice.gov/) agents to local polling stations.

As [Mullin](https://www.dhs.gov/markwayne-mullin) really ought to understand, it’s not nearly so simple. Having [ICE](https://www.ice.gov/) officials unnecessarily patrolling local voting precincts can, and likely would, have the chilling effect of intimidating American citizens who can vote legally but don’t want to deal with harassment from armed federal agents.

In case that weren’t enough, as the [Brennan Center for Justice](https://www.brennancenter.org/) at [New York University](https://www.nyu.edu/) [School of Law](https://www.law.nyu.edu/) [recently explained](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/sending-ice-polling-places-illegal), there’s ample evidence to conclude that sending [ICE](https://www.ice.gov/) agents to polling places is illegal.

[Mullin](https://www.dhs.gov/markwayne-mullin) went on to tell Hunt that [ICE](https://www.ice.gov/) agents could be deployed in response to requests from state and local officials or in response to immediate threats, such as a bomb threat.

As a rule, when responding to a bomb threat, [ICE](https://www.ice.gov/) isn’t the first law enforcement agency that comes to mind — what are they going to do, detain the bomb for weeks in squalid conditions before deporting it to a country it’s never been to before? — but the [DHS](https://www.dhs.gov/) [secretary](https://www.dhs.gov/markwayne-mullin) appears to have a different perspective.

I have a hunch we haven’t heard the last of this one. Watch this space.
