[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) cheats on everything: wives, businesses, taxes, golf, politics and life. Living the easy way by cheating, he wants the easiest way to extricate himself from the [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) war.

## [Trump wants a shortcut to peace with Iran. It won’t work.](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-agreement-israel-lebanon)

_[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) keeps delivering “peace” deals that try to circumvent, or outright ignore, the groups actually doing the fighting._

Jun. 23, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Nicholas Grossman](https://www.ms.now/author/nicholas-grossman)

Only a few days after the United States and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) signed a memorandum of understanding, [Iran announced that the Strait of Hormuz was closed again](https://www.ms.now/news/iran-says-strait-of-hormuz-is-closed-citing-israeli-attacks-in-lebanon). But it wasn’t American forces that drew the sharp response from [Tehran](https://tehran.ir/). Instead, the [Iranians](https://irangov.ir/) said [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) operations against [Hezbollah](http://moqawama.org.lb/) in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) meant America violated the deal. Neither [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) nor Hezbollah are party to the MOU, but the “immediate and permanent termination” of their conflict in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) is listed as a condition [the document’s first article.](https://www.ms.now/news/us-iran-mou-contents-donald-trump)

Professional diplomats would have insisted on more specificity about what that language meant. It’s unlikely seasoned American negotiators would have agreed without getting [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) buy-in first, but the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) regime disdains that sort of expertise. This disregard for detail fits [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s consistent approach to conflict diplomacy: seek the minimum that can be called a “peace deal,” punting on core issues and excluding conflict participants as needed. Produce something that’s more vision than plan and proclaim peace. Public praise and [positive press will follow](https://apnews.com/article/lebanon-israel-hezbollah-us-talks-ceasefire-washington-e7f26e207fc7543fe1f25a5318ff9ce3). Actual follow-through will not.

A closer examination of the MOU shows how predictable it was that [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) would become a sticking point.

A closer examination of the MOU shows how predictable it was that [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) would become a sticking point. A ceasefire usually means combatants freeze in place and negotiate more permanent arrangements, but the somewhat ambiguous language in the MOU “ensuring the territorial integrity and sovereignty of [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/)” arguably demands [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) withdrawal to prewar lines.

But neither [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) nor [Hezbollah](http://moqawama.org.lb/) are a party to the MOU or participated in the talks leading up to deal nor its hasty signing, so neither feels bound by it. [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) says it retaliated to a deadly [Hezbollah](http://moqawama.org.lb/) strike in southern [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/), where the [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) military set up a “[buffer zone](https://apnews.com/projects/israel-expansion-maps/)” it intends to keep. [Hezbollah](http://moqawama.org.lb/) says [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) forces were on the move and their ongoing presence in [Lebanon](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) is unacceptable. And [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) now has a new point of leverage to pressure the U.S. to either keep its ally on a tighter leash or see the whole deal collapse.

The MOU followed a monthslong U.S. strategy of trying to [make peace in Lebanon by going around Hezbollah](https://www.jpost.com/israel-news/defense-news/article-898323) to speak directly with the [Lebanese government](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/). But the fighting is between [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) and [Hezbollah](http://moqawama.org.lb/), and the [Lebanese state](https://www.presidency.gov.lb/) cannot control the militant group. Cutting out the combatants cannot achieve the peace [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has promised is coming.

Look no further than the agreement to end the war in Gaza that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) helped broker. The initial deal didn’t address the underlying issues that prompted the war or establish a workable framework for the future of Gaza. Further efforts took place between the [U.S.](https://www.usa.gov/), [Egypt](https://www.presidency.eg/), [Turkey](https://www.turkiye.gov.tr/) and [Qatar](https://hukoomi.gov.qa/), which held [a formal signing ceremony](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/world/middle-east/gaza-peace-deal-israel-hamas-wont-be-at-signing-of-ceasefire-in-egypt-who-will-be-at-event-trump/articleshow/124501738.cms) without any representative from [Hamas](http://almoqawma.com/) or the [Israeli](https://www.gov.il/) government. I cannot think of another “peace signing” in history that did not include either of the main combatants. 

The aftermath has been shambles. Nine months after the “peace deal” fanfare, [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) still [occupies over half of the Gaza Strip](https://apnews.com/projects/israel-expansion-maps/), [Hamas](http://almoqawma.com/) is still the local authority there, deadly fighting has continued — albeit at a smaller scale than before — and talks have stalled out. The [so-called “Board of Peace”](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-board-of-peace-gaza-show) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) set up to oversee Gaza reconstruction has floundered. [Very little of the pledged money has transferred](https://www.france24.com/en/middle-east/20260527-trump-board-of-peace-official-gaza-fund-is-empty-despite-billions-pledged-source-says) and none has been spent — not least because [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) punted on broader [Israel](https://www.gov.il/)-[Palestine](https://www.president.ps/eng/) issues and did nothing to provide the security that could allow reconstruction to take place.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) tried a similar approach to the [Ukraine](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/) war, which he repeatedly swore [he could end in 24 hours](https://www.cnn.com/2025/04/25/politics/fact-check-trump-ukraine-war). His plan was to go around the [Ukrainians](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/), cutting a deal directly with [Russia](http://kremlin.ru/) then pressuring [Kyiv](https://kyivcity.gov.ua/) to accept it. The [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) denigrated [Ukrainian](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/) [President](https://president.gov.ua/) [Volodymyr Zelenskyy](https://www.president.gov.ua/president/about-president), [parroted the absurd Russian propaganda](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/trump-ukraine-russia-putin-world-war-iii-propaganda-rcna194596) that the war [Russia](http://kremlin.ru/) started somehow wasn’t [Russia](http://kremlin.ru/)’s fault and rolled out the red carpet ([literally](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/resources/idt-4d306ec6-4e6e-4f57-b9f8-adcc74fe7f0d)) for [Russian President](http://kremlin.ru/) [Vladimir Putin](putin.kremlin.ru/) in [Alaska](https://www.alaska.gov/).

 Finding a lasting end to an armed conflict isn’t possible without agreement from the primary forces involved in the fighting.

In doing so, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) adopted [Putin](http://putin.kremlin.ru/)’s preferred frame of the war as the U.S. and [Russia](http://kremlin.ru/) settling spheres of influence like 19th century empires. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) envoy [Steve Witkoff](https://www.witkoff.com/) put out a [28-point plan that amounted to Ukrainian surrender](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-ukraine-russia-peace-plan-putin-win), which [Ukraine](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/) unsurprisingly rejected. After almost a year and a half of the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) [denying Ukraine’s agency](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/trumps-russia-ukraine-peace-talks-are-farce-rcna207156) failed to end the war, rather than switch strategies, [the U.S. quit the peace process](https://tvpworld.com/93402017/us-ends-peace-talks-between-russia-and-ukraine).

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) followed a similar approach in his first term too, [negotiating with the Taliban](https://theconversation.com/how-trump-the-master-deal-maker-failed-when-it-came-to-negotiating-with-the-taliban-in-afghanistan-250835)  without including the [Afghan](https://ocs.gov.af/) government. In early 2020, [Trump let thousands of Taliban fighters out of prison](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-54155768) and ceased anti-[Taliban](https://alemarah.af/) military operations in exchange for the [Taliban](https://alemarah.af/) refraining from attacks on American forces as the U.S. withdrew. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) set the withdrawal date after the end of his term, leaving the hard part to the next [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/). When the U.S. withdrew in 2021, the elected [Afghan](https://ocs.gov.af/) government America spent two decades propping up [collapsed almost immediately after](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2021/aug/15/the-fall-of-kabul-a-20-year-mission-collapses-in-a-single-day).

In Gaza, [Ukraine](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/) and [Afghan](https://ocs.gov.af/)istan, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) tried to find shortcuts to peace, touting arrangements that didn’t include some of the combatants. Every time he’s done so by excluding a conflict participant from the negotiating table has had bad results.  Finding a lasting end to an armed conflict isn’t possible without agreement from the primary forces involved in the fighting.

As [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has inadvertently shown, there’s no shortcut to peace. Trying to do an end run around conflict participants doesn’t work —and sometimes makes things worse. By linking [Israel](https://www.gov.il/) and [Hezbollah](http://moqawama.org.lb/)’s fighting to the ceasefire without their buy-in, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has made the already difficult job of ending a war much harder.
