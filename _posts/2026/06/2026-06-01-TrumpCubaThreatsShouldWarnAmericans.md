Cuba is a demolition buy. It will cost more to fix Cuba than to tear it down and start over.

## [Trump's threats to invade Cuba should alarm all Americans](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-cuba-invasion-military-threats)

_What on earth is the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) thinking?_

May. 26, 2026, 4:30 PM EDT
By [Zeeshan Aleem](https://www.ms.now/author/zeeshan-aleem)

As unimaginable as it may sound, [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is seriously considering invading yet another country. The [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s [escalating pressure tactics against Cub](https://www.ms.now/the-beat-with-ari/watch/all-eyes-on-what-trump-will-do-in-cuba-after-venezuela-and-iran-2501108291859) resemble the ones the U.S. used against [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/) before [ousting its president](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-venezuela-nicolas-maduro-regime-change) and [proudly seizing control](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy4qdnj5vl9o) of its oil. [Bored with](https://www.theatlantic.com/national-security/2026/05/iran-war-trump-deal/687100/) his misadventure in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), [the mad emperor](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-war-plan-attack) appears to be eyeing another toy.

But an invasion of [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) is highly unlikely to go the way the attack on [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/) did, and the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is in a far worse position to sell the idea to the public — including his die-hard base. That [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) appears to be building toward a new military action while [still mired in his self-made crisis](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-uranium-necessary-public-relations) in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) illustrates just how disconnected he’s becoming from even the most basic political constraints. And his party is doing nothing to stop him. 

The decision to potentially invade another country speaks to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s increasingly radical disregard for public opinion.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has been floating the idea of [regime change](https://thehill.com/policy/international/5759118-cuba-friendly-takeover-trump/) in [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) for months, but his saber-rattling has become loud in the past week. The [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) parked the [USS Nimitz aircraft carrier](https://www.airpac.navy.mil/Organization/USS-Nimitz-CVN-68/) and its escort warships in the southern Caribbean Sea on Wednesday in what appeared to be a show of force. The same day, the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) [announced murder charges](https://www.ms.now/news/u-s-indicts-raul-castro-cuba-brother-of-fidel) against former [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) [President](https://www.presidencia.gob.cu/) Raúl Castro over the 1996 downing of two small planes by a [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) fighter jet. [Acting Attorney General Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/dag/) [said he](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/watch-blanche-says-he-expects-raul-castro-to-face-charges-in-u-s-of-his-own-will-or-another-way) expects Castro to show up in court in the U.S. by “his own will or by another way.”

On Thursday, [Secretary of](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) [State](https://www.state.gov/) [Marco Rubio said](https://thehill.com/policy/national-security/5892092-trump-cuba-invasion-pretext/) [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) was a “national security threat” and accused it of being “a leading state sponsor of terror.” He also said that he preferred a negotiated agreement with [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) but that “the likelihood of that happening is not high.” [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [told reporters](https://apnews.com/article/trump-rubio-cuba-castro-intervention-a7a470404229ce2cf89b10501e8692b7) the same day that previous [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)s have looked to intervene in [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) for “50, 60 years” and “it looks like I’ll be the one that does it.”

Politico, citing a U.S. official and a person familiar with the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s discussions on [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/), [reported last week that](https://www.politico.com/news/magazine/2026/05/18/the-odds-of-trump-attacking-cuba-are-going-up-00926317) that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and his aides have “grown frustrated” that even though his pressure campaign — including [an oil blockade](https://cepr.net/publications/newsweek-trumps-oil-blockade-of-cuba-is-economic-violence-it-must-end/) — has wreaked havoc on the island nation, it has not led the [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) government to agree to reforms. Now the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) is “taking the military option more seriously than previously.” Those actions, Politico reports, could include an extraction operation of the kind used against [Venezuelan](https://gob.ve/) [President Nicolás Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classPresidente.php), but they could also involve attacks ranging “from a single airstrike meant to scare the regime into concessions to a ground invasion meant to uproot it.”

I had already deemed [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) a [mad emperor](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-war-plan-attack) for launching a war in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) with no clear rationale or plan. Remarkably, it looks like he may be angling to do that again, even though his efforts in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) have failed spectacularly and left the global economy hostage. 

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) may think of [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) as just another scenario like [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/) — his aggression there had limited effects on the country’s stability and on the surrounding economy. But in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) he’s already seen proof of how interventions can easily slip out of control and alter the global arena in unexpected ways. And [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) is not [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/). 

As [Daniel R. DePetris](https://www.defensepriorities.org/people/daniel-depetris/), a fellow at the [Defense Priorities](https://www.defensepriorities.org/) think tank, [noted in a column](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-cuba-talks-challenges) for MS NOW in in March, “the [Cuban Communist Party](http://www.pcc.cu/) is more unified and durable than [Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classPresidente.php)’s regime ever was, which means attempts by the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) to crack it by searching for a more pliable successor will prove more difficult.” [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) could quickly get caught up in another bloody, destabilizing intervention. And the search for a [Cuban](http://cubaminrex.cu/) equivalent of [Delcy Rodríguez](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/) ([Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classPresidente.php)’s former [vice president](https://vicepresidencia.gob.ve/), who is now working with the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)) could be futile. 

No matter how [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) ultimately goes, the decision to potentially invade another country speaks to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s increasingly radical disregard for public opinion. The war with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) is one of the biggest errors of his political career. High gas prices have hurt his standing with his base and the public more broadly, and he has infuriated supporters who believed him when he promised “no new wars.” How is he contemplating another one?

One more intervention will further diffuse [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s attention, which ought to be laser-focused on ending his catastrophic war of aggression in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/). It will further convince disillusioned elements of his base that he was lying to their face when he said he would not be another American [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) obsessed with meddling in other countries’ affairs. And it will be far more difficult for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) to manufacture a pretext or imperialistic “reward” to justify it. [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) has been a U.S. adversary with nuclear ambitions for a long time. [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/) has oil. But nobody is going to believe that [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) poses a serious national security threat to the U.S., or that pursuing regime change in that tiny, impoverished and isolated nation is necessary or would be clearly advantageous to the U.S.

[A YouGov poll](https://yougov.com/en-us/articles/54332-more-americans-disapprove-than-approve-of-the-us-blocking-oil-shipments-to-cuba-and-the-us-embargo) conducted in late February showed that a plurality of Americans disapproved of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s oil blockade against [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/), and 61% said they would oppose the U.S. using military force to attack [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/). It’s hard to imagine those numbers looking better for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) after his fiasco in [Iran](https://irangov.ir/).

Because [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is saber-rattling at the same time as he’s sowing other chaos at home and abroad, including two foreign interventions, it’s difficult to comprehend how close we may be to a third. A rebuke from his own party could cause him to think twice, or limit his ability to wage war, but we know by now that the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) has surrendered itself to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s malicious desires.

[Zeeshan Aleem](https://www.ms.now/author/zeeshan-aleem) is a writer and editor for MS NOW. He primarily writes about politics and foreign policy.
