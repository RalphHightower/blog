## [The White House UFC fight is a grand pageant for an 80-year-old boy king](https://www.ms.now/opinion/white-house-ufc-match-trump-birthday)

_A $60 million price tag outstrips even the most lavish spectacles that medieval tournaments provided for feudal lords._

Jun. 14, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Hayes Brown](https://www.ms.now/author/hayes-brown)


But the timing of the event, taking place on [President Donald Trump’s 80th birthday](https://www.ms.now/opinion/donald-trump-aging-medical-nyt), is no mere coincidence. As with [last year’s military parade](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/trump-military-parade-cost-birthday-rcna205872), ostensibly in honor of the [U.S. Army](https://www.army.mil/)’s 200th birthday, the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has seized on the opportunity to [disguise a taxpayer-funded gift to himself as a present](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-250-anniversary-bill-monuments) as a present to the nation. While the [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) attempts to link these fights to the revolutionaries who rebelled against a monarch, Sunday’s event more closely resembles a king and his court attending a tournament in the late Middle Ages. The federal government has spent an embarrassingly large sum in the hopes of providing a diversion for [America’s would-be king](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-king-approval-iran-tariffs) as the country’s greatest ills go untreated.

> **The federal government has spent an embarrassingly large sum in the hopes of providing a diversion for America’s would-be king as the country’s greatest ills go untreated.**

[Presidents](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) enjoying a bit of rough-and-tumble is nothing new. Abraham Lincoln was known for being a wrestler in his youth, while [Theodore Roosevelt’s love of boxing](https://www.washingtonpost.com/dc-md-va/2026/06/09/president-held-fight-white-house-before-he-was-partially-blinded/?) followed him to the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/). [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is neither. He is a man who has late in life gravitated towards mixed martial arts and in particular its blend of pro-wrestling’s dramatic characters and the bare-knuckled beatings its practitioners administer. It befits a man who once [reportedly made his son fast-forward](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/1997/05/19/trump-solo) through all the boring plot exposition of a favorite action movie, “[Bloodsport](https://melmagazine.com/en-us/story/an-oral-history-of-trumps-love-of-van-dammes-bloodsport).”

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s presence in the hastily constructed arena, [known as “The Claw,”](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/2026/05/29/inside-the-white-house-ufc-arena-how-big-south-lawn-setup-really-is/90275023007/) lends itself perfectly to the image of a feudal lord observing most modern conceptions of medieval tournaments. Unlike the recreations seen at Renaissance Faires and Medieval Times outposts around the country, the original tournaments focused on warriors [clashing in a massive melee](https://www.reddit.com/r/AskHistorians/comments/bwczhz/comment/epxeeln/?). Knights and other men-at-arms could display their battlefield skills in a more contained setting to win renown and ransom from their defeated opponents. Only over time [did tournaments shift to emphasize](https://www.english-heritage.org.uk/visit/inspire-me/brief-history-of-knights-tournaments/) the more scaled-down, but still dangerous, events like jousting.

By the 16<sup>th</sup> century, tournaments had also become a way for their noble organizers to show off their vast wealth or herald auspicious moments in their lives. When King Henry VIII of [England](https://www.gov.uk/) held a royal summit with King Francis I of [France](https://www.info.gouv.fr/) in 1520, it took place at the [“Field of the Cloth of Gold,”](https://www.hrp.org.uk/hampton-court-palace/history-and-stories/the-field-of-cloth-of-gold/) so named for how much of the expensive fabric was used in crafting the tents and costumes assembled there. Both royals [took part in the tournaments](https://web.archive.org/web/20150924092236/http:/www.royalarmouries.org/assets-uploaded/documents/KWatts_Web.pdf) to show off their own athleticism as a stand-in for their military prowess. [Prof. Glenn Robinson of St. Mary’s University estimated](https://www.stmarys.ac.uk/news/2020/st-marys-professor-marks-field-of-the-cloth-of-gold-500th-anniversary) estimated in 2020 that the whole shebang roughly cost the equivalent of £15 million pounds — [roughly $25 million](https://www.smithsonianmag.com/history/five-hundred-years-ago-henry-viii-and-francis-i-spent-19-million-18-day-party-180975116/).

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) will not be following Henry and Francis’ lead; the octogenarian [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) will not be competing in the Octagon, even though [25% of Republican men](https://yougov.com/en-us/articles/54707-how-many-americans-think-they-could-beat-donald-trump-in-a-fight) in a recent [YouGov](https://yougov.com/) survey claimed they’d be unable to beat [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) in a fight. But the price-tag of the [UFC](https://www.ufc.com/) event dwarfs that of the Tudor-era spectacle.  According to documents filed in response [a lawsuit that attempted to halt the fight](https://www.ms.now/deadline-white-house/deadline-legal-blog/trump-ufc-fight-white-house), the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) says that it has [already spent a jaw-dropping $60 million](https://apnews.com/article/trump-ufc-costs-federal-agencies-lawsuit-5bd8382d8d106d7685b024508a178748) pulling together the [presidential](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) fight night.

That absurd amount of money befits an absurdly grotesque event. It feels surreal that a cage match will be held on the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) lawn in honor of a man holding thousands of immigrant men, women, and children in cages around the country. It feels bewildering that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) will be on hand to watch displays of martial excellence even as the actual war he started with Iran simmers unresolved in the background. And it seems likely that this event will [yield a hefty profit](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-ufc-white-house-money) for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s allies like [UFC](https://www.ufc.com/) CEO Dana White — and [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) himself, given [his purchase of stock in UFC’s parent company](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/7319008/2026/05/29/trump-white-house-ufc-fight-stock/) before announcing the event last year.

> **This is being done not as a celebration of America’s origins but in the name of providing amusement for the aging boy king and his retinue**

The Roman Empire famously provided the plebians [free wheat and gladiatorial games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bread_and_circuses) to keep them in check and keep them from wanting more. [The White House likewise hopes](https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/12/politics/ufc-trump-white-house) that the showcase of sweaty brutality will help [lure back the young male voters](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/11/us/politics/trump-ufc-claw-white-house.html) who’ve grown disillusioned after voting for [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) in 2024. But for today’s masses there is no bread, only circuses, as underscored by an event held by the rich, for the rich, with another estimated 120,000 crowding to watch from the Ellipse.

All of this is being done not as a celebration of America’s origins but in the name of providing amusement for the aging boy king and his retinue. Given his professed love of the sport, maybe [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [manage to stay more engaged](https://deadline.com/2026/06/late-night-hosts-dunk-on-trump-nba-finals-ny-knicks-1236952262/) throughout the title card fight than he was during the [NBA](https://www.nba.com/) [Finals](https://www.nba.com/playoffs/2026/nba-finals) [game](https://www.nba.com/game/sas-vs-nyk-0042500403) he attended last week. At least the commute home for him, after the lights have dimmed, the victors named, and his birthday wish already granted, will be a short one.
