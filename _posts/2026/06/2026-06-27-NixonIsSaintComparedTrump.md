## [JD Vance’s Nixon defense is part of the GOP's war on accountability](https://www.ms.now/opinion/jd-vance-watergate-nixon-defense-trump)

_There’s a reason [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s allies take issue with the exposing of rot in a [presidential](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)._

Jun. 27, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Paul Waldman](https://www.ms.now/author/paul-waldman)

For half a century, [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) has been the quintessential American scandal, so much so that we frequently affix “-gate” to new episodes of official wrongdoing in an attempt to make it sound significant and sinister.

But what if, asked [Vice President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [JD Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/), we consider [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) no big deal? Or even better, why not decide that former [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Richard Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/), our pre-[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) model of corruption and abuse of office, was not the perpetrator of [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) but its victim?

“I’m actually fascinated by [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/) as a character in history,” [Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) said [at an appearance](https://apnews.com/article/vance-nixon-watergate-9a82141f1b4f5b2c973a4bdb107812d9) this week at the [Nixon Library](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/) in [California](https://www.ca.gov/). “I think that his historical legacy is enjoying a bit of a renaissance, but I think deservedly so. As I joked with Robert backstage, if [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) happened tomorrow, it would be like a 12-hour news story. Like, the idea that it would have taken down a presidency is crazy.”

> **Rewriting history is a longtime hobby of American conservatives.**

[Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) continued: “And by the way, if you look at the story of how the deep state took down [Richard Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/), it’s not all that different from what the same groups of people, the same institutions tried to do to [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and the first [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/). There is a parallel.”

Rewriting history is a longtime hobby of American conservatives, both to absolve them of their sins and reshape the American mind to align with their values. For instance, the “Lost Cause” counternarrative of the Civil War, which portrays the Confederacy’s treasonous slavery advocates into noble men simply defending their homes, [continues to this day](https://www.publicnotice.co/p/trump-confederacy-rises-again), with the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) [renaming military bases](https://apnews.com/article/trump-army-bases-confederate-names-69f63771d0e7ca859d42c485129d1228) and [erecting statues](https://apnews.com/article/trump-pike-statue-confederate-dc-george-floyd-3b0e8fd940a6bb6432992bb615b66336) to honor those traitors. And [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has convinced much of his party to believe that the [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf) [was stolen from him](https://www.cnn.com/2023/08/03/politics/cnn-poll-republicans-think-2020-election-illegitimate) and that the [Jan. 6 insurrection was merely a largely peaceful protest](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/less-than-half-of-gop-say-jan-6-was-very-violent-ap-norc-poll) carried out by true patriots.

[Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) has been part of this revisionist project since the day [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/) resigned the presidency. [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/)’s legal arguments against having to hand over [Oval Office](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) tapes to investigators, though unsuccessful at the time, became crucial to the development of [the unitary executive theory](https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2025/05/donald-trump-unitary-executive-theory/), championed by [former Vice President Dick Cheney](https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/news/releases/2005/12/20051220-9.html), and the Roberts court’s conservative majority holds. Most recently, [prominent figures](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/politics-news/campaign-make-richard-nixon-great-rcna240000) on the right, including [Tucker Carlson](https://tuckercarlson.com/), [Steve Bannon](https://warroom.org/) and [Christopher Rufo](https://christopherrufo.com/), have argued that [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) was all a set-up by the “deep state” to frame [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/), who was innocent. [Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) has now joined their number.

Remember: [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) was far more than just a break-in at [Democratic Party](https://www.democrats.org/) headquarters in the [Washington](https://dc.gov/) office/apartment complex that gave the scandal its name. The burglary merely led to the exposure of the rot within the [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/): a mountain of criminal acts from money laundering to obstruction of justice to multiple additional break-ins to a shocking abuse of government power.

At one point, [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) counsel John Dean, who later located his conscience and told the public what he had seen and done, [penned a memo](https://exhibits.lib.unc.edu/items/show/7896) for other White House officials exploring “how we can use the available federal machinery to screw our political enemies.” The [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) tapes recorded [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/) personally committing criminal acts, including making a plan to order the [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) to quash the [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/)’s investigation of the break-in at the [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) (the so-called [“smoking gun” recording](https://www.politico.com/story/2018/08/05/watergate-smoking-gun-tape-released-aug-5-1974-753086)).

In the end, dozens of people in [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/)’s [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) and political orbit pled guilty or were convicted of crimes. Those sent to prison included the [attorney general](https://www.justice.gov/), the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) chief of staff and the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s chief domestic policy adviser.

> **We’ll need a raft of post-[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) reforms beyond even what [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) passed after [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/) slinked away in disgrace.**

In a twisted sense, [Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) is almost right about the “deep state,” since he and other [MAGA](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) figures use that term to refer to civil servants who are loyal to the country rather than to whatever corrupt scheme [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) wants to recruit them for. But they didn’t frame [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/); they exposed him. During [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html), the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and his top aides pressed multiple government officials, including some appointed by [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/) himself, to do things they knew were illegal or unethical. Those officials simply refused; some, like Dean, went public.

That is similar to what happened in [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s first [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/): Again and again, officials refused to go along with his corrupt schemes; some of them testified before [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) in [his first impeachment](https://abcnews.com/Politics/trump-impeachment-inquiry-testified-congress/story?id=66763043) or [before the Jan. 6 committee](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ip4USpK9TpI). But in [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s second [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/), the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and his closest advisers have ensured that no one with any integrity will be around to object.

[Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) passed a raft of post-[Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) laws and set up new systems intended to restrain the presidency and make government more transparent, less corrupt and more accountable. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has systematically [set out to destroy them](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/02/us/politics/president-trump-powers-watergate.html), firing inspectors general, weakening civil service protections, [defying the laws](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/03/politics/trump-presidential-records-act-watergate) he doesn’t like and turning the [Department of Justice](https://www.justice.gov/) into his personal machine of political revenge.

After [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html), we were told that “the cover-up is worse than the crime.” It wasn’t actually true — the cover-up was appalling, but it was preceded by a whole series of atrocious crimes.  [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), however, decided not to even bother with the cover-up. If you don’t try to hide your corruption, lawmakers won’t be as shocked. And if your own party controls [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/), they’ll be too terrified of your voters to hold you accountable.

So when [Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/) says [Watergate](https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/american_originals/nixon.html) would be a one-day story today, he isn’t just observing; he’s boasting. He’s saying: Look what [MAGA](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has done to American politics and media. Look how we have normalized corruption and degraded systems of accountability. We can do whatever we want.

For now, they can. Which is why we’ll need a raft of post-[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) reforms beyond even what [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) passed after [Nixon](https://www.nixonlibrary.gov/) slinked away in disgrace. The most corrupt [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) in history and his contemptible cronies are trying to justify their own misdeeds by rehabilitating the image of the second-most corrupt [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) in history. We can’t allow it to succeed.

[Paul Waldman](https://www.ms.now/author/paul-waldman) is a journalist and author focused on politics and culture.
