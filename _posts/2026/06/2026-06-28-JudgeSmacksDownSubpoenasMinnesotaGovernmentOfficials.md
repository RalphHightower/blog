## [Judge smacks down DOJ on subpoenas for Minnesota officials, including Walz and Frey](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/judge-smacks-down-doj-subpoenas-minnesota-walz-frey)

_The jurist condemned “the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s well-established history of using criminal investigations to retaliate” against [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s political foes._

Jun. 22, 2026, 2:22 PM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

When the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) announced more than four months ago that it was [ending its immigration enforcement operation in Minnesota](https://www.ms.now/news/federal-immigration-operation-in-minnesota-is-ending-homan-says) — an operation that left two Americans dead and sparked a national outcry — one brutal chapter of a larger story came to an end.

It was not, however, the final chapter. On the contrary, the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and his team launched a series of less violent tactics, which included [trying to cut Medicaid funds](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-administration-medicaid-funding-minnesota) to [Minnesota](https://www.mn.gov/), and just last week, [charging 15 people](https://www.ms.now/news/federal-charges-minneapolis-anti-ice-protest) in connection with their involvement in protests against the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s so-called Operation Metro Surge in [Minneapolis](https://www.minneapolismn.gov/). (Prosecutors accused Direct Action [Minnesota](https://www.mn.gov/) of being affiliated with antifa, which [seems](https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mog6qh6tjs2v) like [a stretch](https://newrepublic.com/post/211947/doj-indicts-protesters-minnesota).)

But this wasn’t the only foray from [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) related to [Minnesota](https://www.mn.gov/). [Politico reported](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/06/22/trump-justice-department-tim-walz-minnesota-subpoenas-00969849):

> _A federal judge has thrown out [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) grand jury subpoenas aimed at [Minnesota](https://www.mn.gov/) [Gov.](https://mn.gov/governor/) [Tim Walz](https://mn.gov/governor/about-gov/timwalz/) and his allies, calling them an abusive and retaliatory process to punish [Walz](https://mn.gov/governor/about-gov/timwalz/) based on his refusal to assist [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s immigration crackdown._
>
> _In a blistering ruling, [U.S. District](https://www.mnd.uscourts.gov/) [Judge Patrick Schiltz](https://www.mnd.uscourts.gov/PJS) said there was “no doubt” that the subpoenas were issued to damage [Walz](https://mn.gov/governor/about-gov/timwalz/) — part of what he said was a pattern of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) efforts to use criminal process to punish the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s adversaries._

“Initiating a criminal investigation in order to harass political opponents or to coerce them into taking official action — particularly official action that the federal government cannot directly require those political opponents to take — is a blatantly unlawful and unethical use the grand-jury process,” [Schiltz](https://www.mnd.uscourts.gov/PJS), who was appointed by [George W. Bush](https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/), wrote [in a 29-page ruling](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.mnd.234490/gov.uscourts.mnd.234490.1.0.pdf) unsealed Monday.

The timeline of events is worth keeping in mind. As January got underway and [Minnesota](https://www.mn.gov/)’s incumbent [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) [governor](https://mn.gov/governor/about-gov/timwalz/) announced he was ending his bid for a third term, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and his [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) team said the end of the [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) [governor](https://mn.gov/governor/about-gov/timwalz/)’s campaign [wasn’t enough](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/for-the-white-house-tim-walzs-decision-not-to-run-for-re-election-isnt-enough), and they wanted [Walz](https://mn.gov/governor/about-gov/timwalz/) to [face a Justice Department investigation](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/for-the-white-house-tim-walzs-decision-not-to-run-for-re-election-isnt-enough).

Less than a week later, [The Wall Street Journal reported](https://www.wsj.com/politics/policy/trump-has-complained-about-pam-bondi-repeatedly-to-aides-fd424df3?mod=hp_lead_pos5) that the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) had begun complaining privately about then-[Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Pam Bondi](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-attorney-general), “describing her as weak and an ineffective enforcer of his agenda,” at least in part because she hadn’t pursued his perceived political enemies as quickly as he would prefer. The following day, the [Journal](https://www.wsj.com/) published [a follow-up report](https://www.wsj.com/politics/policy/trump-blasted-federal-prosecutors-at-white-house-event-calling-them-weak-c185412b?mod=hp_lead_pos1), adding that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) also criticized some of his own U.S. attorneys at a [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) event, “complaining they weren’t moving fast enough to prosecute his favored targets.”

A few days after the [Journal](https://www.wsj.com/)’s report reached the public, the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) [opened an investigation](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trumps-doj-to-investigate-walz-and-frey-but-not-ice-agent-who-shot-renee-good) into [Walz](https://mn.gov/governor/about-gov/timwalz/) and [Minneapolis](https://www.minneapolismn.gov/) [Mayor](https://www.minneapolismn.gov/government/mayor/) [Jacob Frey](https://www.minneapolismn.gov/government/mayor/about-the-mayor/).

Evidently, the federal judge who just threw out [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) grand jury subpoenas aimed at [Minnesota](https://www.mn.gov/) officials took note of these relevant details.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s repeated vows of “retribution,” [Schiltz](https://www.mnd.uscourts.gov/PJS) wrote, “establishes beyond reasonable dispute” that the grand jury subpoenas “were a part of a broader campaign to coerce state and local officials in [Minnesota](https://www.mn.gov/) to assist the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) in its enforcement of immigration laws.”

In case that weren’t quite enough, the jurist tied the subpoenas to what he called “the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s well-established history of using criminal investigations to retaliate against and pressure the [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s political and personal adversaries.”

[Walz](https://mn.gov/governor/about-gov/timwalz/) celebrated the decision as “a victory for the rule of law and our democracy,” which it most certainly was, notwithstanding the likely appeal.
