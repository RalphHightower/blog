## [How 3 documents show Trump is on a path to reject the midterm results](https://www.ms.now/news/news-analysis/trump-election-interference-claims-documents-china)

_The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is using the same playbook he did to deny his [2020 loss](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf): spreading conspiracy theories about “deep state” plots and undermining trust in elections._

Jul. 17, 2026, 5:07 PM EDT
By [David Rohde](https://www.ms.now/author/david-rohde)

Documents that [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) ordered released don’t support his sweeping claims made during a primetime address on Thursday that [China](https://www.gov.cn/) manufactured fake votes for former [President Joe Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/) in 2020 and that a “deep state” cabal hid [China](https://www.gov.cn/)’s activities from him.

A review of the declassified intelligence reports showed that, in fact, his current [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) director, [John Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/), either was part of the deep state plot or failed to discover it when he was [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s top intelligence official during his first term. It also showed that [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) was briefed in June 2020 on [China](https://www.gov.cn/)’s efforts to influence American politics.

Conspiracies that [China](https://www.gov.cn/) took actions to change vote totals  in 2020 were investigated and debunked by intelligence and [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) officials during [Trump first term administration](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/)’s, documents show. Many of the claims in the documents were also not new.

[Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s Thursday night [speech](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-speech-election-stolen), though, may have already done its intended damage, according to former intelligence officials and election experts. He is using the same playbook he did in 2020: spreading conspiracy theories, describing himself as a victim of plot and refusing to accept election results.

Only this time, his target is the midterm elections.

“He’s buying an option on any election,” said a former senior U.S. intelligence official, who spoke on condition of anonymity. “If [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) win, he can say it’s because they fortified elections, and if they lose, that’s proof of his claim that they’re rigged.”

Either way, [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) “wins,” said the former official, “and the country loses trust in a fundamental pillar of our democracy.”

> **“He’s buying an option on any election,” said a former senior U.S. intelligence official, who spoke on condition of anonymity, referring to [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/). “If [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) win, he can say it’s because they fortified elections, and if they lose, that’s proof of his claim that they’re rigged.”**

[Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) called Thursday’s speech “frightening” and a sign that [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is on a path toward rejecting the midterm results in November if [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) stand to lose control of [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/).

That, they fear, would set the country on a path toward another Jan. 6-like confrontation and the further erosion of American democracy.

“His whole speech is designed to be the ‘I told you so’ he needs when he inevitably condemns the elections next November when they deliver political accountability to his failed presidency,” [Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Jim Himes (D-CT4)](https://himes.house.gov/), the ranking member of the [House Intelligence Committee](https://intelligence.house.gov/), told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/).

The faces gathered in front of [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) in the [East Room](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) of the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) during his speech also suggested that he is in a more powerful position than he was in 2020.

Gone were key former officials who resisted or declined to be part of his effort to overturn the 2020 election results — [Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Bill Barr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Barr?wprov=sfla1), [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) [Director Chris Wray](https://www.fbi.gov/history/directors/christopher-wray), [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) counsel [Pat Cipollone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pat_Cipollone?wprov=sfla1) and [Defense](https://www.defense.gov/) [Secretary Mark Esper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Esper?wprov=sfla1). They have been replaced by [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) loyalists [Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/dag), [Kash Patel](https://www.fbi.gov/about/leadership-and-structure/director-patel), [David Warrington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Warrington?wprov=sfla1) and [Pete Hegseth](https://www.defense.gov/About/Biographies/Biography/Article/4040890/hon-pete-hegseth/).

Former intelligence officials told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) that [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s continued use of false “deep state” claims is likely to cause experienced officials to retire early or leave.

A former senior [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) official, who spoke on condition of anonymity, citing fears of retaliation, predicted that the speech would cause fewer people to want to join the [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) and fewer nations to want to share intelligence with it.

“More demoralizing straws on the camel for those hardworking Americans at [Langley](https://www.fairfaxcounty.gov/) to accelerate their retirement, for new recruits to think twice about working there,” the former official said. “And for partners and agents to question sharing information with the U.S.”

[Jonathan Diaz](https://campaignlegal.org/staff/jonathan-diaz), the director of voting advocacy at the [Campaign Legal Center](https://campaignlegal.org/), an ethics watchdog group, criticized [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s tactics but predicted he would fail to derail the midterm elections.

“Declassifying documents, threatening election officials with prosecution, making wildly unsupported claims about voter registration numbers,” [Diaz](https://campaignlegal.org/staff/jonathan-diaz) said, referring to [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s recent actions. “But just like in 2020, if the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and his allies lose the election, I expect the will of the voters to prevail in the end.”

Here’s a close look at 3 documents [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) released that undermine his election interference claims:

### The president’s daily brief

![A clipping of a document released by the White House that shows a Presidential Daily Briefing doc from June 25, 2020 The White House](/assets/images/20260720-PresidentialDailyBrief.webp)

A clipping of a document from June 25, 2020 with a headline that reads "[Beijing](https://www.beijing.gov.cn/) Escalating Effors To Shape US Policies on [China](https://www.gov.cn/)"A clipping of a document released by the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) that shows a Presidential Daily Briefing doc from June 25, 2020 The [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)
The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s daily brief, or PDB, is considered highly classified and a collection of the intelligence community’s most sensitive information. It is designed to contain the nation’s most urgent and confident assessments of the threats facing the country and is delivered to the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) each day

A PDB declassified by [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is dated June 25, 2020 —the final year of [Trump](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/)’s first term. It disproves [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s claim in primetime that intelligence officials did not tell him about [Chinese](https://www.gov.cn/) efforts to influence American politics.

The heading of the document says, “[Beijing](https://www.beijing.gov.cn/) Escalating Efforts to Shape US policies on [China](https://www.gov.cn/).” A section that is not redacted discusses warning “a [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) official that [Beijing](https://www.beijing.gov.cn/) had derogatory information on him in an effort to compel him to take a restrained approach to [China](https://www.gov.cn/).”

In his speech, [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) said his daily brief did not include important information about [China](https://www.gov.cn/).

### TikTok and fake driver’s license

![A FBI document released by the White House. The White House](/assets/images/20262020-TikTocDriversLicense.webp)

“These were briefings I would get almost every day,” he said. “Everything was kept out that was of importance.”

[Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) falsely said in his address that [Chinese](https://www.gov.cn/) officials took steps to produce illegal ballots in 2020. The allegation [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) cited was not new and had been released more than a year ago by [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Chuck Grassley (T-IA)](https://www.grassley.senate.gov/). The document is a memo by the [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/)’s [Albany, New York, office](https://www.fbi.gov/contact-us/field-offices/albany) describing an individual with secondhand information.

The individual said an associate said [Chinese](https://www.gov.cn/) officials told him [Beijing](https://www.beijing.gov.cn/) was producing fake U.S. driver’s licenses that could be used to cast fraudulent mail-in ballots for [Biden](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/). The person said the [Chinese](https://www.gov.cn/) had collected Americans’ addresses from “millions of [TikTok accounts](https://www.tiktok.com/).”

But the tip didn’t hold up to scrutiny. In the comments section of the memo, an [FBI](https://www.fbi.gov/) agent cautioned that [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/) does not require a person’s street address when they create an account.

“A person’s address information was not a valid field when creating a [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/) account,” the agent wrote.

A former senior federal law enforcement official said the report was also deemed unreliable because the person also said [Covid-19 was being spread](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/16/us/politics/assessing-the-documents-fake-ids-from-china.html) to Americans by secret [Chinese](https://www.gov.cn/)-run locations in the U.S.

The former official, who spoke on condition of anonymity, citing fear of retaliation, said none of the documents that [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) released show proof that voting machines had been hacked or that vote totals had been changed.

“Nothing in those documents shows anything that would flip an election,” the former official said.

### CIA’s unverified intelligence

![A CIA note released by the White House The White House](/assets/images/20260720-CIAUnverifiedDocument.webp)

One of the documents [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) discussed describes a series of startling claims about [China](https://www.gov.cn/). Along with trying to force [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) from power, it said [Beijing](https://www.beijing.gov.cn/) planned to financially pressure Americans who supported [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), according to the document. It also said the [Chinese](https://www.gov.cn/) government sought to identify journalists who wrote negative stories about [Trump](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and pay them to write more of them.

“What we don’t know is where this sits in the body or reporting, the totality of reporting,” one former U.S. intelligence official. “There might be equal or larger bodies of reporting that either contradict or fail to confirm this information. The sourcing is everything.”

The official noted that a 2021 U.S. intelligence assessment that was based on vetted intelligence mentioned none of the allegations. And a dissent by [Ratcliffe](https://www.cia.gov/about/director-of-cia/), then the director of national intelligence, also makes no mention of the claims.

A second former senior US intelligence official said the [CIA](https://www.cia.gov/) note simply looks like a memo of selective past reporting, “a summary but not an analytical product,” referring to an intelligence document based on corroborated information and reviewed by analysts who are subject matter experts.

The senior former federal law enforcement official expressed a sense of deep betrayal.

“We went through hell in 2020 protecting the election and to have the very people who were in charge back then now say we were corrupt is sad but not surprising,” the official said. “History will judge what we did.”
