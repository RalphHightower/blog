## [Rep. Jim Jordan refers Jack Smith to DOJ for potential criminal investigation](https://www.ms.now/news/jim-jordan-criminal-referral-jack-smith)

_The [House Judiciary](https://judiciary.house.gov/) [chairman](https://judiciary.house.gov/the-committee/the-chairman) accused the former special counsel of making false statements about his [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf) probe. The referral does not require the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) to prosecute._

Jul. 22, 2026, 11:45 PM EDT
By [Mychael Schnell](https://www.ms.now/author/mychael-schnell) and [Carla Herreria](https://www.ms.now/author/carla-herreria)

[House Judiciary Committee](https://judiciary.house.gov/) [Chairman](https://judiciary.house.gov/the-committee/the-chairman) [Jim Jordan](https://jordan.house.gov/) referred former special counsel [Jack Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) to the [Department of Justice](https://www.justice.gov/) on Wednesday for a potential criminal investigation, accusing [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) of intentionally making false statements to [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) last year.

In his letter to acting [Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Todd Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-acting-attorney-general-0), [Jordan](https://jordan.house.gov/) alleged that there is “strong evidence” that [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) “made willfully and intentionally false statements” in a closed-door deposition Dec. 17. Specifically, the [Ohio Republican](https://ohiogop.org/) pointed to [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s answers to questions about whether prosecutors obtained phone records of members of [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) during his investigation into efforts to overturn the [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf).

During the deposition, [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) testified that he did not seek search warrants for the contents of any text messages from members of [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) during his investigation, and only sought toll records. However, in his letter to [Blanche](https://www.justice.gov/ag/staff-profile/meet-acting-attorney-general-0), [Jordan](https://jordan.house.gov/) noted that documents the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) produced this month show [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s team obtained text messages from 44 lawmakers.

“The responses provided by [Mr. Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) gave the impression that neither [Mr. Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) nor his team had access to the content of Members’ privileged text messages,” [Jordan](https://jordan.house.gov/) wrote in the referral. “We now know this to be false.”

In their response letter to the department, [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s attorneys called the referral “meritless” and based on a “spurious claim” that the former special counsel gave false testimony not because of anything he said, but because he didn’t volunteer that his team had, in fact, obtained text messages from some members of [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) simply by pulling them from the [National Archives](https://www.archives.gov/).

A congressional criminal referral is a nonbinding request asking the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) to investigate whether someone has violated federal law and to determine whether further action is needed. The [DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/) is not required to pursue charges or even open an investigation.

[Rep.](https://www.house.gov/) [Jamie Raskin](https://raskin.house.gov/), the ranking member of the committee, denounced the referral, saying in a statement that [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) “have embarrassed themselves again in this investigation.”

“It seems [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) Members of [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) are lashing out because they just realized their communications with the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) became presidential records preserved at the [National Archives](https://www.archives.gov/) as required by the [Presidential Records Act](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?path=/prelim@title44/chapter22&edition=prelim),” the [Maryland Democrat](https://mddems.org/) said.

“This referral deliberately distorts and misstates the facts, including what questions the Republicans actually asked at [Mr. Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s interview.”

As special counsel under the [Biden administration](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/), [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) brought two federal criminal cases against [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), including one accusing him of conspiring to overturn the results of the [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf). [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) dropped both cases after [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) won the [2024 presidential election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/2024presgeresults.pdf), citing longstanding [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) policy against prosecuting a sitting president.
