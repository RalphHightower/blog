## "He's Very Unwell" - Marsh Family parody of Chuck Berry's "Never Can Tell" from Pulp Fiction

Apr 19, 2026  FAVERSHAM
The issue of [Christian nationalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_nationalism_in_the_United_States?wprov=sfla1) and claims to righteousness for US military action has surfaced in a few stories this week, so we decided to merge them together in one song. We had to pick something from the [Pulp Fiction](https://www.miramax.com/movie/pulp-fiction/) soundtrack (1994), because that’s the movie script that’s been in the media spotlight since being bizarrely parroted in a prayer service at the [Pentagon](https://www.defense.gov/) led by the US [Secretary of](https://www.defense.gov/About/Biographies/Biography/Article/4040890/hon-pete-hegseth/) [Defence](https://www.defense.gov/), [Pete Hegseth](https://www.defense.gov/About/Biographies/Biography/Article/4040890/hon-pete-hegseth/). Although we were sorely torn by “Son of a Preacher Man” (a better song), in the end we plumped for the iconic twist track that features the smooth moves of [Uma Thurman](https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0000235/) and [John Travolta](https://travolta.com/) in a café running a dance contest, and is titled “You Never Can Tell” (though often referred to as “C’est La Vie”). The track was written by [Chuck Berry](https://www.chuckberry.com/) while he was in federal prison and finally released in 1964.

The verses gave us the chance to poke some fun at three circling stories: [Pete Hegseth](https://www.defense.gov/About/Biographies/Biography/Article/4040890/hon-pete-hegseth/)’s language and attempt to render a [Samuel L. Jackson](https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0000168/) monologue, amidst his hawkish attitude and celebration of violence and vengeance in the [Iran war](https://irangov.ir/); [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s posting on his Truth Social account of a ridiculous picture of him in a Christ-like pose, before backtracking and claiming he thought he was a doctor; and the deepening chasm between the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s discourse and policies and the Christian ethos explained by the [Pope](https://www.vatican.va/).
