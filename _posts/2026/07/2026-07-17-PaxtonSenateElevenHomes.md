## [Ken Paxton’s many real estate holdings become an issue in Texas Senate race](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/ken-paxton-real-estate-holdings-texas-senate)

_[State Rep.](https://house.texas.gov/) [James Talarico](https://house.texas.gov/members/3685) said the [state attorney general](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/) owns 11 homes. According to public documents, that’s accurate._

July 15, 2026, 3:37 PM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

[Texas](https://www.texas.gov/) [Attorney General](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/) [Ken Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office)’s [long list of scandals](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/the-long-list-of-scandals-that-will-dog-texas-ken-paxton-from-now-until-election-day) has caused unease among some [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/). [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Bill Cassidy (T-LA)](https://www.cassidy.senate.gov/), for example, [said in May](https://x.com/AnthonyAdragna/status/2056784596226732481) that [Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office) “could reasonably be indicted for a felony.” [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Thom Tillis (T-NC)](https://www.tillis.senate.gov/) [added](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/26/us/politics/texas-runoff-senate-cornyn-paxton.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share), “To call [Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office) ethically challenged is to call Jeffrey Dahmer suffering from an eating disorder.”

Complicating matters for the [Texan](https://www.texas.gov/) is that his list of controversies [continues to grow](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/texas-senate-ken-paxton-voter-registration). Last week, [ProPublica](https://www.propublica.org/article/ken-paxton-voter-registration-election-law) and [The Texas Tribune](https://www.texastribune.org/2026/07/07/ken-paxton-voting-election-law/) reported that [Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office) appears to have used an address where he did not live “while voting in six elections in the past two years.” (A spokesperson for the [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) called the story “a baseless, lie-filled tabloid story” but offered nothing of substance to challenge its accuracy.)

This might not seem especially notable, were it not for the fact that [Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office), just months earlier, reminded [Texans](https://www.texas.gov/) that under state law, “you must register to vote using the address where you reside.”

This dovetailed with related questions about why the [state attorney general](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/) and his [now-estranged wife](https://senate.texas.gov/member.php?d=8) [declared three separate homes as their primary residence](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/07/24/us/politics/ken-paxton-mortgage.html) in mortgage documents, a practice his party has, in other situations, [characterized as an outrageous abuse](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/mortgage-fraud-allegations-generate-new-political-headaches-team-trump-rcna229883).

Stepping back, however, the reporting sparked a related question: Just how many homes does [Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office) own?

His 2026 opponent, [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) state Rep. [James Talarico](https://house.texas.gov/members/3685), recently sat down with [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/)’s [Jen Psaki](https://www.ms.now/author/jen-psaki) [and offered an answer](https://bsky.app/profile/dscc.bsky.social/post/3movaup65cl2d): “Since taking office, [Ken Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office) has now become a multimillionaire, while our pay has remained stagnant for years, for decades. He now owns 11 homes, while most [Texan](https://www.texas.gov/) can’t afford one,” he said.

[Talarico](https://jamestalarico.com/) has [said this](https://bsky.app/profile/jamestalarico.bsky.social/post/3mny2ddy7g22a) before — as [Texan](https://www.texas.gov/) probably know, it’s become [a standard line](https://bsky.app/profile/jamestalarico.bsky.social/post/3mny2ddy7g22a) in his stump speech — but it got me wondering: Is that correct?

According to publicly available documents, including financial disclosure filings, the answer is yes. [Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office), either in his personal capacity or through the trust that oversees his assets, owns five homes in [Texas](https://www.texas.gov/), three in [Florida](https://www.myflorida.gov/), two in [Utah](https://www.utah.gov/) and one in [Oklahoma](https://www.oklahoma.gov/). (Those same publicly available materials also point to a lot in [Hawaii](https://www.hawaii.gov/), although there does not appear to be any structure on the property.)

The [Wall Street Journal](https://www.wsj.com/) [similarly reported](https://www.wsj.com/politics/policy/texas-ken-paxton-attorney-general-millionaire-a3be9c94?st=Hr2NTG&) last fall, “The [Paxtons](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office) now own at least 11 residential properties around the U.S. with an assessed value of \$7.5 million.”

To date, [Paxton](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/about-office) has not made any public effort to contest Talarico’s talking point.

[MaddowBlog](https://www.ms.now/maddowblog) reached out to the [Texas](https://www.texas.gov/) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)’s [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) [campaign](https://www.kenpaxton.com/) multiple times to ask about his real estate holdings and how he came to own so many homes, but these requests for comment have so far gone ignored.

Time will tell whether, and to what extent, this information affects the competitive [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) race in the [Lone Star State](https://www.texas.gov/), but the circumstances brought to mind a related story from the recent past.

By mid-August 2008, the Great Recession was starting to take a brutal toll on the economy. In that month alone, the U.S. job market hemorrhaged 286,000 jobs, and with just 10 weeks remaining before Election Day, Americans’ focus turned to the ongoing economic crisis.

It was against this backdrop that a Politico reporter asked then-[Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [John McCain](https://bioguide.congress.gov/search/bio/M000303), the [Republican Party](https://www.gop.com/)’s presidential nominee, how many homes he owned.

“I think — I’ll have my staff get to you,” [McCain said](https://www.politico.com/story/2008/08/mccain-unsure-how-many-houses-he-owns-012685).

Then-[Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Barack Obama](https://bioguide.congress.gov/search/bio/O000167), his [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) rival, wasted little time seizing on the quote. Literally the same day that [McCain](https://bioguide.congress.gov/search/bio/M000303) made the comment, the [Obama](https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/) [campaign](https://bioguide.congress.gov/search/bio/O000167) [launched a campaign ad](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vpmFd25tRqo) hammering the [Republican](https://www.gop.com/)’s uncertainty over this basic fact, driving home the argument that [McCain](https://bioguide.congress.gov/search/bio/M000303) couldn’t relate to the hardships of regular people because he couldn’t even keep track of his vast collection of properties.

The name of the ad was “Seven,” a reference to the seven different homes [McCain](https://bioguide.congress.gov/search/bio/M000303) and his wife owned in locations across the country.

Roughly 18 years later, a similar story is unfolding in one of the nation’s most closely watched [Senate](https://www.senate.gov/) races.
