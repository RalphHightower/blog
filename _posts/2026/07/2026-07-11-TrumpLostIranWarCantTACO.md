## [Trump lost his war with Iran — now he’s trapped in it](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-strikes-mou-strait-hormuz)

_The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) cannot deal with the reality of his humiliating defeat, which perpetuates the conflict and deepens the economic damage it’s causing._

Jul. 10, 2026, 6:00 AM EDT
By [Nicholas Grossman](https://www.ms.now/author/nicholas-grossman)

The [U.S. and Iran are shooting again](https://www.ms.now/news/u-s-launches-new-airstrikes-on-iran-and-tehran-fires-back-at-3-gulf-arab-states), the second (and larger) flare up since they signed a [Memorandum of Understanding](https://www.ms.now/news/news-analysis/memorandum-of-understanding-reveals-some-significant-wins-for-iran) in June.

The main point of contention is the [Strait of Hormuz](https://www.ms.now/news/iran-oil-tankers-strait-hormuz-warning), a vital waterway for international trade that [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) blocked in response to the initial [U.S.](https://www.usa.gov/)-[Israel](https://www.gov.il/) attack in February. The [MOU reflects](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/18/nx-s1-5863027/us-iran-trump-memorandum-of-understanding-full-text) U.S. desperation to get ships flowing again, giving [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) economic benefits in exchange for [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) merely promising to “make arrangements … for the safe passage of commercial vessels,” without specifying what those arrangements are. [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) has [consistently asserted it will control Hormuz](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/15/world/middleeast/shipping-fees-tolls-strait-hormuz.html) and [charge fees for passage](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/iran/iran-oman-propose-fee-plan-strait-hormuz-sources-say-rcna352822), while the U.S., especially [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), has been acting as if the strait is totally open and on a fast track back to prewar normal.

Except it clearly isn’t. More ships went through after the MOU, but even at peak it was [fewer than half](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/09/iran-strait-hormuz-oil-tanker-traffic-gulf-trump.html) the daily prewar average. They avoided the middle of the strait, fearing mines, and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) told them to travel by [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) coastal waters and register with a new “Persian Gulf Strait Authority.” The U.S., for its part, encouraged ships to bypass [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) by [hugging Oman’s coast](https://x.com/AP/status/2070813212648329665?s=20) on the opposite side. [Iran shot at some ships](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/07/iran-resumes-hormuz-attacks-us-officials) near [Oman](https://www.oman.om/) to assert control, the [U.S. shot at Iran](https://www.kshb.com/us-news/iran-war/us-says-170-iran-targets-hit-in-retaliation-for-ship-attacks) to contest it, then [back-and-forth retaliation](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/iran/us-iran-new-attacks-trump-ceasefire-over-gulf-hormuz-rcna353615) followed.

> **Reaching a real peace agreement is even harder when the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) keeps lying about the reality of the situation, and demanding that others do as well.**

The fight could escalate or settle down again, but the underlying problem remains: a large gap between (1) the facts on the ground and the text of the MOU, and (2) the fantasy in [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s imagination. The U.S. government has been warped by an effort to pretend [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s make-believe is real, making peace impossible.

The U.S. [lost the war Trump started](https://www.ms.now/opinion/us-iran-war-strait-hormuz-strikes), failing to achieve the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s declared goals of regime change and unconditional surrender, or his fallback aim of forcing [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) to accept tight nuclear restrictions. The U.S. also failed to militarily prevent [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) from blocking Hormuz. The [MOU amounts to American surrender](https://www.arcdigital.media/p/the-us-iran-deal-is-basically-american), with de facto recognition of the [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) regime, zero nuclear concessions — merely a promise to talk about it more in the future — and front-loaded economic benefits to [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) to open Hormuz, which was open prewar. Nevertheless, the U.S. [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) incessantly claims success.

Lying, delusional, indifferent to truth and just saying anything he thinks sounds good in the moment — whatever the reason [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) pretends an obvious loss is a win, the fact that he keeps doing it perpetuates the conflict and deepens the economic damage it’s causing.

It’s already hard to make a real agreement with the U.S. under the second [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) presidency, since his record of lies, lack of follow through and reneging on deals [undermines America’s credibility](https://www.ms.now/opinion/iran-war-strait-hormuz-diplomacy-trump). That goes double with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/), since [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) reneged on the [Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/cmsdata/122460/full-text-of-the-iran-nuclear-deal.pdf) — the [Obama](https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/)-era [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) nuclear deal — without cause, and launched an aggressive war that killed top [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) officials, including the [Supreme Leader](https://www.leader.ir/), [Ayatollah Ali Khamenei](https://english.khamenei.ir/).

Reaching a real peace agreement is even harder when the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) of the [United States](https://www.usa.gov/) keeps lying about the reality of the situation, and demanding that others do as well.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) repeatedly insists that [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) agreed to nuclear concessions, as he did on July 8 when he [accused Iran of violating the MOU](https://journalrecord.com/2026/07/08/trump-ceasefire-iran-over-renewed-strikes/): “We make a deal, everyone’s agreed, no nuclear weapons.” But the text of the MOU punts the nuclear issue to be dealt with later.

He says the U.S. won’t give [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) any money, like [Obama](https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/) did. On June 18, he [posted to Truth Social](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116772135215188960), “That’s Fake News! All there is for the U.S. is Success, Lower Oil Prices, and Victory” — but the MOU, in fact, [promises Iran a lot more money](https://www.wral.com/news/state/fact-check-us-iran-300-billion-rebuild-june-25/), and not in exchange for verified nuclear restrictions like in the [JCPOA](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/cmsdata/122460/full-text-of-the-iran-nuclear-deal.pdf), just for letting ships go through Hormuz.

He [frequently claims](https://x.com/StateDept/status/2051391360234885166?lang=en), absurdly, [that](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116776790136289354) [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) “doesn’t, any longer, have an Air Force, a [Navy](https://nedaja.aja.ir/), Antiaircraft Equipment, Radar, or practically anything else.” And he berates the media for not going along with that, as if bullying them into generous coverage will make his fantasy real.

It’s an obvious lie, literally unbelievable (If [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) has no military capabilities, who is the U.S. shooting at?), the sort of lie that functions as an authoritarian loyalty test and display of dominance. That might appeal to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s domestic base, maybe jawbone markets, but it doesn’t help the U.S. succeed with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/). If anything, the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s ongoing insistence on a transparent fiction is actively detrimental.

> **If [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) has no military capabilities, who is the U.S. shooting at?**

To some extent, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s public statements are a show for domestic audiences, but the [Iranians](https://irangov.ir/) know the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) issues orders to the [U.S. military](https://www.defense.gov/). And [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) can see how the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), [Republican Party](https://www.gop.com/) and right-wing media rally around whatever [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) says, even when it contradicts something he previously said. It’s also possible U.S. negotiators, including [Vice President JD Vance](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/jd-vance/), are lying to [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), making concessions to [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) and saying the U.S. will honor them, then telling the U.S. [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) they got side deals with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) that adhere closer to his fantasy.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) bragged that oil prices are back down, but that was a market reaction to the MOU, assuming the U.S. would honor it. Except in [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s fantasy, he lowered oil prices by forcing [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) into submission, so he feels less pressure to follow through on concessions since markets acted like things were fine. But with the new fighting, oil is rising again.

It’s not clear how or when this disruption will be resolved — meaning actually resolved, not just [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) saying it is and [Wall Street buying it](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-wall-street-iran-war-stalemate). The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) appears out of ideas, and is rerunning moves that didn’t work before. (At least not in reality. They did great in [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s fantasy.)

The U.S. is bombing [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) again. But the original, larger aerial campaign failed to collapse the [Iranian](https://irangov.ir/) government or stop [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) from blocking Hormuz, so there’s no reason to expect a smaller bombing effort to succeed.

In [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s fantasy narrative, his strength forced [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) to the table — with strength to a significant extent understood as performative bluster on TV or in internet posts — so he’s trying [insults](https://abcnews.com/International/trump-mou-calls-iranian-leaders-scum-latest-strikes/story?id=134576539) and [threats](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TICxOIPDMOM) again.

The situation in the Gulf is bad for the U.S., with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) in a stronger position. The U.S. [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s response is to pretend it’s the opposite. As long as that persists, the conflict will remain unresolved.
