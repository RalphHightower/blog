## [Tickets for the Republicans’ midterm convention as high as $20,000](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/midterm-convention-republicans-ticket-cost-trump-rnc)

_“It’s not a real convention,” a [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) consultant conceded. “It’s just this grifter free-for-all.”_

July 13, 2026, 11:43 AM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

When delegates to the major party nominating conventions gather every four years, they don’t have a whole lot to do. Before the modern [primary system](https://www.ms.now/opinion/ranked-choice-voting-presidential-primary-fix-2028), these conventions were often raucous and contentious gatherings where real work was done choosing candidates for national office. But in recent decades, they have been commonly panned as “infomercials” and devoid of important work.

That said, it’d be an exaggeration to say these quadrennial conventions are completely pointless affairs. If nothing else, nominating conventions feature delegates from across the country who, if nothing else, approve a party platform and formally nominate candidates to lead the national ticket.

[Republicans](https://www.gop.com/)’ plans for a [for a midterm convention](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/republican-midterm-convention-dallas-trump-election), however, are a very different story.

The [Republican National Committee](https://www.gop.com/) recently announced plans [for a national convention](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/republican-midterm-convention-dallas-trump-election), to be held in [Dallas](https://dallascityhall.com/) on Sept. 9-10, reviving a practice [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) [abandoned decades ago](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/republican-midterm-convention-dallas-trump-election). The move comes with significant risks: One of the underlying points of a national convention is nationalizing an election cycle — and given how [very unpopular](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/polls/donald-trump-approval-rating-polls.html) the [incumbent](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is, the party should be going out of its way to localize the midterms.

Despite common sense, the [RNC](https://www.gop.com/) appears to be eager to lean into the risk, with [party chairman](https://gop.com/about-our-party/rnc-leaders/) [Joe Gruters](https://www.linkedin.com/in/joegruters/) telling [Faux News](https://www.foxnews.com/) two weeks ago that the gathering in [Dallas](https://dallascityhall.com/) [will be a “Trumpapalooza,”](https://politicalwire.com/2026/07/01/rnc-chair-previews-midterm-convention/) designed to motivate his followers.

That’s a curious strategy, which [Democratic](https://www.democrats.org/) officials might very well approve of, but there’s another element to the story that’s starting to come into focus. [Politico reported](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/07/09/rnc-midterm-convention-to-be-candidate-fundraiser-with-tickets-reaching-20-000-in-some-states-00992235):

> _An invitation from the [Denton County](https://www.dentoncounty.gov/) [Republican Party](https://www.gop.com/) outside of [Dallas](https://dallascityhall.com/) offers attendees at the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/)’s midterm convention a chance to be “honorary delegates” for a cool \$20,000._

And in [California](https://www.ca.gov/), a \$250,000 “golden VIP member” ticket to the convention buys four floor badges, four hotel rooms, hotel transfers, gifts – and concierge services for the weekend.

A related [HuffPost](https://www.huffpost.com/) report noted that the [Texas GOP](https://texasgop.org/) is charging \$20,000 for an “honorary delegate” credential, which includes a two-night hotel stay and a “Texas Shirt and Cowboy Hat.”

The [Politico](https://www.politico.com/) report, which has not been independently verified by [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), added that the [Republican National Committee](https://www.gop.com/) has encouraged state parties to use the midterm convention as a “fundraiser” for their own candidates.

It’s a plan that opens the door to an obvious line of criticism: This isn’t a major-party convention; it’s a fundraising grift masquerading as a pep rally. No work will be done, and nothing of substance will be accomplished. The party is throwing a two-day soiree for itself and encouraging people to pony up thousands of dollars for the privilege of hanging out with [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) officials and candidates.

[HuffPost](https://www.huffpost.com/) spoke with a [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) consultant who conceded, “It’s not a real convention. … It’s just this grifter free-for-all.”
