title: "Paycheck Diversity Triggers Trumpers Fears of DEI"
---

Paula and I started at [NCR’s E&M](https://www.ncr.com/) division in [West Columbia](https://westcolumbiasc.gov/) in early 1977 — she in January, me in February. I didn’t know it then, but I had just met my future wife.

She was laid off in November 1990 after thirteen years. I followed in November 1991 after fourteen. That double layoff taught us a simple rule for economic survival: paycheck diversity. It sounds like an HR term, but it’s just common sense — the employment version of diversifying a retirement portfolio. Don’t put all your eggs in one basket.

Years later, I was working at a regional financial company. One morning I shared an elevator with Wayne and his wife, Ryan — one of three or four married couples employed there. Wayne asked what Paula did. “Computer support,” I said. He asked why she didn’t apply. I answered, “Paycheck diversity.” Wayne mouthed the words, processed them, and said, “Gotcha.” Ryan didn’t say anything, but she heard the whole exchange.

DEI wasn’t a political flashpoint back then. It became one when Trump entered politics. Today, anything containing the word “diversity” can trigger suspicion, even when it has nothing to do with demographic policy.

[South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) [Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/) [Alan Wilson](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/meet-the-attorney-general/) illustrates the point. He wants to enforce [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s anti‑DEI stance — selectively. [Starbucks](https://www.starbucks.com/) and [Costco](https://www.costco.com/) get letters. [BMW](https://www.bmwgroup.com/) and [Boeing](https://www.boeing.com/) do not. The state’s economy depends on them, and both operate under DEI requirements imposed by the EU and global aviation markets.

It’s an odd moment: politicians railing against DEI as ideology, while the companies that keep the state employed treat it as a compliance reality. Meanwhile, Paula and I learned our own version decades ago — not ideological, not political, just practical.

**Paycheck diversity wasn’t DEI. It was survival.**
