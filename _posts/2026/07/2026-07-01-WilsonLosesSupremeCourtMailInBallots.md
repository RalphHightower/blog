## [Attorney General Alan Wilson responds to U.S. Supreme Court's ruling on mail-in ballots - South Carolina Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/news/attorney-general-alan-wilson-responds-to-u-s-supreme-court-s-ruling-on-mail-in-ballots/)

JUN 29, 2026

([COLUMBIA](https://www.columbiasc.gov/), [S.C.](https://www.sc.gov/)) – [Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/) [Alan Wilson](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/meet-the-attorney-general/) released the following statement regarding the [U.S. Supreme Court](https://www.supremecourt.gov/)'s decision on mail-in ballots:

"Today’s decision in [Watson v. RNC](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.mssd.123732/gov.uscourts.mssd.123732.1.0_1.pdf) is deeply disappointing. By allowing states to count mail-in ballots received after Election Day, the [Court](https://www.supremecourt.gov/) has undermined confidence in our elections and validated the kind of election practices that have eroded public trust in states like [California](https://www.ca.gov/) and [New York](https://www.ny.gov/).

Election Day should mean Election Day. The American people deserve timely, secure, and transparent elections where every lawful vote is counted and the results are final.

As [Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/), I have fought for election integrity by defending [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/)’s [Voter ID law](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/code/t07c001.php), supporting the [SAVE Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296), and standing up for commonsense election safeguards. I will never stop fighting to protect free, fair, and secure elections for the people of [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/)."
