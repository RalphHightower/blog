## [Trump, despite his record, eyes prime-time address on foreign election interference](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trump-foreign-election-interference-2020)

_The [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) may want to renew the public conversation about foreign election interference in 2020, but he might not like where that conversation ends up._

July 14, 2026, 9:02 AM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

As this week got underway, [Donald Trump announced](https://trumpstruth.org/statuses/40009) by way of his social media platform that he would deliver “a Speech to the Nation” on Thursday night, though the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) initially didn’t say a word about why or what he intended to say. Hours later, however, he spoke via phone to [Newsmin](https://www.newsnax.com/) and shed some light on what the public can expect to hear.

“Our elections are crooked and we’ve got to straighten them out,” [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) said, peddling familiar, tiresome nonsense. “We’ve got to have voter ID; we’ve got to have proof of citizenship; and we have got to do something about the mail-in ballots, which are just corrupt, crooked and should not be allowed.”

In other words, the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) remains focused on his voter suppression proposal, which the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) has labeled the [Save America Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7296) and which [congressional](https://www.congress.gov/) [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/) have [already conceded](https://www.huffpost.com/entry/gop-senator-says-theres-zero-chance-of-passing-key-piece-of-trumps-agenda_n_6a504d8ee4b0aaa37424e8bf?origin=home-latest-news-unit) they can’t pass.

We’ll learn soon enough what makes its way onto the [Republicans](https://www.gop.com/)’s teleprompter, but there’s one thing of particular interest that’s likely to make the cut.

[MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/), citing comments from two [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) officials granted anonymity to discuss internal plans, reported on Monday afternoon that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) plans to address newly declassified intelligence reports that the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) asserts reveal plans by foreign nations to interfere in the [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf).

One obviously can’t fact-check claims that have yet to be made, but if the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and his team follow through on these plans, there are a few things the public should keep in mind.

First, there is some irony to the idea that [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), of all people, would voice concerns along these lines: In 2016, [Russia](http://kremlin.ru/) launched a military intelligence operation that targeted the American [presidential](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) election for the express purpose of putting [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) in power. Confronted with overwhelming evidence of that effort, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) falsely dismissed the evidence [as a “hoax”](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/report-details-team-trump-s-connections-russia-s-2016-scheme-n1237264) and ultimately said [he trusted Russian President Vladimir Putin more](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trump-isnt-quite-done-siding-putin-us-officials-rcna68330) than his own country’s intelligence agencies.

Second, if the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) really wants to talk about foreign interference in the [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf), the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) should probably expect a few questions about that time [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) explicitly said [he would welcome foreign election interference](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/how-will-the-gop-defend-trumps-line-foreign-election-interference-msna1241956) in the [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf).

Third, ahead of Election Day 2020, [congressional](https://www.congress.gov/) [Democrats](https://www.democrats.org/) pushed a variety of measures intended to discourage foreign election interference. [Republicans rejected all of those proposals](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/gop-quietly-kills-measure-discourage-foreign-election-interference-n1232664).

And finally, let’s not forget that when U.S. intelligence again showed that [Russia](http://kremlin.ru/) was targeting the [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf) to help [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) stay in power, the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) responded by announcing that [it would no longer provide briefings to Congress](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trump-admin-curtails-briefings-foreign-election-interference-n1238839) about foreign interference in the [2020 election](https://www.fec.gov/resources/cms-content/documents/federalelections2020.pdf).

In other words, if the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) wants to renew a public conversation about foreign election interference six years ago, he might not like where the conversation ends up.
