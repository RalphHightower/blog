## [Trump’s disastrous cycle of bad decisions continues as Iran ceasefire collapses](https://www.ms.now/all-in/trump-national-security-iran-war-ceasefire-qatari-jet-air-force-one)

_Once again, the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) has proved it is incapable of actually solving any of the monstrous, idiotic problems it has created._

Jul. 10, 2026, 3:08 PM EDT
By [Chris Hayes](https://www.ms.now/author/chris-hayes)

This is an adapted excerpt from the [July 9](https://www.ms.now/all) episode of [“All In with Chris Hayes.”](https://www.ms.now/all)

On Wednesday at the [NATO summit](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-nato-summit-allies-ukraine-defense) in [Turkey](https://global.tbmm.gov.tr/), [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.ms.now/white-house) said he thought his ceasefire with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) was dead. Just hours later, he made sure it was.

Sources told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/liveblog/iran-war-news-today-july-9-2026) that the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) was in midair leaving [Turkey](https://global.tbmm.gov.tr/) on [Air Force One](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104588/vc-25-air-force-one/) when he basically redeclared war. But the plane he was aboard wasn’t the same [Air Force One](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104588/vc-25-air-force-one/) on which he left the United States.

He had arrived in [Turkey](https://global.tbmm.gov.tr/) on his flying bribery palace, the [luxury 747,](https://www.ms.now/news/trump-new-air-force-one-qatar-jet) “gifted” to him by the emir of [Qatar](https://hukoomi.gov.qa/). [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) showed it off to foreign leaders at the summit and beamed about it to the press. But when it came time to leave — and to bomb [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) again — that plane departed without the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) aboard.

> **Soliciting and then using that plane may have been one of the most reckless national security decisions ever.**

Instead, the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) flew out on an older [Air Force One](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104588/vc-25-air-force-one/) from the existing U.S. fleet. On that flight, passengers were told to keep their window blinds closed.

[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) tried to explain the change by telling reporters it was so his gifted jet, which has been repainted in the same color scheme as his private planes, could be shown off to service members on its way home. But when a reporter asked why passengers were told to keep the blinds down, [Trump replied](https://x.com/Acyn/status/2075007925496803741), “Well yeah, because you’re, you know, probably on a dangerous flight.”

While [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) tried to brush away such questions, two former national security officials familiar with the matter confirmed to [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/news/officials-say-trump-switched-out-new-air-force-one-over-security-concerns) that the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) switched planes over concerns his new [Qatari](https://hukoomi.gov.qa/) air palace lacked the necessary security systems “needed to safely manage a rapidly escalating conflict with [Iran](https://irangov.ir/).” Specifically, according to a law enforcement official briefed on its capabilities, “the gifted plane lacks key military defenses of the original [Air Force One](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104588/vc-25-air-force-one/), including the ability to refuel midair and missile-defense systems.”

Two other former security officials told [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/) the new plane also lacks the command-and-control functions necessary, like secure communications, to serve as an airborne “situation room.”

It’s worth noting, as an aside, that [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/08/us/politics/trump-air-force-one-security.html) reported that as much as a billion dollars may have been taken from the defense budget to retrofit [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s new toy for service.

So the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) of the [United States](https://www.usa.gov/) was flying around on a plane he got from [Qatar](https://hukoomi.gov.qa/) — a plane he has said will go with him when he leaves office, a plane that doesn’t have adequate missile defenses or secure communications and a plane that is not capable of refueling in midair — that he couldn’t use anyway.

Soliciting and then using that plane may have been one of the most reckless national security decisions ever, rivaled only by every other decision [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) has made in the past four months to bring us to this point. Because, of course, we’re back at war, and it’s clear the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) can’t figure out how to end it.

Remember last year, around this time? [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) did the thing he said no other [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) would do and [bombed [Iran](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/middle-east/strikes-iran-set-back-nuclear-program-several-months-sources-say-rcna214853). He said its nuclear sites were “completely and fully obliterated”: Problem solved.

At the time, many of us knew that would not be the end.

And of course, it wasn’t. Despite having said we destroyed its nuclear systems the last time, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) launched [a new war](https://www.ms.now/opinion/trump-iran-strikes-mou-strait-hormuz) against [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) in February.

And then in April, [he said](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116386735130758581) the U.S. had “completely destroyed” [Iran](https://irangov.ir/)’s military. However, the fighting continued. Problem not solved.

Then [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) announced the U.S. and [Iran](https://irangov.ir/) had finally [made peace](https://www.ms.now/opinion/us-iran-deal-trump-vance-spin) and a new era would begin: Once again, problem solved!

That was, until he [threw out that ceasefire deal](https://www.ms.now/liveblog/iran-war-news-today-july-8-2026) and restarted the war a few days ago.

At every turn, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) and his entourage have beaten their chests about how only they can solve a problem no one else before them could solve, and at every turn, that problem is revealed to be not solved.

The [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/) is, unsurprisingly, incapable of actually solving the monstrous, idiotic problems it has created.

So here we are again. The ceasefire is over. How many more times will we go through with this? How many more people will die? How many more will suffer economic devastation?

Allison Detzel contributed.
