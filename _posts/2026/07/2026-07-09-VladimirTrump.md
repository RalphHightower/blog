## Trying to Save Democracy — Country Over Party
### Just the facts, ma’am.

Reporting from [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/)’s [MaddowBlog]() documents a pattern. During a 37‑minute press gaggle aboard [Air Force One](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104588/vc-25-air-force-one/), [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) named or threatened multiple foreign countries as potential targets for U.S. military action. The statements are recorded in the transcript and summarized by [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen).
*Source: [MS NOW MaddowBlog](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/its-not-just-venezuela-trump-already-eyeing-a-series-of-potential-foreign-targets)*

Below is a factual summary.

## Venezuela
[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) authorized a U.S. operation to capture [Nicolás Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classPresidente.php). He told reporters [Venezuelan](https://gob.ve/) officials should expect a “second strike” if they “don’t behave.” He said he had not ruled out putting U.S. troops on the ground.
Earlier, he told *[The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/)* that [Delcy Rodríguez](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classIndex.php) “is going to pay a very big price, probably bigger than [Maduro](https://presidencia.gob.ve/Site/Web/Principal/paginas/classPresidente.php)” if she “doesn’t do what’s right.”
[Secretary of](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) [State](https://www.state.gov/) [Marco Rubio](https://www.state.gov/biographies/marco-rubio/) described the operation as “a law enforcement action.” [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) contradicted him.

## [Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/)
[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) said: “You have to do something with [Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/).”
No clarification. No elaboration.
But the implications are concrete. [Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/) is structurally fused to the United States in two critical ways: **autos** and **food**.

### Auto Supply Chain
[Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/) is a core pillar of North American auto manufacturing.
Key components — wiring harnesses, seat assemblies, engine parts, transmission housings, electronics, stamped metal — cross the border **daily**.
A disruption does not slow production. It **halts** it.
U.S. plants in [Michigan](https://www.michigan.gov/), [Ohio](https://www.ohio.gov/), [Kentucky](https://www.kentucky.gov/), [Tennessee](https://www.tn.gov/), [Alabama](https://www.alabama.gov/), and [South Carolina](https://www.sc.gov/) depend on [Mexican](https://www.gob.mx/) parts arriving on schedule.
[Canada](https://www.canada.ca/)’s auto sector relies on the same pipeline.
Any instability involving [Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/) would trigger immediate line stoppages across the continent.

### Food Supply Chain
[Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/) is also a primary source of U.S. produce.
Tomatoes, avocados, berries, peppers, cucumbers, citrus, squash, onions — staples, not luxuries — arrive in refrigerated trucks every day.
Restaurants, grocery chains, and fast‑food suppliers rely on this flow.
A disruption would create **shortages**, not “tightness.”
Prices would rise quickly.
Shelf availability would fall just as fast.

### Trump’s Big Mac Economy
[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s foreign‑policy posture isn’t the only pattern the reporting makes clear. His economic posture follows the same logic. He talks about the price of a [Big Mac](https://www.mcdonalds.com/us/en-us/product/big-mac.html) as if it’s a personal grievance, but the reality is simple: the price will rise. Higher fuel costs, higher food‑import costs, and instability with [Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/) — a primary source of U.S. produce — all push fast‑food prices upward. At the same time, [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s travel habits mean taxpayers cover the cost of his own meals. When he orders [McDonald’s](https://www.mcdonalds.com/us/en-us/) on [Air Force One](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104588/vc-25-air-force-one/) or at his golf properties, the public pays the bill. It’s the split economy of [Trumpism](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/): rising costs for everyone else, subsidized [Happy Meals](https://www.mcdonalds.com/us/en-us/meal/hamburger-happy-meal.html) for him.

### Why the comment matters
When a [U.S. president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) says, “You have to do something with [Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/),” the consequences are not theoretical.
They are economic, immediate, and continental.
The auto sector and the food sector are tightly linked to [Mexico](https://www.gob.mx/).
Any instability would hit U.S. consumers, U.S. workers, and U.S. prices within days.

## Cuba
[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) said [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/)’s government is “ready to fall.”
He added that his administration would be “talking about” [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) because it is “very similar” to [Venezuela](https://gob.ve/).
Rubio echoed that [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/)n officials are “in a lot of trouble.”
*Source: [MS NOW MaddowBlog](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/its-not-just-venezuela-trump-already-eyeing-a-series-of-potential-foreign-targets)*

### Cuba as a Demolition Buy
[Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) is not a distressed opportunity. It is a **demolition buy** — the real‑estate term you’ve used before.
The land has strategic value.
The structures — political, economic, infrastructural — do not.
Everything above ground is a teardown.

The electrical grid is unstable.
The water system is unreliable.
Housing stock is deteriorated.
Transportation is fragmented.
The economy is non‑functional.
The institutional framework is brittle.

Any external actor stepping in would inherit **negative‑value assets**:
- a collapsed economy,
- a failed infrastructure base,
- a population under strain,
- and a government in freefall.

It is not a “fixer‑upper.”
It is a full rebuild — a ground‑up reconstruction of a state.

### Why the comment matters
When [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) says [Cuba](http://cubaminrex.cu/) is “ready to fall,” the implication is not just political.
It is structural.
A collapse would leave behind a territory requiring massive stabilization, reconstruction, and long‑term investment.
That is the definition of a demolition buy.

## My Take: “Vladimir Trump”
When a [U.S. president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) openly names neighboring countries as potential targets, threatens “second strikes,” warns foreign leaders to “watch [their] ass,” and talks casually about acquiring territory, the behavior resembles the geopolitical style of a strongman rather than a traditional [American president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/).

That is why, in my shorthand, I refer to this posture as **“[Vladimir Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/).”**
Not as an insult.
Not as hyperbole.
As a structural description of a leader who treats borders as negotiable and military force as a first‑line instrument of policy.

Just the facts.
