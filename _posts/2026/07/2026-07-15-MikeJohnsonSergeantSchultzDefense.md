## [Stumped on ICE killing, Mike Johnson keeps struggling with the basics of current events](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/mike-johnson-maine-ice-shooting-current-events)

_Confronted with questions he should know how to answer, the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) leader has proven to be surprisingly ignorant about day-to-day news._

July 14, 2026, 1:00 PM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.ms.now/author/steve-benen)

By any fair measure, one of the biggest stories in the United States yesterday unfolded in [Maine](https://www.maine.gov/), where an [Immigration and Customs Enforcement](https://www.ice.gov/) [fatally shot Johan Sebastián Guerrero](https://www.ms.now/news/colombian-man-killed-in-maine-was-not-the-target-of-ices-operation-sen-king-says), a [Colombian](https://www.gov.co/) man [authorized to work in the U.S.](https://maineimmigrantrights.org/maines-immigrant-community-responds-to-ice-shooting-in-biddeford/) who was also father of a 3-year-old girl.

A day later, a reporter asked [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/) [Mike Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) for his reaction to the deadly incident and whether he believed some accountability was in order. The [Louisiana](https://www.louisiana.gov/) [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) was stumped — again.

“I don’t know anything about this event,” the [GOP](https://www.gop.com/)’s top congressional leader told reporters. “I was a little busy yesterday.” He acknowledged there “was a tragic shooting” but said he wouldn’t comment “because I don’t know.”

Mindful of how that might sound, [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) added, “You guys can mock me for not knowing that. I worked about 22 hours in the last few days.”

That work schedule didn’t sound too grueling: 22 hours of work over the last few days suggests the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/) worked roughly seven hours a day, which presumably left him some time to maybe read a newspaper or watch some news coverage on television about current events.

But putting that aside, that the [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) [congressman](https://www.house.gov/) anticipated the mockery was notable because [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) apparently realizes this has been an ongoing problem.

What did the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/) say when asked in December about [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s scandalous pardon [for Changpeng Zhao](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/white-house-struggles-defend-trumps-pardon-founder-binance-crypto-exch-rcna239435)? “I don’t know anything about it,” [he said](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/dont-know-anything-mike-johnson-becomes-low-information-house-speaker-rcna241817). What was his reaction to allegations that the U.S. military killed defenseless survivors following a strike on an alleged drug boat in international waters? “I was pretty busy yesterday,” [Johnson replied](https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3m6zctxlmug2j). “I didn’t follow a lot of the news.”

Last fall, a reporter asked [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) about the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) [administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)’s personnel purge at [ICE](https://www.ice.gov/) and whether congressional oversight might be in order. The [House](https://www.house.gov/) [speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/) said [he didn’t know](https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3m4b632yykk2i) about that, either.

Five days before that, amid widespread reports that the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) was seeking [a $230 million payment](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trump-eyes-230-million-payout-justice-dept-repayment-earlier-investiga-rcna238987) from the [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/), [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) was asked for comment. [He was again stumped](https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3m3ullw6kum2f). A day earlier, he was asked about the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) importing [Argentinian](https://www.casarosada.gob.ar/) beef, and the [Louisiana](https://www.louisiana.gov/) [congressman](https://www.congress.gov/) said [he wasn’t up to speed](https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3m3s3ojohfu2i) on that, either.

As [MS NOW](https://www.ms.now/)’s [Jen Psaki noted in a segment](https://www.ms.now/top-stories/latest/mike-johnson-excuses-trump-investigations-congress-rcna239144) in October, the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [GOP](https://www.gop.com/) leader has claimed ignorance on everything from [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s crypto dealings to the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s reported relationship with Jeffrey Epstein, from U.S. citizens being detained by federal immigration agents to a would-be assassin plotting to kill [House](https://www.house.gov/) [Minority Leader](https://democraticleader.house.gov/) [Hakeem Jeffries](https://jeffries.house.gov/).

In politics, there’s a phrase for Americans who struggle to keep up with current events and who tend to be ill informed about key issues: They’re known as low-information voters.

In 2026, the nation appears to have a low-information [House](https://www.house.gov/) [speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/).

It’s entirely possible there’s an alternate explanation for how often [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) has answered “I don’t know”: Maybe the [House](https://www.house.gov/) [speaker](https://speaker.house.gov/) is well informed, but he’s simply pretending to be clueless to avoid answering politically difficult questions.

That is, perhaps [Johnson](https://mikejohnson.house.gov/) is simply dishonest, not oblivious, though this is a defense he has not yet presented to the public.

## Hogan's Heroes and Sergeant Schultz

Sergeant Hans Schultz was the bumbling, soft‑hearted guard of Stalag 13 on Hogan’s Heroes, played by Austrian‑born actor John Banner. His defining catchphrase — “I see nothing! I know nothing!” — became one of television’s most enduring comedic lines.

### 🪖 Who Schultz Was

Schultz is portrayed as a German sergeant whose job is to guard Allied POWs — a job he performs with a mix of fear, kindness, and strategic obliviousness. Rather than a villain, he’s a comic foil: a man who wants to avoid trouble more than enforce discipline. His constant denial of anything suspicious happening in the camp is both his survival strategy and the show’s running gag.

### 🎙️ His Catchphrase

“I see nothing! I know nothing!” — Schultz’s most famous line, used whenever he stumbles upon Hogan’s sabotage or espionage. It’s a comedic shield: if he “knows nothing,” he can’t be blamed or transferred to the Eastern Front. Variants like “I know nothing! I do nothing!” emphasize his willful ignorance and became part of the show’s rhythm. The humor comes from the contrast between his claim and the blatantly obvious chaos around him.

### 📺 About Hogan’s Heroes

The series ran from 1965 to 1971, a sitcom set in a German POW camp where Colonel Hogan and his men run an underground resistance operation right under the noses of their captors. To make the premise palatable, the show leaned heavily on absurdity: Stalag 13 is “escape‑proof,” yet the prisoners come and go freely; the German officers are bureaucratic, gullible, or incompetent. Schultz’s blend of fear and friendliness made him the perfect gatekeeper for this tone.

### 📌 Why Schultz Endures

Schultz became iconic because he embodied harmless denial — a comedic take on a man trapped in a system he doesn’t truly support. His catchphrase evolved into a cultural shorthand for plausible deniability, used far beyond the show’s context.
