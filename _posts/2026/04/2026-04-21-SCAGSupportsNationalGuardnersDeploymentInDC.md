## [Attorney General Alan Wilson leads support of President Trump's deployment of National Guard to D.C. - South Carolina Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/news/attorney-general-alan-wilson-leads-support-of-president-trump-s-deployment-of-national-guard-to-d-c/)

APR 10, 2026

([COLUMBIA](https://www.columbiasc.gov/), [S.C.](https://www.sc.gov/)) – [Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/) [Alan Wilson](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/meet-the-attorney-general/) co-led a [coalition of 23 states](https://republicanags.com/) in a friend-of-the-court brief supporting [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s constitutional deployment of the [National Guard](https://www.nationalguard.mil/) to [Washington, D.C.](https://dc.gov/)

In the years preceding [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s current term, crime surged in our nation’s capital. Rampant carjackings, robberies, murders, and violence endangered the lives of civilians and government employees.

“[President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)s have defended our capital in times of crisis across generations,” [Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/) [Wilson](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/meet-the-attorney-general/) stated. “The federal government has the right and responsibility to keep our nation’s capital safe for the American people. [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) is doing exactly what our Founders intended; and that is to protect the seat of government when it’s under threat.”

The brief argues that each state in our Union has a vested interest in the safety of [Washington, D.C.](https://dc.gov/) Each state sends delegates to [Congress](https://www.congress.gov/) and employees to work in different areas of the federal government. Citizens frequently travel to the nation’s capital to petition the government and visit various sights.

Acting under the authority granted to the Chief Executive under [Article II](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/article-2/), [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) deployed the [National Guard](https://www.nationalguard.mil/) to safeguard [Washington, D.C.](https://dc.gov/) The [Pentagon](https://www.jcs.mil/) has recently announced these troops will remain deployed to the nation’s capital for the remainder of the [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s second term.

“As a 30-year veteran of the [National Guard](https://www.nationalguard.mil/), I am proud to stand with [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) to ensure the streets of our nation’s capital are safe,” [Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/) [Wilson](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/meet-the-attorney-general/) said. “The [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) is operating well within his constitutional authority to end lawlessness in our capital.”

[Attorney General](https://www.scag.gov/) [Wilson](https://www.scag.gov/about-the-office/meet-the-attorney-general/) is co-leading this brief with [West Virginia](https://ago.wv.gov/). Joining them are the [attorneys general](https://republicanags.com/) of [Alabama](https://www.alabamaag.gov/), [Alaska](https://law.alaska.gov/), [Arkansas](https://arkansasag.gov/), [Florida](https://www.myfloridalegal.com/), [Georgia](https://law.georgia.gov/), [Idaho](https://www.ag.idaho.gov/), [Indiana](https://www.in.gov/attorneygeneral/), [Iowa](https://www.iowaattorneygeneral.gov/), [Kansas](https://www.ag.ks.gov/home), [Kentucky](https://ag.ky.gov/Pages/default.aspx), [Louisiana](https://ag.louisiana.gov/), [Mississippi](https://attorneygenerallynnfitch.com/), [Missouri](https://ago.mo.gov/), [Montana](https://dojmt.gov/), [Nebraska](https://ago.nebraska.gov/), [North Dakota](https://attorneygeneral.nd.gov/), [Ohio](https://www.ohioattorneygeneral.gov/), [Oklahoma](https://oklahoma.gov/oag.html), [South Dakota](https://atg.sd.gov/#gsc.tab=0), [Pennsylvania](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/ogc), and [Texas](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/).
