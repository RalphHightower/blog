## [Jack Smith’s lawyers push back against ethics complaint and its ‘imaginary’ merits](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/jack-smiths-lawyers-push-back-ethics-complaint-imaginary-merits-rcna227457)

*The former special counsel is facing a preposterous and overtly partisan ethics investigation. His lawyers are not amused.*

Aug. 27, 2025, 9:00 AM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.msnbc.com/author/steve-benen-ncpn433601)

[Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) welcomed [South Korean](https://plus.gov.kr/) [President Lee Jae Myung](https://www.president.go.kr/) to the [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) this week, and the [American president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s counterpart briefly commented on the criminal investigation into his predecessor in [Seoul](https://seoul.go.kr/). After making a passing reference to a special prosecutor,[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) interrupted the South Korean translator.

“Is his name deranged [Jack Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith), by any chance?” [the Republican said](https://rollcall.com/factbase/trump/transcript/donald-trump-remarks-bilat-lee-jae-myung-south-korea-august-25-2025/#149), as part of an apparent attempt at humor. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) added, “Deranged Jack. He’s a deranged, sick individual.”

It was a timely reminder that the former special counsel, who indicted [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) on a variety of alleged federal crimes, remains very much on the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s mind. The mere mention of a special prosecutor in a foreign country generated a practically Pavlovian response in the [Oval Office](https://www.whitehouse.gov/), despite the fact that [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) has largely faded from public view.

That said, [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) hasn’t completely exited the stage. [The New York Times reported](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/08/26/us/jack-smith-ethics-complaint-trump.html):

> *Lawyers for [Jack Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith), the former special counsel who investigated [Donald J. Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), have struck back for the first time against some of the accusations conservatives have leveled against him, denouncing an ethics complaint as ‘imaginary and unfounded.’ For months, [Mr. Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) has remained silent as the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) and some of his senior advisers, including top [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) officials, publicly attacked him, accusing [Mr. Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) of engaging in wrongdoing for overseeing two criminal inquiries and indictments of [Mr. Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/).*

For those who might benefit from a refresher, [NBC News reported](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/trump-[administration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/)/office-special-counsel-launches-investigation-ex-trump-prosecutor-jack-rcna222625) earlier this month that federal officials had launched an investigation into [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) in response to [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) allegations that the former special counsel’s [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) investigations amounted to illegal political activity in violation of the [Hatch Act](https://osc.gov/Services/Pages/HatchAct.aspx), which prohibits certain political activities by government officials.

The claims were, by any fair measure, ridiculous. In fact, there was literally no evidence whatsoever of [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) engaging in any kind of wrongdoing. Then-[Attorney General](https://www.justice.gov/) [Merrick Garland](https://www.justice.gov/ag/bio/attorney-general-merrick-b-garland) tapped [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) to serve as a special counsel in November 2022 — two years before the 2024 presidential election — at which point he oversaw the federal investigations into [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/).

The prosecutor proceeded to collect voluminous evidence, to secure indictments and to charge [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) with a great many felonies, but at no point did [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) engage in any partisan political activities, making the basis for such an investigation from the [U.S. Office of Special Counsel](https://osc.gov/) absurd.

Just as notably, it seems rather obvious that this move against [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) is part of a larger partisan vendetta from a party that’s eager to retaliate against those who dared to try to hold [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) accountable for his alleged crimes. Indeed, it was [Republican](https://www.gop.com/) [Sen.](https://www.senate.gov/) [Tom Cotton (T-AR)](https://www.cotton.senate.gov/), a close [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) ally, who [requested](https://x.com/SenTomCotton/status/1950616831397642291) that the OSC investigate [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) for “unprecedented interference in the 2024 election,” despite the complete lack of evidence pointing to any interference.

Making matters just a bit worse, it was especially jarring to see [Team Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) pretend to take the [Hatch Act](https://osc.gov/Services/Pages/HatchAct.aspx) seriously after [treating the same ethics law as a joke](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/3-biggest-problems-new-unwarranted-investigation-jack-smith-rcna222856) for years.

It’s against this backdrop that [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s lawyer sent a three-page letter to the acting special counsel, Jamieson Greer.

“The predicate for this investigation is imaginary and unfounded,” the lawyers wrote, according to a copy of the letter obtained by the New York Times. “Mr. [Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) followed well-established legal principles in conducting the investigations into [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), and the courts presiding over the resulting prosecutions have already rejected the spurious allegations that the manner in which [Mr. Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith) prosecuted these cases was somehow improper.”

[Smith](https://www.justice.gov/archives/sco-smith)’s lawyers wrote that they “welcome the opportunity to engage with your office and are confident that as you become familiar with the facts and the record, you will conclude that there is no basis to find a violation of the [Hatch Act](https://osc.gov/Services/Pages/HatchAct.aspx) and that these allegations are wholly without merit.”
