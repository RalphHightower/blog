## [As more federal prosecutors are ousted, Team Trump’s campaign reaches an unsettling level](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/federal-prosecutors-are-ousted-team-trumps-campaign-reaches-unsettling-rcna238620)

*The corrupt motivations behind this political campaign against prosecutors is many things, but subtle is not one of them.*

Oct. 20, 2025, 9:19 AM EDT
By [Steve Benen](https://www.msnbc.com/author/steve-benen-ncpn433601)

While every federal prosecutor’s office is important, the U.S. attorney for the [Eastern District of Virginia](https://www.justice.gov/) stands out for a reason: This is the office that handles many of the country’s most [sensitive national security cases](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2025/09/23/trump-halligan-siebert-us-attorney-virginia-blue-slip-senate/).

It’s also an office that [Donald Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s operation continues to destabilize. [The New York Times reported](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/17/us/politics/trump-prosecutor-fired-letitia-james.html):

> *A federal prosecutor who resisted [President](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s demands to bring charges against [Letitia James](https://ag.ny.gov/about/meet-letitia-james), the [New York](https://www.ny.gov/) [state attorney general](https://ag.ny.gov/), was fired along with her deputy on Friday evening, according to three people familiar with the matter. The dismissal of the prosecutor, [Elizabeth Yusi](https://www.linkedin.com/in/elizabeth-yusi-198025a3/), was the latest fallout from attempts by career [Justice Department](https://www.justice.gov/) officials to pump the brakes on [Mr. Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s wide-ranging efforts to seek retribution against his perceived political opponents.*

The [Times](https://www.nytimes.com/)’ report noted that [Yusi](https://www.linkedin.com/in/elizabeth-yusi-198025a3/), who oversaw major criminal cases in the Norfolk office, told colleagues that there was insufficient evidence to bring a case against the [New York](https://www.ny.gov/) attorney general. [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/), however, [wanted his perceived political enemy to be indicted](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/one-thing-letitia-james-james-comey-indictments-common-rcna236837), and so his team made it happen, ignoring Yusi and her concerns about propriety and merit.

What’s more, [MSNBC reported](https://x.com/DilanianMSNBC/status/1979323126946369964) that Yusi was one of two criminal prosecutors in the Norfolk office who were ousted, not because of wrongdoing but because they expressed skepticism about filing a weak case against a [presidential](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) opponent.

If this sounds at all familiar, it’s not your imagination. Erik Siebert was [also ousted](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/forcing-disobedient-prosecutor-trumps-new-nominee-makes-matters-worse-rcna232922) as the U.S. attorney in [Virginia’s eastern district](https://www.justice.gov/usao-edva/) because he [wouldn’t bring baseless criminal charges](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/forcing-disobedient-prosecutor-trumps-new-nominee-makes-matters-worse-rcna232922) against [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s political enemies, and soon after [Michael Ben’Ary](https://www.linkedin.com/in/michael-ben-ary-55095a6/), the top national security prosecutor in [Virginia](https://www.justice.gov/usao-edva/), [was fired](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/trumps-justice-department-fires-wrong-prosecutor-wrong-reason-rcna235424) after a pro-[Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) activist peddled a baseless accusation against him related to another case against one of [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)’s political enemies.

In other words, in this one office — the one that handles many of the country’s most [sensitive national security cases](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2025/09/23/trump-halligan-siebert-us-attorney-virginia-blue-slip-senate/) — the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/)’s team has ousted at least four prosecutors, including a [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)-nominated U.S. attorney.

This is not to be confused with the ouster of the [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/)-nominated U.S. attorney for the [Western District of Virginia](https://www.justice.gov/usao-wdva), [also for political reasons](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/team-trump-forced-another-us-attorney-failed-toe-white-house-line-rcna237766), which roughly coincided with [the firing](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/federal-prosecutors-are-ousted-purge-intensifies-trumps-justice-depart-rcna234453) of a federal prosecutor in Miami because far-right activists discovered that he criticized [Trump](https://www.donaldjtrump.com/) eight years ago while in private practice, or with [the dismissal](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/federal-prosecutors-are-ousted-purge-intensifies-trumps-justice-depart-rcna234453) of a federal prosecutor in [California](https://www.ca.gov/) because she urged immigration officials to comply with a court order.

For that matter, the full list of [prosecutors](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/01/31/us/politics/prosecutors-us-attorneys-office-trump-firings.html) caught up in the [purge of federal law enforcement](https://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/doj-fires-career-prosecutors-team-trumps-revenge-tour-continues-rcna189586) because they worked on cases the [president](https://www.whitehouse.gov/) didn’t like has been [difficult to keep up with](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2025/07/19/justice-career-prosecutors-staff-firings-trump/).

The corrupt motivations behind this political campaign against prosecutors is many things, but subtle is not one of them.
